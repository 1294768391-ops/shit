# 1. Bibliographic Information
## 1.1. Title
The full title of the paper is *Research on Domestic Animation Films with Myth Theme since the New Century* (Chinese: 新世纪以来神话题材的国产动画电影研究). Its central topic is the innovative transformation and development of Chinese domestic animated films adapted from traditional mythological themes in the 21st century, covering story innovation, visual narrative innovation, existing industry challenges, and future development directions.
## 1.2. Authors
The author is Cao Yuan, a master's graduate from the School of Journalism and Communication, Nanchang University, supervised by Professor Xu Aizhu. Her research focus is radio and television art, with a specialization in domestic animation creation and traditional cultural communication. This work is her 2021 master's degree thesis.

Master's theses in this field are important references for research on the development of China's domestic animation and cultural creative industries, reflecting cutting-edge exploratory work on policy-aligned cultural industry development.

The thesis was successfully defended on May 29, 2021, and officially published in 2021.
## 1.5. Abstract
This paper starts from the creation practice of myth-themed domestic animated films, sorts out the phased influence of Chinese mythological themes on domestic animated films since the 20th century, and takes two core dimensions: story innovation and image narrative innovation as entry points, combined with relevant literary and art theories to analyze myth-themed domestic animated films produced in the new century. Taking representative classic works as examples, it conducts specific research on the reshaping of classic characters, modernization of myth values, cross-media image breakthrough, and other aspects, summarizes the motif arrangement rules of domestic myth-themed animated films in creation, explores the advantages of rational use and innovative adaptation of myth themes in the production process, identifies the difficulties and problems faced in the innovative inheritance of myth themes, and provides reference for the future creation of Chinese myth-themed animated films.

# 2. Executive Summary
## 2.1. Background & Motivation
### Core Problem to Be Solved
The paper aims to address how to realize the creative transformation and innovative development of traditional Chinese mythological themes in domestic animated films: how to avoid low-level repetitive adaptation, integrate modern values and contemporary aesthetic preferences while inheriting traditional cultural connotations, and create high-quality domestic animated works that meet the needs of contemporary audiences of all ages.
### Importance of the Problem
1.  **Policy support:** In 2019, the National Radio and Television Administration launched the *Chinese Classic Folk Story Animation Creation Project*, which explicitly supports the adaptation and creation of animated works based on traditional folk stories including myths, as part of the national strategy for the inheritance of excellent traditional Chinese culture.
2.  **Market demand:** Since 2015, myth-themed animated films such as *Monkey King: Hero Is Back*, *White Snake: Origin*, and *Ne Zha: Birth of the Demon Child* have achieved outstanding box office results (with *Ne Zha* grossing over 5 billion RMB), proving the huge market potential of myth-themed works. However, the industry also faces widespread problems such as uneven work quality, repetitive theme selection, and inconsistent integration of traditional and modern elements.
3.  **Research gap:** Existing studies mostly focus on single aspects such as the integration of domestic animation and traditional culture, character shaping, or value adaptation, and lack a systematic research framework for the innovative expression of mythological themes in domestic animated films, with limited practical guidance for creation.
### Innovative Entry Point
The paper takes story innovation and visual narrative innovation as two core entry points, sorts out the diachronic influence of mythological themes on domestic animated films from the 1930s to the 21st century, combines in-depth case studies of representative works in the new century, explores the inherent law of adaptation and transformation of mythological themes, and puts forward targeted development suggestions for the industry.
## 2.2. Main Contributions / Findings
### Primary Contributions
1.  Constructed a complete diachronic research framework for the influence of mythological themes on Chinese domestic animated films, sorting out the development trajectory and characteristic of such works in four historical stages.
2.  Summarized the general motif sequence of Chinese mythological stories, and analyzed how new century animated films adjust the motif arrangement to realize story innovation while retaining the core of traditional myths.
3.  Explored the cross-media breakthrough path and aesthetic integration strategy of mythological themes in visual narrative innovation, and proposed forward-looking development directions including building a Chinese myth matrix and deepening the exploration of traditional ethnic style.
### Key Conclusions
1.  Mythological themes have always been an important source of creative inspiration for domestic animated films, and their role has evolved from providing basic story texts in the early stage to providing creative thinking and profound cultural connotations in the new century.
2.  Story innovation of myth-themed animated films in the new century is mainly realized through three paths: motif reconstruction, character modernization transformation, and modern rationalization of traditional plots, which can integrate modern values while retaining the core of traditional myths.
3.  Visual narrative innovation needs to follow the law of cross-media transcoding, integrate traditional Chinese aesthetic elements with modern art and ACGN (Animation, Comic, Game, Novel) culture, to create works with both traditional charm and contemporary aesthetic characteristics.
4.  The current development of myth-themed animated films still faces three key problems: low-level repeated theme selection, abrupt integration of heterogeneous elements, and excessive vulgar content, which need to be improved in future creation.

# 3. Prerequisite Knowledge & Related Work
## 3.1. Foundational Concepts
To understand this paper, beginners need to master the following core concepts:
- **Broad definition of myth theme:** Proposed by leading Chinese mythologist Yuan Ke, it covers 9 categories of folk narrative works: myth, legend, historical folklore, fairy tales, weird stories, folk tales with fairy tale characteristics, mythological characters and stories derived from Buddhist scriptures, mythological legends related to festivals, spells, treasures, customs and local landscapes, and mythological legends of Chinese ethnic minorities. This is the definition of myth theme adopted in this paper, which is broader than the narrow definition of primitive prehistoric myths.
- **Motif:** A basic structural element that repeatedly appears in folk literature narratives, identified by comparing different stories. It can be a character type, plot background, or single event, and has the function of promoting plot development. It is the smallest stable unit in folk narrative, and different permutations and combinations of motifs can generate rich and diverse story forms.
- **Cross-media narrative:** A concept proposed by American communication scholar Henry Jenkins, which refers to the practice of expanding a unified story world across multiple media platforms. Each medium makes full use of its own advantages to expand and supplement the story content, so that the audience can obtain a complete immersive experience of the story world.
- **ACGN culture:** An umbrella term for Animation, Comic, Game, and Novel subculture, which originated in Japan and has become a mainstream youth subculture in China, with unique aesthetic preferences and narrative logic focusing on strong visual impact, character charm, and emotional resonance.
- **Chinese School of Animation:** Refers to Chinese animation works created from the 1950s to the 1980s, which absorbed a large number of traditional Chinese art elements such as ink painting, paper-cutting, puppet show, and Peking Opera, forming a unique national style that enjoyed high reputation in the international animation industry. Representative works include *Havoc in Heaven* (1964) and *The Cowboy's Flute* (1963).
## 3.2. Previous Works
The paper builds on the following key prior studies:
1.  **Research on myth and animation creation:** Foreign studies include Hayao Miyazaki's statement that his animated works draw inspiration from Japanese folk legends, Walt Disney's view that animation turns myths and legends into vivid performances, and Nietzsche's argument in *The Birth of Tragedy* that myth is the foundation of a nation's cultural creativity. Domestic studies include animation pioneer Wan Laiming's emphasis that Chinese animation must take the nationalization road, and scholar Wang Xiaoyin's point that domestic animation is the most suitable cultural expression carrier for traditional Chinese myths.
2.  **Research on motif application:** Stith Thompson's *Folktale Classification* divides motifs into three categories: characters, plot backgrounds, and single events. Ding Naitong's *Index of Types of Chinese Folk Stories* sorted out 843 motif types of Chinese folk stories. Zhang Yanju proposed that motifs are the cultural code of a nation, which evolve into different forms in different eras while retaining core cultural connotations.
3.  **Research on character shaping and aesthetic integration:** Wei Yuanliang analyzed the composite character setting in *Ne Zha: Birth of the Demon Child*, Wu Bin pointed out that innovative adaptation of traditional myth characters needs to break the inherent binary opposition stereotype. Xu Chunrong proposed that the core of traditional aesthetic of domestic animation is the artistic thinking of "between likeness and unlikeness", which balances respect for tradition and innovative expression.
4.  **Research on cross-media development:** Henry Jenkins' cross-media narrative theory, and domestic studies on IP development of animation in the Internet era, which point out that myth themes can learn from mature IP operation strategies to build series of works and expand industrial value.
## 3.3. Technological Evolution
The development of Chinese myth-themed animated films has gone through four distinct stages:
1.  **1930s-1940s: Rise stage:** The first Chinese animated feature *Princess Iron Fan* (1941), adapted from *Journey to the West*, marked the beginning of Chinese myth-themed animation. In the context of the Anti-Japanese War, myths were used as a carrier to publicize the spirit of national resistance.
2.  **1950s-1960s: Maturity stage:** The establishment of Shanghai Animation Film Studio led to the emergence of the "Chinese School of Animation". Representative works include *The Magic Brush* (puppet animation), *Zhu Bajie Eats Watermelon* (paper-cut animation), and *Havoc in Heaven* (1964), which combined myth themes with traditional Chinese art forms, bringing Chinese animation to the peak of international recognition.
3.  **1980s-1990s: Re-rise and transition stage:** Representative works include *Legend of Sealed Book* (1983) and *The Monkey King Conquers the Demon* (1985), which began to try bolder adaptation of myths and explore the human nature of myth characters. Later, affected by the influx of foreign animation and market-oriented reform, the overall quality of domestic animation works declined.
4.  **21st century: Innovation and upgrading stage:** After the phenomenal success of *Monkey King: Hero Is Back* (2015), myth-themed animated films entered a period of rapid development, combining 3D animation technology, modern values, and ACGN culture to explore new adaptation paths. This paper focuses on the works of this stage.
## 3.4. Differentiation Analysis
Compared with previous studies, the core innovations of this paper are:
1.  **Systematic research framework:** It integrates story innovation and visual narrative innovation as two core dimensions, combines diachronic historical sorting and synchronic case analysis to form a complete research system for myth-themed domestic animated films, making up for the lack of targeted systematic research in previous studies.
2.  **Novel motif sequence analysis perspective:** It summarizes the general motif sequence of Chinese myth stories, and analyzes how contemporary animated films adjust the motif arrangement to realize story innovation, which is a new research perspective compared with previous studies that only focus on single motif application.
3.  **Practically oriented development suggestions:** It puts forward targeted, operable development paths such as series development of Chinese myth matrix and deep mining of traditional ethnic style, avoiding the empty theoretical discussion of previous studies, and has strong guiding significance for practical animation creation.

# 4. Methodology
## 4.1. Principles
The core research idea of the paper is to take the innovative transformation of myth themes in domestic animated films as the core, combine historical sorting and multi-case comparative analysis, explore the inherent law of integration between traditional myths and modern animation creation, and provide practical guidance for future creation. Its theoretical basis includes folk literature theory (motif theory), communication theory (cross-media narrative theory), and film and television art theory (narrative theory, aesthetic theory).
## 4.2. Core Methodology In-depth
The paper uses three complementary research methods:
### 1. Literature Analysis Method
- **Principle:** By systematically retrieving and sorting relevant academic literature, industry reports, and policy documents, the researcher understands the current research status, learns from valuable existing research results, and forms independent research ideas and basic analytical frameworks.
- **Execution process:** The author sorted out three categories of literature:
  1.  Myth research theoretical literature, including works by Yuan Ke, Mao Dun, and Stith Thompson, to clarify the definition and characteristics of myth themes.
  2.  Animation industry and creation research literature, including industry development reports, research on the history of Chinese animation, and animation creation theory.
  3.  Case analysis literature on representative myth-themed animated works, including studies on *Monkey King: Hero Is Back*, *Ne Zha: Birth of the Demon Child*, and other hit works.
      On this basis, the author sorted out the diachronic development trajectory of myth-themed animated films, and constructed the dual-dimensional analysis framework of story innovation and visual narrative innovation.
### 2. Case Study Method
- **Principle:** By selecting representative, typical cases for in-depth analysis, the researcher summarizes the general law applicable to the whole category of works.
- **Execution process:** The paper selects three representative high-quality works in the new century as core research cases: *Monkey King: Hero Is Back* (2015), *White Snake: Origin* (2019), *Ne Zha: Birth of the Demon Child* (2019). These three works are all adapted from classic Chinese myths, have achieved outstanding box office and word-of-mouth results, and represent the highest level of innovative adaptation of myth-themed animated films in the new century. The author conducts in-depth analysis from the aspects of motif arrangement, character shaping, plot adaptation, visual narrative, and aesthetic integration, to summarize successful adaptation experience.
- **Motif sequence analysis (core analytical tool):** The paper summarizes the general motif sequence of Chinese myth stories as:
  \$
  M \rightarrow C \rightarrow B \rightarrow Et \rightarrow Sh \rightarrow B' \rightarrow Se
  \$
  Where:
  - $M$: `take mission`, the core task that runs through the whole film for the main character, including rescue missions, crisis resolution, atonement, etc.
  - $C$: `main character`, the core character around which all functional motifs revolve, usually a supernatural character (god, demon, immortal) with special abilities.
  - $B$: `bound`, the restriction on the ability or body of the main character, which can be magical artifacts, power suppression by higher-level gods, or amnesia.
  - `Et`: `experience in test`, the experience and test that the main character needs to go through to achieve growth, including descending to the mortal world for practice, subduing demons, learning spells, etc.
  - `Sh`: `supernatural helpers`, characters or magical artifacts that help the main character overcome difficulties, including magical weapons, extraordinary companions, gods with special abilities, and magical animals.
  - $B'$: `free from bondage`, the lifting of the restriction on the main character, which is usually the result of passing the experience test, and prepares the main character for the final battle.
  - `Se`: `supernatural enemy`, the final opponent against the main character, usually a demon, evil god, or irresistible natural force.
    The following figure (Figure 1 from the original paper) shows this general motif sequence:

    ![图1中国神话故事的通用母题序列图](images/1.jpg)
    *该图像是示意图，展示了中国神话故事中的通用母题序列，包括角色、任务和神奇物品等元素。该图呈现了主要角色与其经历之间的关系，以及超自然角色的帮助和影响。*

  Then the author analyzes the adaptive adjustment of motif arrangement in the three cases:
  - *Monkey King: Hero Is Back*: Sets three layers of bondage for Sun Wukong (sealed under Five Elements Mountain, restraining spell on his back, Dharma seal on his wrist that suppresses his power), repeats the `free from bondage` motif twice: first unlocking physical bondage, then unlocking ideological bondage (self-redemption), to highlight the theme of regaining初心.
  - *White Snake: Origin*: Repeats the `supernatural enemy` motif to increase the difficulty of the protagonist's experience, adds the `demonization` motif to turn the human character Xu Xuan into a supernatural helper, highlighting the modern theme of equal love across species.
    The following figure (Figure 3 from the original paper) shows the motif arrangement of *White Snake: Origin*:

    ![图3电影《白蛇：缘起》的母题排列关系图](images/2.jpg)
    *该图像是电影《白蛇：缘起》的母题排列关系图，展示了各个角色及其关系。图中主要角色与背景任务之间的联系，以及不同角色之间的互动关系，形成了该片的故事脉络。*

  - *Ne Zha: Birth of the Demon Child*: Sets two layers of experience test: the first is to learn spells and shape correct values, the second is the ideological test after learning the truth of his identity as a demon pill reincarnation, adjusts the motif order to highlight the modern theme of "my fate is determined by myself, not by heaven".
    The following figure (Figure 4 from the original paper) shows the motif arrangement of *Ne Zha: Birth of the Demon Child*:

### 3. Comparative Analysis Method
- **Principle:** By comparing myth-themed animated works with different box office and word-of-mouth performance, the researcher summarizes the favorable paths for the integration of myth themes and modern animation, and the problems that should be avoided in creation.
- **Execution process:** The author compares works of the same theme with different quality:
  1.  Compares the successful three-dimensional character shaping in *Ne Zha: Birth of the Demon Child* with the flat, stereotyped character shaping in low-quality myth-themed works.
  2.  Compares the natural integration of traditional aesthetic elements in *Monkey King: Hero Is Back* with the abrupt element integration in *White Snake: Origin* (the Medusa-like shape of the snake mother antagonist, which is inconsistent with Chinese myth context).
  3.  Compares the appropriate use of folk carnival language in *Ne Zha* (Sichuan dialect spoken by Taiyi Zhenren) with the excessive vulgar content in some low-quality works, to summarize experience and lessons of innovative adaptation.

## 5.2. Evaluation Metrics
As a humanities and art research, the evaluation metrics are qualitative comprehensive evaluation indicators:
### Story Innovation Evaluation
It includes three dimensions:
1.  **Motif innovation:** Whether the motif arrangement is reasonable, and whether it has new era connotation while retaining the core of traditional myth.
2.  **Character innovation:** Whether the character setting is three-dimensional, and whether the modern transformation is natural and in line with modern values.
3.  **Plot innovation:** Whether the traditional plot is modernized and rationalized, whether the logic is smooth, and whether the integration of modern values is natural.
### Visual Narrative Innovation Evaluation
It includes two dimensions:
1.  **Cross-media transcoding effect:** Whether the cross-media transformation from text myth to animation image is natural, and whether it meets the aesthetic preferences of contemporary audiences (including ACGN culture audience).
2.  **Aesthetic integration effect:** Whether the traditional Chinese aesthetic elements are naturally integrated with modern art, and whether the work has unique national style and aesthetic value.
### Market and Social Value Evaluation
Including box office performance, audience word-of-mouth (scores on mainstream platforms such as Douban and Maoyan), and the effect of spreading traditional culture and guiding positive values.
## 5.3. Baselines
The baselines for comparison in the paper are:
1.  **Classic traditional myth-themed animated works:** Such as *Havoc in Heaven* (1964), *Nezha Conquers the Dragon King* (1979), representing the highest level of traditional Chinese myth animation, to compare the innovation of new century works.
2.  **Low-quality myth-themed animated works:** Such as low-cost, low-word-of-mouth *Journey to the West* adapted works, to compare the advantages of high-quality works and summarize existing problems.
3.  **Foreign outstanding animated works adapted from myths/folk tales:** Such as Disney's *Mulan*, *Moana*, Pixar's *Coco*, to compare the gap between domestic works and the international top level, and learn from advanced experience.

# 6. Results & Analysis
## 6.1. Core Results Analysis
### Diachronic Development Law Results
The proportion of myth-themed works in domestic animated films showed an upward trend in the first three stages (3.8% in the 1930s-40s, 5% in the 1950s-60s, 6.5% in the 1980s-90s), and slightly decreased to 5.7% in the 2000s-2010s, but the absolute number increased significantly. The role of myth themes has evolved: from providing basic story texts in the early stage, to serving as a "springboard" for exploring national animation forms in the 1950s-60s, to providing creative thinking and cultural connotations in the new century.
### Story Innovation Results
The story innovation of new century myth-themed animated films is mainly realized in three ways:
1.  **Motif reconstruction:** By adjusting the order, adding or deleting motifs, traditional myth stories are endowed with modern connotations. For example, *Ne Zha: Birth of the Demon Child* adjusts the motif arrangement to turn the traditional theme of "filial piety and resistance to feudal oppression" into the modern theme of "fighting against fate and breaking prejudice".
2.  **Character modernization transformation:** By setting composite overlapping character identities, modernizing character positioning, and enriching female character images, characters are more in line with modern values. For example, the image of Ne Zha's mother Lady Yin in *Ne Zha* is transformed from a marginal supporting role in the original *Investiture of the Gods* to an independent professional woman who balances career and family, reflecting the improvement of modern women's social status.
3.  **Modern rationalization of traditional plots:** Delete feudal dross and violent and bloody content in traditional myths, adjust plot logic to conform to modern values. For example, in *Ne Zha*, the contradiction between Ne Zha and the Dragon King is adjusted from Ne Zha's unprovoked troublemaking in the original work to the conflict caused by fate arrangement, which makes the plot more logical and acceptable to contemporary audiences.
### Visual Narrative Innovation Results
1.  **Cross-media breakthrough:** By retaining the core elements of the myth, extending the narrative through multi-media, and integrating ACGN culture, myth stories are successfully transformed from text to 3D animation images. For example, *Monkey King: Hero Is Back* uses 3D special effects to present the battle scene of Sun Wukong, which meets the aesthetic preferences of contemporary young audiences.
2.  **Aesthetic integration:** By integrating traditional Chinese aesthetic elements such as ink painting, green landscape painting, traditional opera, and folk music into animation production, works form a unique "Chinese style" aesthetic. For example, the "Mountains and Rivers State Map" scene in *Ne Zha* draws on the aesthetic of the Song Dynasty masterpiece *A Thousand Li of Rivers and Mountains*, presenting a unique traditional aesthetic charm.
### Industry Problem Results
The paper identifies three main problems in the current development of myth-themed animated films:
1.  **Low-level repeated theme selection:** From 1940 to 2020, there are 35 animated works adapted from *Journey to the West*, and the over-development of Sun Wukong and other classic characters leads to audience aesthetic fatigue.
2.  **Abrupt element integration:** Some works blindly integrate Western elements or modern science fiction elements, resulting in a sense of tear between the work and the myth background. For example, the snake mother antagonist in *White Snake: Origin* has a shape similar to Medusa in Greek mythology, which is inconsistent with the Chinese myth background.
3.  **Vulgar content:** Some works excessively use crude language and vulgar plot to please the audience, which is not suitable for the family audience group of animated films.

## 6.3. Component Validation Analysis
The paper implicitly verifies the effectiveness of different adaptation strategies through comparative analysis:
1.  **Validation of motif adaptation strategy:** By comparing the motif arrangement of successful works and failed works, it is found that retaining the core motif of traditional myths while making reasonable adjustments to the motif sequence can not only retain the cultural connotation of traditional myths, but also endow the work with modern connotation, which is an effective adaptation strategy. If the core motif is completely discarded, the work will lose the cultural foundation of the myth and be difficult to be accepted by the audience.
2.  **Validation of character adaptation strategy:** By comparing the character shaping of *Ne Zha* (Lady Yin as an independent professional woman) and traditional works (Lady Yin as a marginal supporting role), it is found that the modern transformation of characters in line with contemporary social values can significantly improve the audience's recognition of the characters and the work. If the character setting still retains the feudal dross of the original myth, it will be rejected by contemporary audiences.
3.  **Validation of aesthetic integration strategy:** By comparing the natural integration of traditional aesthetic elements in *Monkey King: Hero Is Back* and *Ne Zha* with the abrupt element integration in some works, it is found that the integration of traditional aesthetic elements must be consistent with the story background and narrative logic, and blind stacking of elements will cause a sense of tear and reduce the aesthetic experience of the audience.

# 7. Conclusion & Reflections
## 7.1. Conclusion Summary
This paper systematically sorts out the diachronic development trajectory of Chinese myth-themed domestic animated films, takes story innovation and visual narrative innovation as two core dimensions, analyzes the innovative adaptation strategies of representative works in the new century, summarizes the development law and existing industry problems, and puts forward future development directions. The main conclusions are:
1.  Mythological themes are an important creative source for domestic animated films, and their integration with animation has natural advantages: they can not only provide rich story materials for animation creation, but also endow works with profound cultural connotations, and promote the inheritance and dissemination of traditional Chinese culture.
2.  The innovative adaptation of myth-themed animated films in the new century has achieved remarkable results. Through motif reconstruction, character modernization transformation, modern rationalization of traditional plots, cross-media visual breakthrough, and integration of traditional aesthetics and modern art, a number of excellent works have been created, which are widely recognized by the market and audiences.
3.  The current development of myth-themed animated films still faces three key problems: low-level repeated theme selection, abrupt element integration, and vulgar content, which need to be improved in future creation.
4.  The future development of myth-themed animated films should focus on two directions: first, carry out series development of Chinese myth matrix, build a unified Chinese myth universe, and avoid repeated development of individual popular themes; second, deepen the excavation of traditional ethnic style, make good use of national cultural elements, and create Chinese cultural symbols with international influence.
## 7.2. Limitations & Future Work
### Limitations of the Paper
1.  The selected cases are mainly high-quality works with good box office and word-of-mouth, and the analysis of low- and medium-quality works is relatively insufficient, which may affect the generalization of conclusions to the entire industry.
2.  The study focuses on works produced before 2021, and lacks analysis of myth-themed animated films after 2021 (such as *New Gods: Yang Jian*, *Deep Sea*, etc.), so the research conclusions need to be updated with the development of the industry.
### Future Research Directions
1.  Expand the scope of case studies, include more works of different quality and different types, and further improve the universality of the summarized adaptation law.
2.  Conduct in-depth research on the cross-media operation of myth-themed IP, explore the practical path of building a Chinese myth universe, and promote the overseas dissemination of Chinese myth culture.
3.  Study the adaptation of ethnic minority myth themes, enrich the types of myth-themed animated films, and show the diversity of Chinese national culture.
