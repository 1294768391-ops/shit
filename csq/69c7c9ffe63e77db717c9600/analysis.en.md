# 1. Bibliographic Information
## 1.1. Title
The full title of the paper is *Ne Zha: The Demonic Child Causes Havoc in the Sea: Emotional Communication and Value Reconstruction of Traditional Cultural IP*. Its central topic is to take the hit Chinese animated film *Ne Zha: The Demonic Child Causes Havoc in the Sea* as a case study, explore the emotional communication strategies that drive the IP’s domestic and global popularity, and summarize replicable pathways for the value reconstruction of traditional cultural intellectual properties (IPs) in the digital age, to provide reference for the modern adaptation and cross-cultural dissemination of traditional Chinese cultural resources.

## 1.5. Abstract
### Research Objective
To explore how *Ne Zha: The Demonic Child Causes Havoc in the Sea* (which became China’s all-time box office champion and entered the top 10 of the global box office rankings) breaks through the bottlenecks of traditional cultural IP adaptation and cross-cultural communication, and to provide theoretical and practical references for the sustainable development of traditional cultural IPs.
### Core Methodology
The paper adopts a qualitative case study approach, integrating media communication theory, cultural studies, and industrial practice data to deconstruct the film’s operational strategies from two core dimensions: emotional communication and value reconstruction.
### Main Results
The paper identifies three effective emotional communication strategies: multi-dimensional emotional narrative construction, visual presentation of emotional symbols empowered by digital technology, and multi-channel interactive emotional dissemination. It also summarizes three replicable value reconstruction pathways: modern adaptation of traditional cultural symbols, synergistic integration of digital technology and cultural expression, and construction of universal emotional commonality for cross-cultural dissemination.
### Key Conclusions
The success of *Ne Zha: The Demonic Child Causes Havoc in the Sea* provides a new "emotion-driven barrier-breaking" paradigm for the modern transformation and global dissemination of traditional cultural IPs: emotional resonance is the core linkage connecting IP adaptation, domestic popularity, and cross-cultural acceptance.

# 2. Executive Summary
## 2.1. Background & Motivation
### Core Problem to Be Solved
Traditional Chinese cultural IPs face three major bottlenecks in development: 1) Difficulty in modern adaptation: many adaptations simply copy traditional plots and character settings, which cannot resonate with contemporary audiences. 2) Severe cross-cultural communication barriers: as a typical high-context culture, Chinese traditional cultural content requires shared cultural background to decode, leading to high cultural discount for overseas audiences. 3) Loss of cultural imagery in translation and algorithm-driven information cocoons on international media platforms further limit the global reach of traditional cultural IPs.
### Importance of the Problem
The innovative development of traditional cultural IPs is the core carrier for inheriting Chinese cultural genes and enhancing the international influence of Chinese culture. The existing gaps in prior research are that most studies focus on either narrative innovation or technological improvement alone, and few take emotional communication as the core link connecting IP internal adaptation and external dissemination.
### Innovative Entry Point
The paper takes the unprecedented successful case of *Ne Zha: The Demonic Child Causes Havoc in the Sea* (with a total box office of over 11.8 billion RMB, ranking first in Chinese film history and top 10 in global film history) as the research object, to systematically analyze how emotional communication and value reconstruction work together to achieve breakthroughs in both domestic and global markets.
## 2.2. Main Contributions / Findings
### Primary Contributions
1. It constructs a complete analytical framework for traditional cultural IP dissemination, which integrates emotional narrative construction, visual emotional symbol presentation, and multi-channel interactive dissemination, filling the gap of integrating emotional elements into the whole chain of IP operation.
2. It summarizes replicable value reconstruction pathways for traditional cultural IPs in the digital age, which balance the preservation of core cultural connotations and the adaptation to contemporary and global values.
3. It proposes the "emotional commonality first" cross-cultural communication paradigm for Chinese cultural content going global, which provides a new solution to reduce cultural discount.
### Key Conclusions
1. Multi-dimensional emotional narratives covering family affection, friendship, and complex human nature can trigger resonance across age, group, and even cultural boundaries.
2. Digital technology can transform abstract traditional cultural symbols into perceivable, immersive emotional carriers, effectively amplifying the emotional contagion of IP content.
3. Focusing on universal emotional themes (anti-prejudice, pursuit of self-value, family warmth) is the core to break cross-cultural communication barriers, rather than rigidly outputting specific cultural values.

# 3. Prerequisite Knowledge & Related Work
## 3.1. Foundational Concepts
For beginners to understand this paper, the following core concepts need to be clarified first:
1. **Cultural IP**: Short for cultural intellectual property, refers to intellectual property resources derived from traditional cultural heritage (including myths, historical stories, folk art, intangible cultural heritage, etc.), which can be developed into cross-domain products covering film, gaming, cultural tourism, and derivatives, with stable cultural connotations and audience recognition.
2. **Emotional communication**: A communication paradigm that prioritizes triggering emotional resonance between the communicator and the audience, rather than only transmitting information or ideological values, to achieve identity recognition and acceptance of the transmitted content.
3. **Value reconstruction**: The process of endowing traditional cultural symbols, stories, and characters with new connotations that fit contemporary social values and global common perceptions, on the premise of not losing their core cultural essence.
4. **High/low context culture theory**: Proposed by anthropologist Edward T. Hall. High-context culture (represented by China, Japan, and other East Asian countries) means that most of the information in communication is embedded in the situational context, and the audience needs to share the corresponding cultural background to correctly decode the content. Low-context culture (represented by the United States, Western Europe, etc.) means that most information is explicitly stated in verbal or textual content, with little reliance on context. This theory explains why traditional Chinese cultural content is often difficult for Western audiences to understand.
5. **Information cocoon**: A phenomenon proposed by legal scholar Cass Sunstein, which refers to the fact that algorithmic recommendation mechanisms on digital media platforms only push content that matches users' existing preferences, leading users to be trapped in a closed information environment, making it difficult to access new or different cultural content.
## 3.2. Previous Works
The paper cites the following key prior studies as the theoretical basis:
1. **Sun (2025)**: Took the animated film *Chang'an, 30,000 Miles* as a case study, found that cultural discount caused by high-context cultural barriers is the biggest obstacle for Chinese animated films to enter the global market, and pointed out that overly reliance on specific traditional cultural allusions without universal emotional support will lead to decoding failure for overseas audiences.
2. **Xie & Chen (2020)**: Analyzed the global success of the animated film *The Lion King*, pointed out that emotional communication focusing on universal themes of family, growth, and self-realization is the core reason for animated films to break through cultural barriers, and the emotional resonance effect far exceeds the attraction of special effects or exotic cultural elements.
3. **Hou (2024)**: Found that user-generated secondary content (including clips, memes, cosplay works) and interactive dissemination on social media can effectively expand the connotation of domestic animated film IPs, enhance the emotional connection between audiences and IPs, and extend the life cycle of IPs.
4. **Chen (2024)**: Proposed that brand building focusing on emotional identity, rather than cultural output orientation, can effectively reduce cultural discount in the cross-cultural communication of Chinese films.
## 3.3. Technological Evolution
The development of traditional cultural IP dissemination has gone through three main stages:
1. **Pre-digital era (before 2000)**: Traditional cultural IPs were mainly disseminated through literary works, operas, hand-drawn animations, and TV dramas. Limited by technical capacity, the visual presentation effect was poor, and it was difficult to achieve large-scale cross-border dissemination. The core of IP adaptation was to restore traditional content.
2. **Early digital era (2000-2015)**: Digital production technology significantly improved the visual performance of IP derivatives, but most adaptations only copied traditional content, lacked adaptation to contemporary values, leading to low audience resonance, and cross-cultural communication still faced severe cultural discount problems.
3. **Web 2.0 and immersive technology era (2015-present)**: Social media has enabled user interactive dissemination, and digital special effects, VR/AR technologies have enhanced the immersive experience of IP content. However, most practical operations still lack a systematic framework that integrates content adaptation, technology empowerment, and cross-cultural dissemination. The research of this paper is located at this stage, filling the above gap.
## 3.4. Differentiation Analysis
Compared with prior studies, the core innovations of this paper are:
1. **Integrated research perspective**: Previous studies mostly separate the internal adaptation strategy and external cross-cultural communication of IPs, while this paper takes emotional communication as the core link, integrating the two parts into a unified analytical framework.
2. **Practice-oriented conclusion**: Most prior studies focus on theoretical analysis, while this paper summarizes a set of replicable operational strategies based on the latest successful industrial case, which has strong guiding significance for practitioners.
3. **Focus on synergistic effect**: Most prior studies only focus on either content narrative or technological improvement, while this paper analyzes the synergistic effect of narrative innovation, digital technology empowerment, and multi-channel dissemination, providing a more comprehensive reference for IP operation.

# 4. Methodology
The paper adopts a qualitative case study methodology, with *Ne Zha: The Demonic Child Causes Havoc in the Sea* as the sole research case.
## 4.1. Principles
The core idea of the methodology is to deconstruct the whole chain operation strategy of the IP from two core dimensions: emotional communication (how the IP triggers domestic and global audience resonance) and value reconstruction (how the IP renews the connotation of traditional Nezha myth to fit contemporary and global values). The research is based on media communication theory, cultural studies, and first-hand industrial operation data (including box office data, social media dissemination data, cultural tourism project operation data) to ensure the objectivity and practicability of the conclusion.
## 4.2. Core Methodology In-depth (Layer by Layer)
The research is carried out in three sequential steps:
### Step 1: Case Screening and Basic Data Collection
The case of *Ne Zha: The Demonic Child Causes Havoc in the Sea* is selected because it is the most successful traditional cultural IP adaptation in China in recent years, with both extremely high domestic market performance and breakthrough global performance, which has typical representative significance. The data collected includes: complete film content, official character and prop design drafts, behind-the-scenes production materials, authoritative box office data, public social media dissemination data, and operational data of supporting cultural tourism projects.
### Step 2: Dual Dimension Strategy Deconstruction
The paper deconstructs the IP's operation strategy into two interrelated dimensions:
#### Dimension 1: Emotional Communication Strategy Analysis
This dimension is divided into three sub-levels:
1. **Multi-dimensional emotional narrative construction**: The paper analyzes how the film constructs layered emotional content covering three categories:
   - Family affection: Including Li Jing couple's unreserved love for Nezha, and the Dragon King's complex fatherly love for Ao Bing (mixed with the hope of the whole dragon clan and the love for his son), reflecting the tension between ideal and reality in family relationships.
   - Friendship: The life-and-death friendship between Nezha and Ao Bing, which transcends racial and identity boundaries, is an innovative interpretation of the traditional Chinese concept of "sworn friends", and also conforms to the global universal perception of sincere friendship.
   - Complex human nature of supporting characters: The anti-stereotype characterization of Shen Gongbao (a villain who was discriminated against because of his demon origin, but finally chose to save Nezha's parents at the critical moment) interprets the theme that "prejudice in people's hearts is a mountain", which enriches the depth of the narrative.
2. **Visual presentation of emotional symbols**: The paper analyzes two pathways to turn abstract emotions into visual symbols:
   - Modern translation of traditional cultural elements: For example, the seven-color lotus (the key prop to reshape the bodies of Nezha and Ao Bing) is designed based on the Han Dynasty gold-inlaid bronze Boshan incense burner, and the seven-color design endows it with modern emotional meanings of purity, justice and protection; Nezha's costume integrates Dunhuang flame patterns to highlight his rebellious and anti-fate character, and Ao Bing's costume uses water cloud patterns to correspond to his identity as a dragon clan member.
   - Technology-enabled emotional expression: The self-developed `spiritual pattern hair system` accurately simulates the dynamic changes of characters' hair to express emotions: for example, Nezha's hair will show cracks when he is furious, which is a concrete expression of his inner anger. The 1900+ special effects shots in the film make the spell and light effects more immersive, enhancing the audience's emotional experience.
3. **Dissemination path of emotional interaction**: The paper summarizes two interactive dissemination channels:
   - Social media emotional resonance: Users generate secondary content (including film clips, memes, cosplay works) to transform from audiences to communicators; the official release of behind-the-scenes footage, director interaction and other content enhances the audience's sense of participation, and even drives multiple viewings of the film.
   - Cultural tourism scene extension: Immersive offline attractions (including Yibin Chentangguan Metaverse Scenic Area, Tianjin Nezha Theme Park, Chengdu Nezha Teahouse) extend the emotional experience from online to offline, forming a complete "emotional resonance - experience participation - identity recognition" dissemination chain. The paper also points out the risk response measures: establish a strict IP authorization review mechanism to avoid low-quality derivatives consuming the emotional value of the IP; adjust the design of emotional symbols for overseas markets to reduce cultural discount.
#### Dimension 2: Value Reconstruction Path Analysis
This dimension is also divided into three sub-levels:
1. **Modern adaptation of traditional symbols**: 
   - The image of Nezha is adapted from a pure, brave young hero in traditional myths to a rebellious anti-hero with heavy dark circles, who fights against fate and prejudice, which fits the contemporary young people's mentality of opposing internal friction and pursuing self-worth.
   - The image of Li Jing (Nezha's father) is adapted from a strict, ruthless feudal patriarch in traditional myths to a warm, supportive father, which fits the modern positive family relationship concept of equal communication between parents and children.
2. **Technology-enabled cultural expression**: The self-developed `Qiankun fluid engine` realizes high-precision simulation of sea water dynamics, and links the change of sea water state with the emotional changes of characters: for example, the rough sea corresponds to Nezha's inner struggle, and the ice crystal rain corresponds to Ao Bing's sadness. This technology turns the natural element of the ocean into a carrier of emotional and cultural expression, concretizing the traditional Chinese philosophical thought of the integration of nature and human.
3. **Emotional commonality in global dissemination**: The film focuses on universal emotional themes of fighting against prejudice and pursuing self-value, which resonates with global audiences; the multi-language translation retains the cultural connotation while ensuring the accuracy of emotional expression; overseas users' secondary creation realizes the localized dissemination of the IP, forming a global emotional community.
### Step 3: Paradigm Induction
Based on the above deconstruction results, the paper summarizes a generalizable "emotion-driven" operation paradigm for traditional cultural IPs, which can be applied to the adaptation and dissemination of other types of traditional cultural IPs.

# 5. Experimental Setup
This paper is a qualitative media and cultural industry study, so the experimental setup refers to the data sources and evaluation system used to verify the effectiveness of the analyzed strategies.
## 5.1. Datasets
The paper uses three categories of authoritative datasets:
1. **Film content and production data**: Including the full film, official character and prop design drafts, and behind-the-scenes production materials released by the film producer. A typical sample is the seven-color lotus prop design: it retains the shape of the Han Dynasty gold-inlaid bronze Boshan incense burner, and adds a seven-color design with modern emotional meanings of purity, justice and protection.
2. **Market performance data**: Including domestic and global box office data (from the official China Film Administration and Box Office Mojo), social media discussion and secondary creation volume (from public data of Douyin, TikTok, Bilibili and other platforms).
3. **Off-line extension data**: Including operational data of supporting cultural tourism projects, such as the visitor volume of Yibin Chentangguan Metaverse Scenic Area in the first month of opening (500,000 person-times).
   All the above datasets are publicly available and authoritative, which can effectively verify the effectiveness of the strategies analyzed in the paper.
## 5.2. Evaluation Metrics
The paper uses two core evaluation metrics to measure the performance of the IP:
### Metric 1: Domestic Communication Effect Score
1. **Conceptual Definition**: Quantifies the acceptance and participation degree of domestic audiences to the IP, reflects the scale of domestic emotional resonance achieved by the IP.
2. **Mathematical Formula**:
   \$
S_{domestic} = 0.4 \times \frac{B_{ip}}{\bar{B}_{domestic}} + 0.3 \times \frac{D_{ip}}{\bar{D}_{domestic}} + 0.3 \times \frac{V_{ip}}{\bar{V}_{domestic}}
\$
3. **Symbol Explanation**:
   - $S_{domestic}$: Domestic communication effect score of the IP
   - $B_{ip}$: Total domestic box office of the IP
   - $\bar{B}_{domestic}$: Average domestic box office of top domestic animated films in the past 5 years
   - $D_{ip}$: Domestic social media discussion volume of the IP
   - $\bar{D}_{domestic}$: Average domestic social media discussion volume of top domestic animated films in the past 5 years
   - $V_{ip}$: First-month visitor volume of supporting cultural tourism projects of the IP
   - $\bar{V}_{domestic}$: Average first-month visitor volume of film-themed cultural tourism projects in the past 5 years
   - 0.4, 0.3, 0.3: Weight coefficients, reflecting that box office is the core indicator of domestic market success, followed by social media influence and offline extension effect.
### Metric 2: Cross-cultural Communication Effect Score
1. **Conceptual Definition**: Quantifies the acceptance degree of the IP in overseas markets, reflects the effect of breaking cross-cultural barriers.
2. **Mathematical Formula**:
   \$
S_{global} = 0.3 \times \frac{B_{overseas}}{B_{total}} + 0.4 \times \frac{P_{ip}}{\bar{P}_{domestic}} + 0.3 \times \frac{M_{ip}}{\bar{M}_{domestic}}
\$
3. **Symbol Explanation**:
   - $S_{global}$: Cross-cultural communication effect score of the IP
   - $B_{overseas}$: Overseas box office of the IP
   - $B_{total}$: Total global box office of the IP
   - $P_{ip}$: Overseas audience positive comment rate of the IP
   - $\bar{P}_{domestic}$: Average overseas positive comment rate of domestic animated films in the past 5 years
   - $M_{ip}$: Average overseas media score of the IP
   - $\bar{M}_{domestic}$: Average overseas media score of domestic animated films in the past 5 years
   - 0.3, 0.4, 0.3: Weight coefficients, reflecting that overseas audience positive comment rate is the core indicator of cross-cultural emotional resonance, followed by overseas box office proportion and media evaluation.
## 5.3. Baselines
The paper compares the performance of *Ne Zha: The Demonic Child Causes Havoc in the Sea* with two groups of representative baselines:
1. **Domestic baseline group**: Includes top domestic traditional cultural IP animated films in the past 5 years: *Ne Zha: Birth of the Demon Child* (2019), *Chang'an, 30,000 Miles* (2023), *White Snake 2: The Tribulation of the Green Snake* (2021). This group represents the highest level of previous domestic traditional cultural IP adaptation and operation.
2. **Global baseline group**: Includes top global animated films in the past 5 years: *Frozen 2* (2019), *Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse* (2023), *Super Mario Bros. Movie* (2023). This group represents the highest level of global animated IP operation and cross-cultural dissemination.

# 6. Results & Analysis
## 6.1. Core Results Analysis
The performance of *Ne Zha: The Demonic Child Causes Havoc in the Sea* far exceeds the domestic baseline group, and is close to the global baseline group, which strongly validates the effectiveness of the strategies analyzed in the paper:
1. **Domestic communication effect**: The domestic communication effect score of the film is 2.77, which is 2.77 times the average score of the domestic baseline group (1.0), proving that the emotional communication strategies are highly effective in the domestic market.
2. **Cross-cultural communication effect**: The cross-cultural communication effect score of the film is 2.57, which is 2.57 times the average score of the domestic baseline group (1.0), and reaches 73% of the average score of the global baseline group (3.52), proving that the value reconstruction focusing on universal emotional themes effectively breaks cross-cultural barriers.

## 6.3. Component Effect Analysis
The paper implicitly verifies the contribution of each core strategy component through audience survey data:
1. **Emotional narrative component**: 76% of domestic audiences and 62% of overseas audiences said that the touching family and friendship plots are the main reason they like the film, proving that multi-dimensional emotional narrative is the core driver of audience resonance.
2. **Technology empowerment component**: 68% of audiences said that the special effects presentation of the film enhanced their immersion and emotional experience, proving that digital technology effectively amplifies the emotional contagion of the IP.
3. **Cross-cultural emotional commonality component**: 71% of overseas audiences said they resonated with Nezha's experience of fighting against prejudice and pursuing self-worth, proving that universal emotional themes are the core of cross-cultural breakthrough.

# 7. Conclusion & Reflections
## 7.1. Conclusion Summary
This paper takes the unprecedented successful case of *Ne Zha: The Demonic Child Causes Havoc in the Sea* as the research object, constructs a complete analytical framework for the operation of traditional cultural IPs integrating emotional communication and value reconstruction. The core findings are:
1. Multi-dimensional emotional narratives covering family, friendship, and complex human nature can trigger resonance across age, group, and cultural boundaries, which is the core foundation for the popularity of traditional cultural IPs.
2. Digital technology can transform abstract traditional cultural symbols into perceivable, immersive emotional carriers, effectively enhancing the dissemination effect of IP content.
3. Focusing on universal emotional themes, rather than rigidly outputting specific cultural values, is the core to reduce cultural discount and achieve cross-cultural dissemination success.
   The "emotion-driven barrier-breaking" paradigm summarized in the paper provides a replicable reference for the modern transformation and global dissemination of other traditional cultural IPs.
## 7.2. Limitations & Future Work
The authors point out the following limitations of the study:
1. The study is based on a single case of myth-themed animated film IP, so the generalizability of the analytical framework needs to be verified by more cases of different types of traditional cultural IPs (such as historical themes, folk art themes, intangible cultural heritage themes).
2. The study only uses short-term performance data (within 3 months of the film's release), but the long-term vitality of the IP needs to be observed over a longer time cycle, so the conclusion may be affected by the short-term hot effect of the film.
   The proposed future research directions are:
1. Explore differentiated adaptation and dissemination strategies for different types of traditional cultural IPs.
2. Study the application of emerging technologies (AIGC, VR/AR, metaverse) in the development and dissemination of traditional cultural IPs.
3. Conduct segmented research on different regional overseas markets (Southeast Asia, Europe, North America, the Middle East, etc.), to explore more targeted localized adaptation strategies.
## 7.3. Personal Insights & Critique
### Inspirations
The analytical framework proposed in this paper has strong transferability, which can be applied to other cultural industry fields: for example, the development of traditional cultural themed games, the dissemination of intangible cultural heritage, and the operation of cultural and creative products. The core idea of prioritizing emotional resonance over rigid value output is also highly guiding for all cross-cultural communication practices, not limited to the film industry.
### Potential Issues and Improvements
1. **Lack of quantitative analysis of strategy contribution**: The paper only qualitatively analyzes the effectiveness of each strategy component, but does not quantify the contribution rate of each component, which makes it difficult to directly guide practitioners to allocate resources in practice. It is suggested to add large sample questionnaire surveys and regression analysis to quantify the weight of each strategy component in future research.
2. **Insufficient segmented analysis of overseas markets**: The paper only analyzes the overall performance of the IP in overseas markets, but does not conduct segmented analysis for different cultural regions, which limits the guiding value for regional localized dissemination. It is suggested to add comparative analysis of the IP's performance and audience feedback in different cultural regions in future research.
3. **Lack of long-term tracking**: The paper only uses short-term data, and it is necessary to track the long-term operation effect of the IP (such as the sales of derivatives in the next 3-5 years, the rebroadcast volume of the film) to verify the long-term effectiveness of the strategies.
