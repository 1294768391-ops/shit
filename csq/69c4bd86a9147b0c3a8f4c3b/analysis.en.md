## 1.5. Abstract
### Research Objective
To summarize the general practical paths and innovative logic of recent high-performing domestic Chinese animated films in applying traditional cultural elements, and explore the dual value of this integration for animation industry development and traditional cultural inheritance.
### Core Methodology
The paper adopts a qualitative multi-case study method, taking four representative hit animated works (2015 *Monkey King: Hero is Back*, 2016 *Big Fish & Begonia*, 2019 *White Snake: Origin*, 2019 *Ne Zha: Birth of the Demon Child*) as core samples, and conducts a systematic analysis covering material selection, audio design, visual presentation, and spiritual core dimensions.
### Main Results
The paper identifies four actionable practical paths for applying traditional cultural elements: drawing material from traditional literature, adapting traditional music, utilizing traditional visual symbols, and reshaping traditional cultural spirit. It also extracts two core logics of "upholding fundamental principles while breaking new ground" (`Shou Zheng Chuang Xin`): reconstructing tradition by retaining its spiritual essence while innovating external forms, and expressing contemporary values by connecting closely to modern audience needs.
### Key Conclusions
The effective integration of excellent traditional Chinese culture and animated films can inject unique national characteristics and Chinese artistic spirit into domestic animation, help spread traditional culture and enhance cultural confidence, and promote the generation of new business formats in the film and cultural industry.

# 2. Executive Summary
## 2.1. Background & Motivation
### Core Problem to Solve
After years of stagnation, domestic Chinese animated films achieved explosive growth after 2015, with multiple works achieving both high box office returns and widespread cultural recognition. However, prior research lacked a systematic, cross-case summary of how these works successfully integrated traditional cultural elements to achieve commercial and cultural win-win results, and the generalizable practical paths for this integration remained unclear.
### Importance of the Problem
The animation industry is a core component of the modern cultural industry. Building a distinct national style for domestic animation through the application of traditional cultural resources is not only key to enhancing the international competitiveness of Chinese animation, but also an important path to revitalize traditional culture in the modern context and enhance national cultural confidence.
### Gaps in Prior Research
Previous studies mostly focused on analyzing single works or individual dimensions (such as character modeling or scene design) of traditional cultural element application, lacking a holistic framework that covers both superficial formal application and deep spiritual core transformation, as well as cross-case generalizable conclusions.
### Innovative Entry Point
The paper takes four representative post-2015 hit works as samples, conducts a multi-dimensional analysis covering material, audio, visual, and spiritual dimensions, summarizes both actionable practical paths and underlying innovative logic, and explores the industrial and cultural value of this integration.
## 2.2. Main Contributions / Findings
### Primary Contributions
1. It constructs a four-dimensional practical framework for domestic animated films to apply traditional cultural elements, providing clear operational guidance for subsequent animation creation.
2. It extracts two core logics of creative integration, clarifying the balance point between inheriting the essence of traditional culture and adapting to modern audience needs.
3. It reveals the dual value of this integration: it not only promotes the high-quality development of the domestic animation industry, but also activates the modern vitality of traditional culture and supports cultural output.
### Key Conclusions
1. The integration of traditional cultural elements and animated films does not require rigid copying of traditional content and forms. Creative transformation can be achieved by retaining the core spiritual essence while innovating narrative, character design, and presentation forms, and integrating modern technology and values.
2. Integration practices must be closely aligned with contemporary aesthetic needs and value orientation to eliminate the cognitive distance between traditional culture and modern audiences, and achieve win-win results in commercial, artistic, and cultural dimensions.

# 3. Prerequisite Knowledge & Related Work
## 3.1. Foundational Concepts
All core terms are explained below for beginners:
- **Domestic Chinese animated film**: Refers to animated feature films produced by mainland Chinese institutions, with independent intellectual property rights, and primarily targeting Chinese audiences.
- **Traditional cultural elements**: As defined in the paper, this term covers two categories:
  1. Material form: Tangible cultural products including folk crafts, traditional costumes, utensils, ancient architecture, and national musical instruments.
  2. Intangible form: Intangible cultural content including traditional ideology, values, festivals, folk art, and social customs.
- **Creative transformation of traditional culture**: A core concept in Chinese cultural policy and studies, referring to the process of adapting traditional cultural content, forms, and expressions to fit modern social needs, while retaining its core spiritual essence, so that it can be accepted and spread by contemporary audiences.
- **Upholding fundamental principles while breaking new ground (`Shou Zheng Chuang Xin`)**: A guiding principle for contemporary Chinese cultural development, meaning that cultural creation must first adhere to the excellent core of traditional culture and correct value orientation (the "fundamental principles"), while actively innovating in content, form, and communication methods to adapt to the times (the "new ground").
- **Chinese School of Animation**: The dominant production style of Chinese animation from the 1950s to the 1980s, characterized by heavy borrowing from traditional Chinese art forms (ink painting, paper-cutting, shadow puppetry, Peking Opera), strict adherence to traditional literary plots, and a strong distinct national style. Representative works include *Havoc in Heaven* (1961) and *Nezha Conquers the Dragon King* (1979).
## 3.2. Previous Works
The paper identifies two main streams of prior research:
1. Macro-level studies on the reconstruction, communication, and value identity of traditional culture in Chinese animated films: These studies confirm that traditional culture is the core resource for domestic animation to form its own competitive characteristics, but lack summaries of specific, actionable implementation paths.
2. Single-case studies on traditional cultural element application in individual animated films: These studies analyze specific works from dimensions such as theme selection, visual design, and music, providing rich case data, but lack cross-case induction of general rules and innovative logic.
   The paper also draws on foundational theoretical works:
- Yu Yingshi's *On Tradition*, which proposes that tradition has the characteristics of accumulation and continuity, and a living tradition will not stick to fixed ancient forms, but will evolve into a cultural spirit that runs through ancient and modern history.
- Film theory works on the narrative function of film music, the communicative value of traditional visual symbols, and the logic of cultural industry development.
## 3.3. Technological Evolution
The application of traditional cultural elements in domestic Chinese animated films has gone through three stages:
1. Early exploration stage (1950s-1980s, Chinese School period): The core logic is to "translate" traditional culture directly into animation form, with almost no adaptation of traditional content and form. Production technology was dominated by 2D hand-drawing.
2. Stagnation stage (1990s-2014): Domestic animation was heavily influenced by Japanese and American animation, lost its distinct national style, and the application of traditional cultural elements was mostly superficial and rigid, with very few works achieving both commercial and cultural success.
3. Revival stage (2015-present): Represented by *Monkey King: Hero is Back*, domestic animation began to explore creative transformation of traditional cultural elements, combining 3D animation technology, modern narrative structures, and contemporary values, achieving widespread market and cultural recognition. This paper's research is positioned in this third stage, summarizing the practical experience of this period.
## 3.4. Differentiation Analysis
Compared to prior related studies, the core innovations of this paper are:
1. Broader research scope: It breaks through the limitation of single-case studies, takes four representative hit works covering different themes and audience groups as samples, and induces generalizable rules across cases.
2. More comprehensive research dimension: It covers both superficial formal application (material, music, visual symbols) and deep spiritual core transformation, and incorporates industrial development and cultural inheritance dimensions, forming a more complete analytical framework.
3. More practical conclusions: It not only summarizes specific operational paths, but also extracts the underlying innovative logic, providing both operational guidance for creation and theoretical support for the integration of traditional culture and animation industry.

# 4. Methodology
## 4.1. Principles
The core research principle of this paper is induction from practical cases to generalizable theory: it first collects and sorts out specific practices of traditional cultural element application in the selected sample works, then induces common practical paths from these practices, further extracts the underlying innovative logic of these practices, and finally explores the general significance of these paths for the entire industry and cultural inheritance.
Its theoretical basis includes three parts:
1. Cultural studies theory: On the inheritance, transformation, and modern communication of traditional culture.
2. Film art theory: On animation narrative, film audio design, visual symbol communication, and audience reception logic.
3. Cultural industry theory: On the integration of culture and technology, and the generation of new industrial formats.
## 4.2. Core Methodology In-depth (Layer by Layer)
The research process is carried out in three sequential steps:
### Step 1: Representative Case Selection and Data Collection
The paper selects four sample works based on two criteria: they must have achieved both high commercial success (box office performance) and widespread cultural recognition (audience reputation and industry evaluation). The four samples are:
1. *Monkey King: Hero is Back* (2015): The work that marked the start of the domestic animation revival, with a box office of 956 million RMB, breaking the long-term box office monopoly of foreign animated films in the Chinese market.
2. *Big Fish & Begonia* (2016): Representative work with distinct traditional Chinese aesthetic style, box office of 565 million RMB.
3. *White Snake: Origin* (2019): Representative adult-oriented animated work, breaking the stereotype that domestic animation is only for children, box office of 449 million RMB.
4. *Ne Zha: Birth of the Demon Child* (2019): Phenomenal work with a box office of over 5 billion RMB, ranking second in Chinese film history box office at the time of release.
   The collected data includes the full text of the four films, official behind-the-scenes production materials, director public interviews, audience feedback data, and existing academic research results related to the works and research topic.
### Step 2: Multi-dimensional Analysis of Each Case
The paper analyzes each sample work from four complementary dimensions:
1. **Material selection dimension**: Analyze how works draw narrative materials from traditional literary works (myths, legends, classics), and the adaptation strategies adopted for these materials.
2. **Audio dimension**: Analyze how works integrate traditional music, traditional musical instruments, and traditional vocal art forms (such as Peking Opera singing) into film soundtracks, and the effect of this integration.
3. **Visual dimension**: Analyze how works apply traditional visual symbols in costume design, character modeling, scene design, and color application to shape a distinct national style.
4. **Spiritual core dimension**: Analyze how works interpret and reshape traditional cultural spirits (Confucian, Taoist, Buddhist spirits) to align with modern value orientation, and resonate with contemporary audiences.
### Step 3: Induction of Paths, Logic and Value
Based on cross-case comparative analysis, the paper induces three levels of conclusions:
1. Four generalizable practical paths for applying traditional cultural elements in domestic animated films.
2. Two core innovative logics that support the success of these paths, which conform to the principle of "upholding fundamental principles while breaking new ground".
3. The multi-dimensional value of this integration for the animation industry, traditional cultural inheritance, and national cultural confidence building.

Their practices of applying traditional cultural elements are widely recognized by the industry and audiences, so they can effectively reflect the general current path of domestic animation's use of traditional cultural elements.
## 5.2. Evaluation Metrics
The paper adopts a dual qualitative and quantitative evaluation system to judge the effectiveness of traditional cultural element application:
### 5.2.1. Commercial Effectiveness Metric: Total Box Office
1. **Conceptual Definition**: Total box office refers to the total ticket revenue of a film during its entire cinema release cycle, which directly reflects the market acceptance of the film.
2. **Mathematical Formula**:
   \$
\text{Total Box Office} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} (T_i \times P_i)
\$
3. **Symbol Explanation**: $n$ is the total number of days of the film's cinema release; $T_i$ is the total number of tickets sold for the film on the $i$-th day of release; $P_i$ is the average price of a single ticket for the film on the $i$-th day of release, which varies with screening format (2D, 3D, IMAX) and regional price differences.
### 5.2.2. Cultural and Artistic Effectiveness Metric: Average Audience Reputation Score
1. **Conceptual Definition**: The average audience reputation score is a composite score given by audiences who have watched the film on public film review platforms, which reflects the audience's recognition of the film's artistic quality and cultural value. This paper uses the score from Douban (the largest Chinese film, book and music review platform) as the representative metric.
2. **Mathematical Formula**:
   \$
\text{Average Reputation Score} = \frac{\sum_{j=1}^{m} S_j}{m}
\$
3. **Symbol Explanation**: $m$ is the total number of audiences who have rated the film; $S_j$ is the score given by the $j$-th audience, with a value range of 1 to 5 stars (converted to a 2-10 point final score on the Douban platform).
## 5.3. Baselines
The paper uses two groups of baselines for comparative analysis:
1. **Chinese School works (1950s-1980s)**: These works represent the highest level of traditional cultural element application in early domestic animation. Comparing with them highlights the innovative changes of current works in the integration of traditional culture and modern demands.
2. **Domestic animated works (1990s-2014)**: These works represent the previous unsuccessful attempts of domestic animation in integrating traditional culture. Comparing with them verifies the effectiveness of the paths summarized in the paper.
   These baselines are representative because they cover the entire historical evolution of domestic animated film's application of traditional cultural elements, and can clearly highlight the progress and innovation of current practices.

# 6. Results & Analysis
## 6.1. Core Results Analysis
The paper's core results are verified to be effective through cross-case analysis:
### Effectiveness of the Four Practical Paths
1. **Drawing material from traditional literature**: All four works are adapted from widely known traditional literary classics, which not only gives the works a solid artistic foundation, but also reduces the audience's acceptance threshold. For example, *Ne Zha: Birth of the Demon Child* adapted from traditional Ne Zha mythology achieved a box office of over 5 billion RMB, with a Douban score of 8.4 points.
2. **Adapting traditional music**: Integrating traditional musical instruments, classic traditional music works, and traditional vocal arts into soundtracks not only enhances the national style and artistic quality of the works, but also promotes the dissemination of traditional music. For example, the adaptation of the traditional classic *Overture of the Small Swords Society* in *Monkey King: Hero is Back* is widely praised by audiences, and the Peking Opera singing segment has triggered widespread discussion of traditional opera among young audiences.
3. **Utilizing traditional symbols**: Applying traditional visual symbols in costume, character, scene, and color design not only directly shapes the distinct national style of the works, but also promotes the dissemination of traditional folk art, architecture, and customs. For example, the Fujian Tulou (earthen building) scene in *Big Fish & Begonia* has triggered a tourism boom of Tulou among young audiences, and the shadow puppet scene in *Monkey King: Hero is Back* has increased young people's awareness of the traditional shadow puppet art.

### Effectiveness of the Two Innovative Logics
1. **Reconstructing tradition: retain its spirit while changing its form**: Innovating narrative mode, character shaping, and content extension while retaining the core spiritual essence of traditional culture avoids rigid copying of traditional content, making the works refreshing while retaining the charm of traditional culture. For example, *Ne Zha* subverts the traditional Ne Zha image into a mischievous child with dark circles, but retains his core spirit of resisting fate, which is widely loved by audiences.
2. **Expressing the spirit of the times: closely connecting to real life**: Integrating modern technology elements and youth-oriented image language eliminates the cognitive distance between traditional culture and modern audiences. For example, the fingerprint unlock and game-style fighting scenes in *Ne Zha*, and the electronic music elements mixed with traditional music in *White Snake: Origin*, are both highly recognized by young audiences.

## 6.3. Validation of Path Effectiveness
The paper verifies the effectiveness of the proposed paths through cross-group comparison:
- All four sample works apply the four paths to varying degrees, and all have achieved high commercial success and cultural recognition. In contrast, most domestic animated works before 2015 that only adopted superficial application of traditional elements (without systematic application of the four paths, especially without reshaping the spiritual core and innovating forms) failed to achieve market success.
- For example, some animated works before 2015 only copied the plot of traditional classics without any adaptation, which was considered too old-fashioned by modern audiences, and failed to achieve good box office results. This verifies that the innovative logic of "retaining the spirit while changing the form" is necessary for the successful integration of traditional culture and modern animation.

# 7. Conclusion & Reflections
## 7.1. Conclusion Summary
This paper systematically summarizes four generalizable practical paths for post-2015 domestic Chinese animated films to apply traditional cultural elements: drawing material from traditional literature, adapting traditional music, utilizing traditional visual symbols, and reshaping traditional cultural spirit. It also extracts two core innovative logics that conform to the principle of "upholding fundamental principles while breaking new ground": reconstructing tradition by retaining its spiritual core while innovating external forms, and expressing contemporary values by integrating modern technology and audience needs.
The effective integration of traditional culture and animated films has dual value: on the one hand, it injects unique national characteristics and Chinese artistic spirit into domestic animation, promotes the high-quality development of the animation industry, and supports the generation of new cultural industry formats; on the other hand, it activates the modern vitality of traditional culture, promotes the dissemination of excellent traditional culture, and enhances national cultural confidence.
## 7.2. Limitations & Future Work
### Limitations
1. The research sample only includes successful hit works, and does not include works that failed in market or reputation, so it lacks comparative analysis of unsuccessful cases, and cannot fully summarize the common pitfalls and risk points in the integration of traditional culture and animation.
2. The paper mainly focuses on the domestic market performance of the works, and lacks analysis of the effect of these works in overseas markets, so it cannot fully evaluate the role of these paths in the international communication of Chinese culture.
### Suggested Future Work
1. Further explore the deeper application of traditional cultural elements in animated films, tap more underutilized traditional cultural resources such as lost traditional skills, folk customs of ethnic minorities, and regional traditional culture.
2. Explore the adaptation strategies of traditional cultural elements suitable for overseas audiences, to promote domestic animated films with traditional cultural characteristics to enter the global market, and enhance China's international cultural soft power.
## 7.3. Personal Insights & Critique
### Inspirations
The practical framework and innovative logic proposed in the paper are not only applicable to the animation industry, but also can be extended to other cultural and creative industries such as games, short videos, and cultural and creative product design, providing clear guidance for the integration of traditional cultural elements and modern cultural and creative products.
### Potential Issues and Improvement Directions
1. The paper does not address the risk of "excessive adaptation" in the process of creative transformation: if the adaptation of traditional content is excessive, it may distort the core essence of traditional culture, leading to audience misunderstanding of traditional culture. Subsequent research can incorporate failed cases for comparative analysis, and clarify the reasonable boundary of creative transformation.
2. The paper does not propose a clear, operable evaluation standard for the balance between "retaining the spirit" and "changing the form", which may lead to arbitrary judgment on whether the adaptation is appropriate in practice. Subsequent research can construct a quantitative evaluation system for the integration effect of traditional cultural elements and animation, to further improve the practical guidance value of the research.
3. The paper does not discuss the cost problem of applying traditional cultural elements: the in-depth excavation and professional adaptation of traditional cultural elements often require higher research and development costs, which may be unaffordable for small and medium-sized animation production teams. Subsequent research can explore low-cost application paths for small and medium-sized teams, to expand the coverage of the proposed practical framework.
