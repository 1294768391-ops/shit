The title of the paper is *On the Poetic Temperament of Hou Hsiao-hsien's Films* (original Chinese: 论侯孝贤电影的诗性气质). Its central topic is the systematic analysis of the origin, formal expression, and spiritual connotation of the poetic aesthetic characteristics in Taiwanese director Hou Hsiao-hsien's film works, from the perspective of traditional Chinese poetics.

## 1.5. Abstract
### Research Objective
To address the research gap that prior studies on Hou Hsiao-hsien's films mostly focus on technical features (such as fixed-position long takes) and ignore the deep connection between his works and traditional Chinese poetics, this paper systematically explores the reasons for the strong poetic temperament in Hou's films, from the dimensions of artistic techniques, aesthetic pursuits and spiritual orientation.
### Core Methodology
The paper adopts an interdisciplinary research framework combining traditional Chinese poetics and film studies, and analyzes Hou's works from three interconnected layers: poetic narration of cultural motifs, construction of poetic image style, and poetic expression of humanistic spirit, combined with text analysis of representative films in different creation stages of Hou.
### Main Results & Conclusions
The paper finds that Hou Hsiao-hsien's films form a unique "screen poem" style: they use techniques such as fixed long takes, natural flowing narrative structure, and calm character shaping to complete the poetic narration of two core cultural motifs (contrast between rural and urban civilization, entanglement of historical changes and individual life experience), and integrate traditional Chinese literati humanistic care and poetic free spirit. The poetic temperament is the core spiritual feature that makes Hou's films unique in Chinese and world cinema.

# 2. Executive Summary
## 2.1. Background & Motivation
### Core Problem to Solve
As a representative figure of Taiwan New Cinema, Hou Hsiao-hsien's films have unique aesthetic characteristics, but prior research lacks a systematic interpretation of his overall poetic style from the perspective of local Chinese cultural tradition. Most studies either only analyze his technical techniques (such as long takes) in isolation, or only focus on the historical and cultural themes of his works, without connecting the formal features and spiritual connotations of his films with the long-standing Chinese poetic tradition.
### Importance of the Problem
Poetic cinema is an important branch of world cinema, and Chinese poetic cinema has a unique tradition rooted in thousands of years of Chinese poetry aesthetics. Explaining the poetic characteristics of Hou's films, the most representative Chinese poetic film director, helps to clarify the inheritance and innovation of traditional Chinese aesthetics in modern film art, and also provides a reference for the creation of Chinese-style art films.
### Research Entry Point & Innovation
The paper takes the inherent consistency between Hou Hsiao-hsien's creation concept and traditional Chinese poetics as the entry point, and constructs a three-dimensional analytical framework covering theme, form and spirit, which is different from the single-dimensional research framework of prior studies.
## 2.2. Main Contributions & Findings
### Primary Contributions
1. It fills the research gap of the connection between Hou Hsiao-hsien's films and traditional Chinese poetics, and systematically sorts out the source of his poetic temperament for the first time.
2. It expands the application scope of traditional Chinese poetics theory, proves that the core concepts of Chinese poetics (such as `jingjie` (realm), `qiyun` (rhythm), `fenggu` (strength of character)) are also applicable to film art criticism.
3. It summarizes the complete system of Hou's poetic film creation, covering motif selection, formal techniques and spiritual connotation.
### Key Conclusions
1. The poetic temperament of Hou's films is the result of the joint action of three factors: the influence of traditional Chinese poetic culture, his personal life experience (growing up in rural Taiwan, family migration history), and his persistent artistic pursuit of avoiding commercial film routines.
2. The poetic characteristics run through all stages of Hou's creation: not only his early rural-themed works and mid-term historical-themed works, but also his later formal exploration works set in modern cities retain the core poetic temperament.
3. Hou's works inherit and develop the tradition of Chinese poetic cinema, reaching a new height in the integration of traditional Eastern aesthetics and modern film art.

# 3. Prerequisite Knowledge & Related Work
## 3.1. Foundational Concepts
To understand this paper, beginners need to master the following core concepts:
1. **Poetic Cinema**: A type of art film that incorporates creation techniques from poetry, such as metaphor, symbolism, and imagery combination, focuses on lyrical expression and aesthetic conception construction rather than plot-driven narrative, and conveys implicit philosophical connotations and emotional experiences to the audience.
2. **Core Concepts of Traditional Chinese Poetics**:
   - `Jingjie` (Realm): The core evaluation standard of Chinese poetry, referring to the state of perfect integration of the author's subjective emotion and the objective scene depicted in the work, which can resonate with the audience's aesthetic experience.
   - `Qiyun` (Rhythm): The implicit aesthetic charm beyond the literal content or visual image of the work, which is "beyond words" and can only be perceived through experience and comprehension.
   - `Fenggu` (Strength of Character): The inner spiritual connotation of the work, which has a touching emotional power and reflects the author's personality and spiritual pursuit.
3. **Fixed-position Long Take**: A film shooting technique, refers to a continuous shot lasting for several minutes or even longer, where the camera position remains fixed, no panning, tilting or zooming is performed. It maintains the integrity of time and space of the shot content, creates a distant, objective viewing perspective, and is the most representative technical feature of Hou's films.
4. **Taiwan New Cinema**: A film movement that emerged in Taiwan in the early 1980s, represented by directors such as Hou Hsiao-hsien, Edward Yang, and Wan Ren. It focuses on reflecting the real life and historical memory of Taiwanese people, uses realistic shooting techniques, and has a strong humanistic care spirit.
## 3.2. Previous Works
### Global Poetic Cinema Development
- Early 20th century French avant-garde film movement: The first group of directors to explicitly propose the concept of "poetic film", they advocated breaking the narrative routine of commercial films, and used experimental techniques to create dreamlike poetic artistic conception, but fell into the misunderstanding of formalism that separated from real life.
- Soviet poetic film school: Represented by director Eisenstein, he proposed that film can learn the lyrical and epic expression techniques from poetry, and promoted the development of poetic film narrative theory.
### Chinese Poetic Cinema Tradition
- 1930s-1940s pioneering period: Represented by director Fei Mu's *Spring in a Small Town* (1948), which borrowed the expression techniques of traditional Chinese poetry, created a hazy, implicit poetic artistic conception, and is recognized as the peak of early Chinese poetic cinema.
- 1980s development period: Represented by mainland director Wu Yigong's *My Memories of Old Beijing* (1982), which takes "light sadness and deep longing" as the emotional clue, and has a strong poetic charm. At the same time, Taiwan New Cinema directors represented by Hou Hsiao-hsien further promoted the development of Chinese poetic cinema.
### Prior Research on Hou Hsiao-hsien
Prior studies on Hou Hsiao-hsien can be divided into two categories:
1. Formalist research: Focuses on analyzing his unique fixed long take technique, editing style, and other formal features, which is relatively mature, but rarely connects technical features with aesthetic and spiritual connotations.
2. Cultural research: Focuses on analyzing the themes of Taiwan identity, historical memory, and local complex in his works, but ignores the connection between these themes and traditional Chinese poetic aesthetics.
## 3.3. Technological Evolution
The development of poetic cinema has gone through three stages:
1. Early formal experiment stage (1920s-1940s): Focused on exploring the unique expressive techniques of film as an independent art form, separating from drama and literature.
2. Cultural integration stage (1950s-1970s): Poetic cinema began to integrate with local cultural traditions in different regions, forming national characteristics.
3. Diversified development stage (1980s-present): Poetic cinema combines with realistic creation concepts, focuses on reflecting real life and humanistic care, and Hou Hsiao-hsien's works are the representative of this stage in the Chinese-speaking region.
   This paper's research is located at the intersection of traditional Chinese poetics research and film art research, and is the first systematic study to apply the complete traditional Chinese poetics evaluation system to Hou Hsiao-hsien's film analysis.
## 3.4. Differentiation Analysis
Compared with prior related studies, the core innovations of this paper are:
1. **Framework innovation**: It constructs a three-dimensional analytical framework covering theme, form and spirit, rather than the single-dimensional framework of prior studies.
2. **Perspective innovation**: It takes local traditional Chinese poetics as the core analytical perspective, rather than the Western film theory perspective adopted by most prior studies, which can more accurately explain the unique Eastern aesthetic characteristics of Hou's films.
3. **Content completeness**: It covers all stages of Hou's creation from 1980 to 2007, rather than only analyzing individual representative works, so the conclusion is more universal.

# 4. Methodology
This paper adopts three complementary research methods, which are standard research paradigms in the field of humanities and art studies:
## 4.1. Principles
The core principle of this paper's research is to take the inherent logical consistency between Hou Hsiao-hsien's film creation and traditional Chinese poetics as the core clue, and deconstruct the poetic temperament of his films from three mutually supportive layers (content layer, form layer, spiritual layer) to ensure the comprehensiveness and systematicness of the analysis.
## 4.2. Core Methodology In-depth
### Step 1: Literature Research Method
The author first sorted out two categories of literature:
1. Theoretical literature: Including traditional Chinese poetics works (such as Wang Guowei's *Poetic Remarks in the Human World*, Zhu Guangqian's *On Poetry*), film theory works related to poetic cinema, and prior research papers on Hou Hsiao-hsien's films, to lay a theoretical foundation and clarify the research gap.
2. Background materials: Including Hou Hsiao-hsien's personal interviews, memoirs of his collaborators (such as screenwriter Chu Tien-wen, editor Liao Ching-sung), and historical materials related to the background of his film creation, to accurately understand his creation concept and motivation.
### Step 2: Film Text Analysis Method
The author conducted a detailed text analysis of 18 representative works of Hou Hsiao-hsien from 1980 to 2007, focusing on the three layers of analysis:
1. **Content layer: Poetic narration of cultural motifs**
   - First, identify the two core cultural motifs that run through Hou's creation: the contrast between rural and urban civilization, and the entanglement of historical changes and individual life experience.
   - Then, analyze the poetic expression techniques of these two motifs: for the rural motif, he adopts the expression method similar to traditional Chinese pastoral poetry, focusing on depicting idyllic rural scenery and simple human relations, and expressing the nostalgia for traditional rural civilization; for the historical motif, he adopts the expression method similar to traditional Chinese historical poetry, avoiding direct depiction of grand historical events, and reflecting the changes of the times through the fate of ordinary individuals and families, with implicit and profound epic charm.
2. **Form layer: Construction of poetic image style**
   - Analyze the three core formal techniques that form the poetic style:
     1. Fixed-position long take and deep focus long shot: These techniques maintain the integrity of time and space of the shot, create a distant, objective viewing perspective, and integrate the characters' emotions with the natural or social environment, achieving the "fusion of emotion and scene" required by traditional Chinese poetics for poetic realm. Empty shots (shots without characters, only natural scenery or environment) are widely used to express the implicit emotions that cannot be directly expressed by characters' lines or actions, similar to the "lyrics through scenery" technique in Chinese poetry.
     2. Natural flowing fragment structure: Hou abandons the linear narrative structure of "beginning-development-climax-ending" in traditional commercial films, and only selects fragmented fragments of real life to form the film content. The connection between fragments does not follow the causal logic of the plot, but follows the implicit emotional tension, which is similar to the combination of fragmented imagery in Chinese poetry. The matching "qiyun editing method" (editing according to the implicit rhythm and emotional charm of the film, rather than the plot logic) further enhances the poetic charm of the structure.
     3. Calm and self-sufficient character shaping: Hou prefers to use non-professional actors or new actors with little performance experience, advocates natural and non-performance-style performance, and the characters in his films have the introverted, resilient, calm temperament consistent with the aesthetic ideal of traditional Chinese literati, which forms the "fenggu" (strength of character) of the films.
3. **Spiritual layer: Poetic expression of humanistic spirit**
   - Analyze the two core spiritual connotations of Hou's poetic temperament:
     1. Poetic humanistic care: Influenced by writer Shen Congwen's objective and tolerant narrative perspective, Hou treats the joys and sorrows of ordinary people in the changes of times with a compassionate but non-sensational attitude, which integrates the Confucian tradition of compassion for the people and the Taoist tradition of "harmony between man and nature", expressing a deep contemplation of life and time.
     2. Poetic free spirit (chivalrous complex): Influenced by traditional Chinese chivalrous culture and his personal teenage experience of living in the streets, Hou's films generally have a chivalrous spirit of emphasizing loyalty, pursuing freedom, and being brave to take responsibility, which is shown in the rebellious youth in his early works, the progressive intellectuals who dare to pursue ideals in his historical works, and the gangster characters who value loyalty in his later urban works.
### Step 3: Interdisciplinary Comparative Research Method
The author compares the poetic characteristics of Hou's films with the core aesthetic standards of traditional Chinese poetics, and compares Hou's creation with the tradition of Chinese poetic cinema, to prove that Hou's films are the inheritance and innovation of traditional Chinese poetic culture in the field of film art.

# 6. Results & Analysis
## 6.1. Core Results Analysis
The paper draws three core conclusions through systematic analysis:
1. **Poetic motif narration forms the content foundation of Hou's poetic temperament**
   The two core motifs of local complex and historical narrative perfectly match the two main types of traditional Chinese poetry (pastoral poetry and historical poetry). His early local-themed works express the nostalgia for the lost traditional rural civilization under the impact of urbanization, with a fresh and quiet pastoral poetic style; his mid-term historical-themed works reflect the heavy historical memory of Taiwan in the past 100 years through the fate of ordinary people, with a deep and grand epic charm. The two types of motifs complement each other, covering the expression of personal memory and collective historical experience.
2. **Poetic image style forms the formal carrier of Hou's poetic temperament**
   The combination of fixed long take, fragment structure and natural character shaping successfully constructs the poetic realm that integrates emotion and scene, fully meeting the aesthetic requirements of traditional Chinese poetics. For example, in *Dust in the Wind*, three consecutive empty shots of mountain scenery after the male protagonist learns that his lover has married someone else perfectly express the male protagonist's sadness and subsequent relief, without any lines, achieving the aesthetic effect of "expressing all meanings without a word" as in traditional Chinese poetry. Another example is *The Puppetmaster*, which uses only 100 long takes to tell the life experience of an old artist in more than 50 years, with a calm and distant rhythm, just like a slowly unfolding traditional Chinese landscape painting.
3. **Poetic humanistic spirit forms the core connotation of Hou's poetic temperament**
   The integration of Confucian compassionate humanistic care and Taoist free chivalrous spirit gives Hou's films a touching inner power (fenggu). On the one hand, he treats the sufferings of ordinary people in the changes of times with an objective and tolerant attitude, without deliberate sensationalism or criticism, showing a broad mind that embraces all life experiences; on the other hand, his admiration for chivalrous spirit gives his films a strong sense of justice and the pursuit of freedom, avoiding the weakness of being overly indifferent and negative.
Compared with prior studies that only focus on technical features or thematic connotations, this paper's three-dimensional analysis more comprehensively reveals the unique artistic value of Hou's films, and accurately explains why his works are called "screen poems".
## 6.2. Cross-stage Consistency Analysis
The paper also finds that the poetic temperament runs through all stages of Hou's creation, even in his later works set in modern cities or with more formal experiments:
- *Flowers of Shanghai* (1998), which is set in a late Qing Dynasty brothel, uses fixed long takes in a completely closed indoor space, and the slow and gentle camera movement still creates a hazy and implicit poetic realm, reflecting the sadness of the characters' impermanent fate.
- *Millennium Mambo* (2001), which tells the story of young people in modern Taipei, still uses the fragment structure and emotional editing method, and the free and confused spiritual state of the protagonist is also a continuation of the chivalrous complex in his early works.

# 7. Conclusion & Reflections
## 7.1. Conclusion Summary
This paper systematically analyzes the poetic temperament of Hou Hsiao-hsien's films from the perspective of traditional Chinese poetics, and draws the following conclusions:
1. The poetic temperament of Hou's films is the result of the integration of traditional Chinese poetic culture, his personal life experience and his persistent artistic pursuit.
2. The poetic temperament is reflected in three interconnected layers: the poetic narration of local and historical cultural motifs in the content layer, the poetic realm constructed by long takes and fragment structure in the form layer, and the poetic humanistic spirit integrating compassion and freedom in the spiritual layer.
3. Hou Hsiao-hsien inherits and develops the tradition of Chinese poetic cinema, and his works are an important representative of the integration of traditional Eastern aesthetics and modern film art, with important artistic value and cultural significance.
## 7.2. Limitations & Future Work
### Limitations of the Paper
1. The paper focuses too much on the influence of traditional Chinese poetics on Hou's films, and the analysis of the influence of foreign film theories and directors (such as Bazin's long take theory, Japanese director Yasujiro Ozu's static aesthetic style) on his creation is insufficient. In fact, Hou's poetic style is the result of the fusion of Chinese and Western aesthetic ideas.
2. The paper lacks comparative analysis with other contemporary Chinese poetic film directors (such as Wong Kar-wai, Ang Lee), which makes it impossible to fully highlight the uniqueness of Hou's poetic temperament.
3. The paper ignores the problem of polarized audience evaluation of Hou's films: his works are highly praised by professional critics and art film lovers, but are considered boring and difficult to understand by ordinary audiences. The paper does not analyze the reasons for this polarization, nor does it discuss the relationship between the poetic style of art films and market acceptance.
### Future Research Directions Suggested
1. Explore the fusion mechanism of Chinese traditional poetics and foreign film aesthetics in Hou's creation, to more comprehensively explain the source of his artistic style.
2. Conduct comparative studies between Hou Hsiao-hsien and other Chinese poetic film directors, to sort out the different development paths of Chinese poetic cinema.
3. Conduct empirical research on the audience acceptance of Hou's films, explore the communication strategies of poetic art films in the commercial film market, and provide reference for the creation and dissemination of art films.
## 7.3. Personal Insights & Critique
### Inspirations
This paper provides a very valuable analytical paradigm for the study of Chinese film art: applying the traditional local literary and artistic criticism system to modern film art research, rather than completely adopting Western film theory, which can more accurately grasp the unique aesthetic characteristics of Chinese films. This analytical framework can also be transferred to the study of other Chinese directors' works, such as Wong Kar-wai's films, which also have strong poetic characteristics, and can be analyzed using the same framework of "motif-form-spirit".
### Potential Improvements
The paper can add quantitative analysis to make the research more rigorous: for example, count the average length of long takes, the proportion of empty shots, and other indicators in different works of Hou, to quantify the evolution of his formal style. In addition, the paper can add more analysis of the limitations of Hou's creation: his overly personalized expression style does reduce the audience's acceptability, which also limits the spread of his artistic concept to a certain extent.
### Value of the Paper
Despite its limitations, this paper is still a very important research result in the field of Hou Hsiao-hsien's film research. It fills the long-standing research gap of the connection between Hou's films and traditional Chinese poetics, and helps to deepen the understanding of the cultural connotation of Chinese poetic cinema, and also provides important inspiration for the creation of Chinese-style art films with national cultural characteristics.
