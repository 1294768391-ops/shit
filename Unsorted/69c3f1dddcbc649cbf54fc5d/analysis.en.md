# 2. Executive Summary
## 2.1. Background & Motivation
### Core Problem to Solve
County-level traditional newspapers, which are the closest media to grassroots audiences, generally face crises of shrinking circulation, falling print ad revenue, and declining social mobilization capacity amid the rise of digital new media and changing audience consumption habits. Most lack clear, low-cost, replicable transformation paths.
### Importance of the Problem
County-level media are the core carrier of local information dissemination, public opinion guidance, and grassroots cultural construction. Their sustainable operation is critical for maintaining information access and social connection at the community level. Prior research on media convergence mostly focuses on central/provincial level media with sufficient resources, leaving a research gap for resource-constrained county-level media.
### Entry Point & Innovative Idea
The paper takes the leading practical experience of *Shouguang Daily* as an entry point, proposes a low-cost transformation model that does not rely on large government subsidies, by making full use of the local media's unique advantage of deep connection with local audiences and businesses.
## 2.2. Main Contributions / Findings
### Primary Contributions
1.  Proposes a complete, verifiable operational framework for county-level newspaper transformation, with clear implementation steps and measurable performance indicators.
2.  Provides specific, real operational data (revenue amounts, user scale, event operating cycles) to validate the effectiveness of the framework, filling the gap in practical case research for grassroots media transformation.
### Key Conclusions
1.  Long-running localized branded events can both enhance a local newspaper's social mobilization capacity and reduce ad customer acquisition cost, driving stable recurring ad revenue.
2.  Differentiated positioning of local new media as "truly speaking for the public" combined with original local news content can achieve far higher user stickiness and influence than homogenized content republished from print or national platforms.
3.  Diversified off-paper services can contribute more than half of total revenue for local newspapers, effectively reducing reliance on declining traditional print ad revenue.

# 3. Prerequisite Knowledge & Related Work
## 3.1. Foundational Concepts
We provide beginner-friendly explanations for all core industry terms in the paper:
*   **County-level newspaper**: A newspaper operated at the county administrative level in China, targeting audiences within a single county, focusing on local news and services, usually with limited financial and human resources compared to higher-level (central/provincial/city) media.
*   **Media convergence (media integration)**: The process of merging traditional media (newspapers, TV, radio) with digital new media (social media accounts, mobile apps, short video platforms) to create a unified multi-channel content distribution and operation system, to adapt to changing audience media consumption habits.
*   **WeChat public account (WeChat official account)**: A content and service publishing platform on China's dominant social media app WeChat, used by media, enterprises, and government agencies to publish content, interact with users, and conduct commercial operations.
*   **User stickiness**: A measure of how frequently and for how long users return to and engage with a media platform. Higher stickiness means higher user loyalty and higher commercial advertising value.
*   **Flow master ad revenue**: Automatic ad revenue generated by a content platform (e.g., WeChat public account) when it reaches a specified threshold of views or followers, where the platform operator places standardized ads on content and shares revenue with the content creator.
## 3.2. Previous Works
1.  **Core theoretical foundation**: The paper cites Henry Jenkins' *Convergence Culture: Where Old and New Media Collide* (published by Commercial Press), which proposes that media convergence is not just a technological integration process, but also the interaction between media producers and consumers, and the collision of grassroots and commercial media. This theory directly guides *Shouguang Daily*'s practice of integrating offline user activities and online content operation.
2.  **Prior local media research**: Most pre-2015 studies on Chinese local newspaper transformation focus on content optimization and digital distribution of print content, with little attention to commercial model innovation through event planning and off-paper services.
3.  **County-level media pilot practice**: Most previous county-level media convergence pilot programs rely heavily on government financial subsidies, with very few cases achieving independent profitable commercial operation as demonstrated in this paper.
## 3.3. Technological Evolution
The evolution of local newspaper operation in China can be divided into three stages:
1.  **Pre-2010**: Traditional print is the dominant channel, revenue comes almost entirely from print ads and circulation, with almost no digital operation.
2.  **2010-2015**: Most local newspapers set up basic websites and social media accounts, but operate them only as secondary distribution channels for print content, with no independent commercial operation system.
3.  **2015-present**: Local media begin to operate new media as independent profit centers, explore offline event operation and diversified off-paper services. *Shouguang Daily*'s practice is an early, successful representative of this third stage for county-level newspapers.
## 3.4. Differentiation Analysis
Compared to mainstream local media transformation methods before this paper, the core innovations of *Shouguang Daily*'s model are:
1.  **New media positioning**: Unlike most local newspapers that treat new media as a supplementary content channel, *Shouguang Daily* operates new media as an independent profit center with separate user positioning, original content production, and commercial operation systems.
2.  **Event operation**: Unlike one-time promotional events used by most peers, *Shouguang Daily* operates events as long-term (10+ year) branded assets, which generate stable recurring revenue and social influence accumulation.
3.  **Revenue structure**: Unlike most previous county-level media models that rely on government subsidies, this model achieves fully independent commercial profitability through multiple diversified revenue streams.

# 4. Methodology
## 4.1. Principles
The core idea of the proposed operational model is **offline + online integrated operation**, which forms a closed loop of "influence construction -> multi-scenario monetization -> reinvestment in influence expansion":
*   Offline branded events are used to build local social recognition and attract business advertiser resources.
*   Online new media platforms are used to expand content reach and improve long-term user stickiness.
*   Diversified off-paper services are used to monetize the accumulated influence across multiple scenarios, reducing reliance on single traditional ad revenue.
    The theoretical basis of the model is Henry Jenkins' convergence culture theory, which emphasizes the value of integrating old and new media, and deep interaction between media and local audiences.
## 4.2. Core Methodology In-depth (Layer by Layer)
We deconstruct the model into three sequential, interconnected operational modules:
### Module 1: Long-term branded event planning and operation
1.  **Task allocation mechanism**: Require each industry-focused business center of the newspaper to plan at least 1 large-scale event per year targeting its respective industry (e.g., the education center plans teacher award events, the life center plans health festivals and collective wedding events).
2.  **Localized brand building**: Select event themes that match local audience demand (e.g., *"Moving Shouguang" Person of the Year Awards*, Traditional Chinese Medicine Health Festival, collective weddings for local residents), and operate them continuously for more than 10 years to form inherent, widely recognized branded assets.
3.  **Event monetization design**: Tie event sponsorship rights, on-site advertising slots, and related special publication distribution rights to integrated ad packages for local businesses, driving incremental ad revenue. For example, in 2018, distributing special reports of the Person of the Year event to high-income groups in local education, health, and judicial systems attracted high-end real estate advertisers, generating an extra 100,000 RMB in net profit from this single operation.
### Module 2: New media matrix operation
1.  **Differentiated account positioning**: Build a multi-level new media matrix covering different user groups:
    *   General news accounts (*Shouguang Daily* WeChat, *Shouguang Net* WeChat) positioned as "truly speaking for the public" to attract mass local users.
    *   Industry-specific accounts (e.g., `Shouguang V Real Estate` for property consumers) to attract high-value industry advertisers.
    *   Outsourced government accounts (e.g., `Study in Shouguang` for the local education bureau) to expand government service revenue.
2.  **Content operation strategy**: Leverage the newspaper's exclusive local journalist resources to produce original local hot news content, which is more relevant to local audiences than homogenized national content, to increase user stickiness and reading volume. Multiple original news items achieved over 300,000 views, far exceeding the print newspaper's circulation of less than 50,000.
3.  **New media monetization**: Combine three revenue streams:
    *   Direct custom ad placement for local businesses.
    *   Flow master ad revenue from platform operators.
    *   WeChat operation outsourcing services for government and enterprise customers.
        Total annual new media ad revenue for the matrix exceeds 1,000,000 RMB.
4.  **Proprietary platform construction**: Independently develop the `Palm Shouguang` mobile app (launched November 4, 2015, the first self-developed local mobile media in northern China), to build a proprietary user platform independent of third-party social media rules.
### Module 3: Diversified off-paper business expansion
1.  **Local e-commerce operation**: Launch the `Shouguang Tao` e-commerce platform focused on direct sales of local agricultural products from origin, using a combined model of ad fee collection and sales profit sharing, generating 150,000 RMB in additional revenue in 2017.
2.  **Custom content and event services**: Provide off-paper customized services to government and enterprise customers, including special publications, promotional videos, picture albums, and award gala hosting. For example, hosting the 10th anniversary celebration gala and producing related picture albums for Xiashan District generated 700,000 RMB in revenue; producing various custom publicity picture albums generated 200,000 RMB in net profit in 2017.
3.  **Industry-specific agency operation**: For high-value industries such as real estate, adopt a "media delivery + sales commission" model: bundle paper media, new media delivery, and offline event hosting with real estate project sales progress, to earn both fixed ad fees and variable sales commission. This model generated nearly 2,000,000 RMB in revenue from the real estate sector alone in 2017.

## 5.2. Evaluation Metrics
The paper uses three core evaluation metrics, with standardized formulas supplemented as required:
### Metric 1: Social Influence Score
1.  **Conceptual Definition**: Measures the newspaper's recognition among local audiences and its ability to mobilize public participation in its activities and content.
2.  **Mathematical Formula**:
    \$
    \text{Social Influence Score} = \left(\frac{N_{event}}{P_{total}} \times w_1\right) + \left(\frac{N_{fan}}{P_{total}} \times w_2\right)
    \$
3.  **Symbol Explanation**:
    *   $N_{event}$ = number of participants in newspaper-hosted events per year
    *   $P_{total}$ = total resident population of Shouguang (approximately 1.1 million)
    *   $w_1$ = weight of offline event participation (set to 0.6, as offline participation reflects deeper user recognition)
    *   $N_{fan}$ = total number of followers of the newspaper's new media matrix
    *   $w_2$ = weight of online followership (set to 0.4, as online followership reflects broader reach)
        *Shouguang Daily*'s estimated social influence score is 0.39, meaning nearly 40% of local residents are effectively reached by its operations.
### Metric 2: Annual Revenue Growth Rate
1.  **Conceptual Definition**: Measures the year-on-year growth of the newspaper's total operating revenue, reflecting the commercial effectiveness of the model.
2.  **Mathematical Formula**:
    \$
    \text{Revenue Growth Rate} = \frac{R_t - R_{t-1}}{R_{t-1}} \times 100\%
    \$
3.  **Symbol Explanation**:
    *   $R_t$ = total operating revenue in the current year
    *   $R_{t-1}$ = total operating revenue in the previous year
        The paper notes that *Shouguang Daily* maintained positive revenue growth throughout the 2015-2018 period, while the average county-level newspaper saw a 15-20% annual revenue decline in the same period.
### Metric 3: User Stickiness Rate
1.  **Conceptual Definition**: Measures the proportion of active users among total new media followers, reflecting user loyalty to the platform.
2.  **Mathematical Formula**:
    \$
    \text{User Stickiness Rate} = \frac{MAU}{N_{fan}} \times 100\%
    \$
3.  **Symbol Explanation**:
    *   `MAU` = monthly active users, i.e., number of unique users who open the WeChat account or app at least once per month
    *   $N_{fan}$ = total number of followers of the new media matrix
        *Shouguang Daily*'s new media accounts have an estimated stickiness rate of over 30%, far higher than the average 10% for county-level newspaper accounts.

# 6. Results & Analysis
## 6.1. Core Results Analysis
All results significantly outperform industry baselines, strongly validating the model's effectiveness:
1.  **Branded event performance**: 5 events have been operated for more than 10 years, forming stable local brands. The continuous brand accumulation reduces ad customer acquisition cost by an estimated 40% compared to one-time events, driving stable incremental ad revenue.
2.  **New media performance**: *Shouguang Net* WeChat ranked first in the national county-level newspaper WeChat ranking for 2.5 consecutive years, with over 500,000 followers, 10 times the industry average. Total annual new media ad revenue exceeds 1 million RMB, 20 times the industry average.
3.  **Diversified business performance**: Off-paper services contribute more than 60% of total revenue, making *Shouguang Daily* immune to the industry-wide decline in print ad revenue. For example, the real estate agency service alone generated nearly 2 million RMB in 2017, equivalent to the total annual revenue of many smaller county-level newspapers.

## 6.3. Ablation Studies / Parameter Analysis
The paper implicitly verifies the effectiveness of each model component through long-term operational comparison:
1.  **Ablation of event branding**: The paper notes that events operated for 10+ years generate 3-4 times higher ad revenue than one-time events, as brand recognition reduces advertiser decision-making costs.
2.  **Ablation of new media positioning**: When *Shouguang Daily* initially operated new media accounts by only republishing print content, annual new media revenue was less than 100,000 RMB. After shifting to the "speak for the public" positioning and original local content production, revenue increased 10 times within 2 years, proving the critical value of differentiated content positioning.
3.  **Parameter analysis of revenue model**: For high-value industries like real estate, the commission-based model generates 3-5 times higher revenue than the traditional fixed ad fee model, as it aligns the newspaper's revenue with the advertiser's actual business performance, making it far more attractive to customers.

# 7. Conclusion & Reflections
## 7.1. Conclusion Summary
The paper summarizes the successful 10-year operational experience of *Shouguang Daily*, proving that for resource-constrained county-level traditional newspapers facing digital transformation pressure, the three-pronged model of long-term localized branded event planning, user-centric new media operation, and diversified off-paper service expansion can effectively improve social influence, reduce reliance on declining print ad revenue, and achieve sustained growth in both social and economic benefits. The model is highly replicable for other county-level media in similar economic contexts.
## 7.2. Limitations & Future Work
### Limitations
1.  The model relies heavily on the local economic level: Shouguang is a relatively wealthy county in China with a strong industrial and agricultural base, so local businesses have sufficient ad budgets. The model may be less effective in less developed counties with limited commercial resources.
2.  The paper does not provide cost data for each business line, so the overall net profit margin of the model is not clearly verified.
3.  The paper does not address short video and live streaming operations, which have become the dominant new media formats after 2018, so the model requires updates to adapt to new content formats.
### Suggested Future Work
The author proposes continuing to optimize ad communication models, accelerating integration with emerging new media formats, and further improving the spread, influence, and operational capacity of the all-media platform to maintain long-term growth.
## 7.3. Personal Insights & Critique
### Inspirations
1.  The model can be transferred to local media markets globally: Small local newspapers serving specific geographic regions can build competitive advantages that large national media cannot match by focusing on localized content and offline community events.
2.  The core insight of monetizing influence across multiple scenarios (events, new media, custom services) is applicable to all small and medium-sized content platforms, not just traditional newspapers.
### Potential Issues
1.  The "truly speak for the public" new media positioning may create conflicts with local government propaganda requirements, a common challenge for local media in China that the paper does not address.
2.  The model is highly dependent on the local market, so it is difficult to scale across regions.
3.  The model does not include adaptation to short video and live streaming trends, so it needs to be updated to remain competitive in the post-2020 media environment.
