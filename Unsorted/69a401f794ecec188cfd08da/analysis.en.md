## 1.5. Abstract
This paper analyzes the series of reasoning reality show "Star Detective" using theories from narratology. The study examines the program's narrative elements and strategies across seven seasons. The core findings identify three unique narrative features developed beyond classic reality show tropes:
1.  **Intertextual Narrative:** Both narrow (self-reference, character recurrence) and broad (social issues, cross-media collaboration).
2.  **Suspense Narrative:** Using multi-layered structures, "Rashomon" perspectives, and limited spaces to generate and resolve suspense.
3.  **Gamified Narrative:** Incorporating game elements into plot, role-playing, and scene design to create immersive experiences.
    The paper concludes by offering implications for the future development of similar genres, emphasizing vertical innovation, content depth, and value guidance.

# 2. Executive Summary

## 2.1. Background & Motivation
*   **Core Problem:** Domestic variety shows are developing rapidly, yet research specifically on "reasoning reality shows" (推理类真人秀) remains scarce. Existing studies often focus only on single aspects like marketing or production, lacking a systematic narrative analysis.
*   **Importance:** The show "Star Detective" has maintained high popularity and reputation for seven years (Seasons 1-7). Understanding its success mechanism is crucial for the industry to avoid homogenization and improve content quality.
*   **Gaps in Prior Research:** Previous studies lacked a comprehensive梳理 (combing/organizing) of narrative strategies. They tended to analyze specific elements (like suspense or games) in isolation rather than viewing the narrative as an integrated system.
*   **Innovative Idea:** The paper applies classic and post-classical narratology theories to systematically deconstruct the show's narrative architecture, identifying specific strategies like "Intertextual," "Suspense," and "Gamified" narratives that distinguish it from other programs.

## 2.2. Main Contributions / Findings
*   **Primary Contribution:** A structured framework analyzing the show through three distinct narrative strategies. It moves beyond simple description to explain *how* these strategies function theoretically (e.g., applying Greimas' matrix for character relationships).
*   **Key Findings:**
    *   **Intertextuality:** The show creates a "Detective Planet" (明侦星球) through recurring characters (e.g., the "NZND" band) and connecting storylines across seasons.
    *   **Suspense:** The narrative uses a "layer-by-layer" solution method (peeling bamboo shoots) and audiovisual techniques (montage, camera movement) to manage tension.
    *   **Gamification:** The program treats the investigation as a game level, using "Magic Circle" theory to separate game space from reality, enhancing immersion.
*   **Problem Solved:** These findings solve the problem of understanding how long-running reality shows maintain freshness and logical consistency while balancing entertainment with social responsibility.

# 3. Prerequisite Knowledge & Related Work

## 3.1. Foundational Concepts
To understand this research, several key concepts from media studies and narratology must be defined.

*   <strong>Narratology (叙事学):</strong> The study of narrative structure and theory. Originated in the 1970s (structuralist phase) and evolved to include post-classical approaches that consider social context and reader reception. This paper uses both classical and post-classical frameworks to analyze the show as a text.
*   <strong>Intertextuality (互文性):</strong> A term coined by Julia Kristeva. It suggests that no text exists in isolation; every text is a mosaic of quotations from other texts. In this show, it means referencing other movies, songs, previous episodes, or social trends.
    *   **Narrow Intertextuality:** Self-reference within the show (reusing characters/settings).
    *   **Broad Intertextuality:** Connection to external culture (social issues, other media).
*   <strong>Suspense (悬念):</strong> A narrative technique where the audience is kept in uncertainty about future events. It relies on information gaps between the characters/audience and the truth.
*   <strong>Gamification (游戏化):</strong> Applying game design elements (rules, points, challenges) in non-game contexts. Here, the investigative process is structured like a game level.
*   <strong>Magic Circle (魔圈):</strong> A concept from game studies (Huizinga/Zimmerman) referring to the psychological space created by game rules where players agree to accept specific constraints different from real life.
*   <strong>Greimas' Semiotic Square (格雷马斯矩阵):</strong> A structuralist tool to map relationships between characters (contradiction, opposition, implication). Used here to analyze the Detective vs. Murderer dynamic.

# 4. Methodology

## 4.1. Principles
The core principle of this research is **Qualitative Textual Analysis** grounded in **Narratological Theory**. The author treats the TV episodes as "texts" that can be decoded to reveal underlying structural strategies. The intuition is that the show's longevity is not accidental but the result of deliberate, repeatable narrative engineering.

## 4.2. Core Methodology In-depth
The methodology is broken down into four layers of analysis, corresponding to the paper's chapters.

### Layer 1: Case Study Selection
*   **Procedure:** The researcher selected **all 7 seasons** of "Star Detective" (approximately 80+ episodes) as the primary dataset.
*   **Sampling:** Specific typical episodes were chosen for detailed illustration (e.g., S1E03 for character recurrence, S2E07 for suspense music).
*   **Logic:** This ensures the findings are representative of the entire franchise history, capturing its evolution from Season 1 to Season 7.

### Layer 2: Intertextual Narrative Analysis
*   **Step 1: Narrow Intertextuality.**
    *   **Action:** Analyze fixed elements (program flow, fixed actors).
    *   **Example:** Examining the repetition of the "NZND" idol group across 32 episodes to trace the "Detective Planet" network.
*   **Step 2: Broad Intertextuality.**
    *   **Action:** Trace references to external cultural texts.
    *   **Example:** Identifying adaptations of Agatha Christie novels (*Murder on the Orient Express*) or films (*Titanic*).
    *   **Formula/Model Application:** Using the definition of **Intertextuality** ($T = \sum t_i$) where $T$ is the current text and $t_i$ are the referenced texts (conceptualized in the text).
*   **Step 3: Cross-Media Extension.**
    *   **Action:** Map the ecosystem beyond the main show (spin-offs, interactive dramas, offline venues).
    *   **Visualization:** Creating a network diagram of衍生 products (see Table 2.2).

### Layer 3: Suspense Narrative Analysis
*   **Step 1: Suspense Generation.**
    *   **Technique:** Multi-layered storytelling (Outer, Inner,潜/Narration Layers).
    *   **Mechanism:** The "Outer Layer" sets the rules (game suspense); the "Inner Layer" provides conflicting character testimonies (narrative suspense).
*   **Step 2: Suspense Paving.**
    *   **Space:** Utilization of the "One-Act Play" (独幕剧) limited space to concentrate tension.
    *   **Format:** Structuring search phases (Evidence Gathering -> Deduction -> Voting) to control the pace of revelation.
*   **Step 3: Suspense Resolution.**
    *   **Technique:** "Peeling Bamboo Shoots" (剥笋式). Evidence is layered superficially first (identity/motive), then mid-level (method/tool), finally deeply (truth).
    *   **Visual/Audio:** Using specific camera movements (slow push-in) and sound (music/lyrics) to encode emotional suspense.

### Layer 4: Gamified Narrative Analysis
*   **Step 1: Plot Gamification.**
    *   **Structure:** Non-linear, level-based progression (Trigger Task -> Challenge -> Reward).
    *   **Interaction:** Dual interaction (internal player dynamics + external audience feedback via social media).
*   **Step 2: Role Gamification.**
    *   **Performance:** Actors maintain "Alienation Effect" (间离效果) to balance immersion with rational deduction.
    *   **Decentralization:** No single protagonist; all players contribute equally to the narrative (analyzed via He Jiong's role distribution in Table 4.1).
*   **Step 3: Scene Gamification.**
    *   **Environment:** Construction of the "Magic Circle" where game rules override reality.
    *   **Immersion:** Real-world location shooting (Hotels, Trains) combined with virtual narrative elements (time travel, soul swapping) to create a "Heterotopia" (异托邦).

# 6. Results & Analysis

## 6.1. Core Results Analysis
The analysis reveals that "Star Detective" succeeds by creating a cohesive narrative ecosystem.

### Result 1: Intertextuality Builds Loyalty
By reusing characters (e.g., "NZND"), the show encourages viewers to watch previous seasons to fully understand the current story. This creates a high barrier to entry for new viewers but immense loyalty for existing fans.
*   **Evidence:** 32 episodes are linked by the NZND storyline alone.
*   **Outcome:** Fans actively seek out past episodes, driving traffic to the platform.

### Result 2: Suspense Management Controls Pacing
The "Layer-by-Layer" suspense prevents audience fatigue. By hiding information strategically (not revealing the killer until the end, but revealing clues gradually), the show maintains tension without overwhelming the viewer.
*   **Evidence:** Three rounds of evidence searching allow for progressive discovery.
*   **Outcome:** Viewers feel involved in the puzzle-solving process rather than passive observers.

### Result 3: Gamification Enhances Immersion
The separation of "Game World" and "Real World" allows celebrities to act freely. The use of real locations (hotels, trains) reduces the distance between the fiction and reality.
*   **Evidence:** Season 3 built a 4000 sq.m. hotel set.
*   **Outcome:** Visual spectacle attracts clicks and improves perceived value.

## 6.3. Ablation Studies / Parameter Analysis
In the context of qualitative research, "ablation" equates to observing what happens when specific variables are removed or changed over time.
*   **Evolution of Scripts:** Comparing Season 1 scripts to Season 7 scripts shows increased complexity and social commentary depth.
*   **Effect of Spin-offs:** Removing the "Spin-off" variable (e.g., focusing only on the main show) would likely reduce long-term fan retention. The addition of "Name Detective Academy" acts as a training ground for talent, stabilizing the main show's quality.
*   **Audience Feedback Loop:** The paper notes that audience suggestions (e.g., from Weibo users) directly influenced subsequent scripts (e.g., adapting *No Country for Old Men* ideas). This validates the "Interactive Gamification" parameter.

# 7. Conclusion & Reflections

## 7.1. Conclusion Summary
This thesis successfully categorizes the narrative strategies of "Star Detective" into three pillars: **Intertextuality**, **Suspense**, and **Gamification**.
1.  **Intertextuality:** Creates a dense web of connections (characters, settings, cross-media) that fosters a loyal fanbase ("Detective Planet").
2.  **Suspense:** Uses structural devices (multi-layer narration, Rashomon perspective) and sensory tools (audio/visual language) to maintain audience engagement without causing anxiety.
3.  **Gamification:** Blends reality and virtuality through "Magic Circle" rules, allowing high-production immersion and active audience participation.
    The conclusion emphasizes that the show's success lies in balancing **entertainment** with **value guidance** (legal education, social responsibility).

## 7.2. Limitations & Future Work
The author explicitly acknowledges limitations:
1.  **Lack of Critical Reflection:** The paper focuses heavily on strengths and ignores potential weaknesses (e.g., high production costs, script fatigue, celebrity dominance over logic).
2.  **Subjectivity:** Suggestions for the future rely on the author's personal observation rather than quantitative market data.
3.  **Feasibility:** Technical and business feasibility of replicating this model for smaller producers is not deeply explored.
    *Future Directions:* Research should expand to compare "Star Detective" with international counterparts quantitatively and analyze the economic sustainability of high-cost formats in a downturn economy.

## 7.3. Personal Insights & Critique
*   **Transferability:** The "Multi-Layer Suspense" and "Intertextual World-Building" strategies are highly applicable to other episodic media (web series, RPG games, long-form podcasts). The concept of a "Closed Loop Narrative" where every episode feeds into a larger canon is a key takeaway.
*   **Potential Issues:**
    *   **Production Cost:** The reliance on massive set construction (e.g., the 4000sqm hotel) is not sustainable for average budgets. Future iterations might need to find cost-effective alternatives for immersion (e.g., Virtual Production/Virtual Sets).
    *   **Viewer Fatigue:** As noted by the drop in Douban score in Season 7 (to 7.7), audiences eventually tire of high-stakes mystery. The balance between mystery and light-hearted comedy (variety elements) becomes harder to strike over time.
    *   **Legal Risks:** With the "Law Education" segment becoming a core feature, the line between entertainment and education blurs, potentially inviting stricter regulatory scrutiny compared to pure entertainment shows.
*   **Overall Value:** Despite limitations, the thesis provides a robust blueprint for understanding the modern "IP-driven" variety show model, proving that narrative depth is the ultimate moat against content homogenization.
