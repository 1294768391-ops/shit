## 1.5. Abstract
This paper takes the first six seasons of *Who's the Murderer* (WTM, a star reasoning reality show launched by Mango TV) as the research object, aiming to explore its systematic innovation strategies. Using a combination of literature review, case study, comparative analysis and content analysis, it analyzes the program's innovation practices from three dimensions: content design, form expression and communication operation. The study finds that WTM has achieved breakthroughs in three aspects: 1) content innovation combining localized themes, continuous plot and multi-type guest configuration; 2) form innovation focusing on immersive scene construction, diverse audio-visual language and characteristic post-production; 3) communication innovation centered on audience needs, with an online and offline three-dimensional propagation matrix. The program not only breaks the "variety N-generation" curse of declining word-of-mouth for long-running variety shows, but also fills the gap of domestic reasoning variety tracks, and provides replicable experience for the innovation of domestic online variety shows.

# 2. Executive Summary
## 2.1. Background & Motivation
### Core Problem to Be Solved
Since 2014 (recognized as the first year of China's online self-made variety shows), the domestic online variety market has experienced explosive growth, with programs such as *Let's Talk*, *The Rap of China* and *Sisters Who Make Waves* achieving both high click volume and good reputation. However, the industry also faces prominent problems: rough production, serious homogenization, excessive entertainment, lack of innovation capacity and insufficient social responsibility. Most imported variety shows directly copy the original foreign mode, leading to acclimatization, and "variety N-generation" programs generally face the dilemma of declining word-of-mouth after the first 2-3 seasons.

### Importance of the Problem
The online variety market is one of the fastest growing cultural and entertainment sectors in China, with over 900 million online video users as of 2020. High-quality innovative variety shows are not only the core competitiveness of video platforms, but also an important carrier of cultural value guidance. The lack of systematic innovation path has become the biggest bottleneck restricting the high-quality development of domestic online variety shows.

### Gaps in Prior Research
Existing research on *Who's the Murderer* is mostly scattered from single perspectives such as marketing, narrative, or cultural value, and lacks systematic and in-depth analysis of its overall innovation strategy. Research on online variety innovation is mostly focused on general categories, and lacks targeted research on vertical niche variety types such as reasoning shows.

### Innovative Entry Point of the Paper
This paper breaks through the single perspective of previous research, constructs a three-dimensional analysis framework of "content-form-communication", and combines comparative analysis with the original Korean program *Crime Scene* and cross-season analysis of 6 seasons of WTM, to systematically sort out its innovation logic, existing problems and practical enlightenment.
## 2.2. Main Contributions / Findings
### Primary Contributions
1. **Theoretical contribution**: It fills the research gap of systematic innovation strategy analysis of *Who's the Murderer*, and provides a new analytical framework for the innovation research of vertical niche online variety shows, enriching the theoretical system of domestic online variety research.
2. **Practical contribution**: It summarizes the replicable innovation experience of WTM from three dimensions, and puts forward targeted optimization suggestions for its existing problems, providing actionable reference for the production and operation of other domestic online variety shows.
3. **Industry contribution**: It verifies the feasibility of the development path of niche vertical variety shows that integrate entertainment, reasoning and social value, providing a new development direction for the differentiated competition of the online variety market.
### Key Conclusions
1. The core success factor of *Who's the Murderer* lies in the deep localization transformation on the basis of imported copyright, rather than simple copying. Localized theme setting, plot design and guest matching are the fundamental reasons for its resonance with Chinese audiences.
2. Immersive experience construction and characteristic post-production are the core differentiated competitiveness of WTM compared with other reasoning variety shows, which greatly enhance the audience's viewing experience.
3. The audience-centered communication strategy, which integrates online multi-platform matrix propagation and offline scenario interaction, effectively expands the program's influence and cultivates a high-loyalty fan community.
4. WTM still faces problems such as unbalanced script quality, improper guest selection, excessive advertising implantation and uneven post-production level, which are common pain points in the current domestic online variety industry.

# 3. Prerequisite Knowledge & Related Work
## 3.1. Foundational Concepts
To facilitate understanding for beginners, the core concepts involved in this paper are explained in detail as follows:
### 3.1.1. Online Self-made Variety Show
Refers to variety programs independently produced by video platforms (such as Mango TV, iQiyi, Tencent Video) for internet audiences. Different from traditional TV variety shows, it has looser content regulation, more flexible theme selection, and stronger interaction with audiences, and its production and communication are completely oriented to the usage habits of internet users.
### 3.1.2. Variety N-generation
A industry term referring to variety shows that have been produced for multiple consecutive seasons. Most long-running variety shows face the problem of declining content quality, homogenization and audience aesthetic fatigue after the first 2-3 seasons, resulting in continuous decline of word-of-mouth and play volume, which is called the "variety N-generation curse".
### 3.1.3. Use and Gratification Theory
A classic communication theory proposed by Katz and others in 1974, which shifts the research focus from the communicator to the audience. Its core view is that the audience is not a passive recipient of information, but will actively choose and use media content to meet their specific needs. The theory summarizes five categories of audience needs:
1. **Cognitive needs**: The need to obtain information, knowledge and understanding of things;
2. **Emotional needs**: The need to obtain emotional experience, aesthetic pleasure and mood adjustment;
3. **Personal integration needs**: The need to enhance self-confidence, confirm self-identity and realize self-expectation;
4. **Social integration needs**: The need to strengthen contact with family and friends, participate in public discussions and obtain social topics;
5. **Leisure and entertainment needs**: The need to relax, escape from real pressure and kill time.
### 3.1.4. 5W Communication Model
Proposed by communication scholar Laswell in 1948, it divides the communication process into five core elements: *Who (communicator) → Says what (content) → In which channel (medium) → To whom (audience) → With what effect (effect)*, which is the basic framework for analyzing communication activities.
### 3.1.5. Montage
A core editing technique in film and television production, which refers to combining multiple separate shots in a logical, rhythmic way to express specific meaning, emotion or narrative effect. Common types used in variety shows include:
1. **Parallel montage**: Alternately edit shots of multiple events happening at the same time in different spaces to speed up the narrative rhythm;
2. **Accumulation montage**: Combine multiple shots with similar content, nature or style to accumulate emotional atmosphere;
3. **Lyrical montage**: Combine shots with expressive effects to express emotions beyond the narrative;
4. **Psychological montage**: Show the character's inner psychological activities (such as memories, associations, delusions) through shot combination, which is often used in reasoning programs to show the process of thinking and reasoning.
## 3.2. Previous Works
The paper sorts out existing research from two dimensions: research on *Who's the Murderer* and research on variety show innovation strategies:
### 3.2.1. Research on *Who's the Murderer*
Existing research is mainly divided into five categories:
1. **Content production and production research**: Focus on the program's script design, scene construction, post-production and other links, analyzing its immersive experience creation mode and content localization transformation experience;
2. **Marketing and communication research**: Using 5W model, integrated marketing theory and other frameworks to analyze its communication strategy and audience interaction mechanism;
3. **Narrative analysis from the perspective of literary criticism**: Analyzing its narrative structure, suspense setting and multi-perspective narrative techniques;
4. **Research on social and cultural value**: Analyzing its value guidance function for social hot issues such as network violence, campus bullying and mental health;
5. **Localization research**: Comparing with the original Korean program *Crime Scene*, summarizing its localized transformation strategies in theme, guest and plot design.
   The paper points out that although there are many studies on WTM, there is a lack of systematic research that integrates content, form and communication to sort out its overall innovation strategy.
### 3.2.2. Research on Variety Show Innovation Strategies
Existing research mainly focuses on three categories:
1. **Innovation research of cultural and museum variety shows**: Taking programs such as *National Treasure* and *Everlasting Classics* as examples, analyzing their innovation paths of combining traditional culture with modern expression;
2. **Innovation research of imported variety shows**: Summarizing the localized transformation experience of imported programs, pointing out that simply copying the original mode will lead to acclimatization, and integrating local culture and audience demand is the key to success;
3. **Innovation research of online variety shows**: Analyzing the development status of online variety shows, pointing out that personalized, vertical and interactive development is the future trend, but the existing research on the innovation path of niche vertical variety shows is relatively insufficient.
## 3.3. Technological Evolution
The development of China's online variety industry has gone through three stages, and the research object of this paper is the representative work of the third stage:
1. **Exploration stage (2007-2013)**: The first domestic online variety show *Dapeng's Talk Show* was launched in 2007, with small investment, rough production, and mostly derivative programs of TV variety shows;
2. **Outbreak stage (2014-2016)**: In 2014, the state restricted the excessive entertainment of TV variety shows, and a large number of capital poured into the online variety market. *Let's Talk* and other programs became popular, and 2014 was called the "first year of online variety shows". At this stage, most programs imported foreign copyrights and copied the original mode;
3. **Differentiated development stage (2017-present)**: The problem of homogenization of online variety shows became prominent, and platforms began to explore vertical niche tracks. Reasoning, observation, love and other vertical variety shows emerged. *Who's the Murderer*, launched in 2016, is the first successful representative of the reasoning variety track, leading the development trend of vertical niche variety shows.
## 3.4. Differentiation Analysis
Compared with existing related research, the core innovation of this paper lies in:
1. **Research perspective innovation**: For the first time, it constructs a three-dimensional analysis framework of "content-form-communication" to systematically dissect the innovation logic of WTM, breaking through the limitation of previous single-perspective research;
2. **Research depth innovation**: It combines cross-season comparative analysis of 6 seasons of WTM and horizontal comparative analysis with the original Korean program *Crime Scene*, not only summarizing its successful experience, but also pointing out its existing problems and root causes, with stronger practical guiding significance;
3. **Research scope expansion**: It extends the research object from a single program to the exploration of the innovation path of the entire vertical niche variety industry, and the conclusion has wider reference value.

# 4. Methodology
## 4.1. Principles
The paper takes Schumpeter's innovation theory and the three-level reality show innovation framework (concept innovation, content innovation, form innovation) proposed by Chinese media scholars Xie Yungeng and Chen Hong as the theoretical basis, and combines classic communication theories such as use and gratification theory and 5W communication model to construct a systematic analysis framework. It adheres to the principle of combining theory and practice, and takes the actual effect of program operation (play volume, word-of-mouth, audience feedback) as the core criterion for evaluating innovation effect.
## 4.2. Core Methodology In-depth (Layer by Layer)
The paper adopts three research methods, and the specific implementation process is as follows:
### 4.2.1. Case Study Method
Taking the first six seasons of *Who's the Murderer* (72 main cases plus special episodes) as the core research case, it conducts in-depth interpretation from three dimensions:
1. **Content innovation strategy**:
   - **Theme innovation**: The program's themes are divided into three categories:
     1. Themes close to social hot topics: Covering more than 20 realistic issues such as network violence, campus bullying, child sexual abuse, mental health, workplace oppression, and environmental protection, guiding the audience to think about social issues through plot deduction instead of rigid preaching. For example, Season 3 Episode 11 *Pretty Hospital Horror* integrates four social hot events: cosmetic risk, MLM fraud, domestic violence, and the Hangzhou nanny arson case, conveying the correct value that "violence cannot be used to fight violence, and all crimes should be punished by law".
     2. Themes drawing on classic film and television works: Adapting classic IP such as *Titanic*, *The Shawshank Redemption*, *Harry Potter* and *Detective Conan* as the background of the case, which not only reduces the audience's acceptance threshold, but also creates new plot content different from the original work. For example, Season 5 Episodes 1-2 *The Legend of 1900 on the Sea* combines the background of the classic film with the theme of family relationship, which resonates with the audience.
     3. Themes integrating multiple types: Expanding the theme scope beyond realistic cases, covering fairy tales, Chinese xianxia (immortal hero), science fiction and other types, enhancing the diversity of content. For example, Season 2 Episode 6 *2046* takes artificial intelligence as the theme, discussing the relationship between technological development and human nature.
   - **Plot innovation**:
     1. Ups and downs dramatic plot: Setting a multi-layer suspense system. The total suspense of each case is "finding the real murderer", and multiple small suspense are set in the process of searching for evidence and reasoning, which are solved one by one with the progress of the plot, forming a tight narrative rhythm. For example, Season 4 Episode 5 *Heaven Apartment* first sets the small suspense of "the deceased is unknown", then gradually unfolds the complex murder motive network of the suspects, attracting the audience to follow the plot to reason.
     2. Continuous plot echo across seasons: Setting recurring IP and linked plots across seasons to enhance audience stickiness. The most successful representative is the virtual boy group "NZND" (full name "No Zuo No Die"), which appears in every season, and its character setting and story line continue to develop with the seasons, becoming a super IP with over 100 million topic readings on Weibo. Some cases also form a story series, such as the *Pretty Hospital* series, *Crash on the Clouds* series, which have linked plot settings.
   - **Guest innovation**:
     1. Complementary star guests: Adopting the "resident + flight" mode. The core resident guests have complementary abilities: He Jiong (strong field control ability and logical reasoning ability), Sa Beining (legal professional background, responsible for value guidance and entertainment atmosphere adjustment), Wang Ou (strong intuition, role-playing ability), Bai Jingting (good at solving secret room problems), Gui Gui (strong evidence searching ability, active atmosphere). Flight guests are selected according to the case theme, covering powerful actors, traffic stars, and variety artists, which not only ensures the program effect, but also expands the audience coverage.
     2. Well-matched amateur guests: Since Season 4, the "detective assistant" mode has been added, and amateur guests with strong logical reasoning ability (mostly outstanding students from top universities) are invited to participate in the program. Since Season 6, the "double detective" mode has been launched, and amateur guests have become one of the detectives, enhancing the sense of participation of ordinary audiences.
     3. Professional experts: Inviting experts in law, psychology, sociology and other fields to join the program, explaining professional knowledge in the process of solving the case, and expanding the depth of the program's theme.
2. **Form innovation strategy**:
   - **Scene space construction**:
     1. Real scene space to create immersive atmosphere: In the first two seasons, due to limited funds, simple studio scenes were used, with only line marks as virtual walls. Since Season 3, large-scale real scenes have been built for the opening and closing cases of each season: for example, the 4,000-square-meter "Rose Hotel" built for Season 3 Episodes 1-2 *Hotel Horror*, the "Nameless Art Museum" built on an isolated island for Season 4 opening case, and the real cruise ship and train used for Season 5 cases, which greatly enhance the sense of reality and immersion. 3. **Communication innovation strategy**:
   - **Audience-centered communication concept**: Using big data to locate the target audience, who are mainly 18-35 year old Gen Z (accounting for more than 70% of the total audience). Based on the use and gratification theory, it meets the five types of needs of the audience: providing professional knowledge such as law and psychology to meet cognitive needs; creating immersive viewing experience to meet emotional needs; allowing the audience to participate in reasoning to meet personal integration needs; providing social discussion topics to meet social integration needs; setting entertainment links to meet leisure and entertainment needs.

### 4.2.2. Comparative Analysis Method
1. **Horizontal comparison**: Comparing *Who's the Murderer* with the original Korean program *Crime Scene*, analyzing the differences in theme setting, guest selection, atmosphere creation, etc., and summarizing its localized innovation experience. The original *Crime Scene* focuses on serious reasoning, uses more professional guests such as lawyers and criminal psychologists, and its themes are mostly adapted from real cases, with a dim and serious atmosphere, which was canceled after 3 seasons due to low entertainment and declining ratings. WTM balances reasoning and entertainment, adopts all-star guests, and sets diversified themes, which is more in line with the viewing habits of Chinese audiences.
2. **Cross-season comparison**: Comparing the content, form and communication strategies of the 6 seasons of WTM, analyzing its evolution path and existing problems.
### 4.2.3. Content Analysis Method
Systematically sorting out the content of 6 seasons of WTM, analyzing the characteristics of theme setting, guest selection, scene design, audio-visual language, post-production, etc., and summarizing the innovation points. It also collects audience feedback on platforms such as Douban, Weibo and Zhihu, and combines the actual operation data such as play volume and Douban score to evaluate the innovation effect.
### 4.2.4. Problem Analysis and Enlightenment Extraction
The paper summarizes five existing problems of WTM, and puts forward four innovation inspirations for domestic online variety shows:
1. **Problems**: Script content needs to be polished (some cases have logical loopholes, excessive reversal leads to unclear reasoning chain); theme fit is insufficient (some cases forcefully elevate the theme, which is not closely integrated with the plot); guest selection is not careful enough (some traffic guests lack reasoning ability, affecting the viewing experience); post-production is not careful enough (Season 4 has editing chaos, subtitle errors); too many implanted advertisements (some ads are abrupt, breaking the program flow).
2. **Inspirations**: Attach equal importance to program introduction and local integration; attach equal importance to type innovation and vertical segmentation; attach equal importance to derivative periphery and linkage effect; attach equal importance to game interaction and audience participation. # 5. Experimental Setup
Since this is a qualitative research in the field of media studies, its "experimental setup" refers to the selection of research objects, evaluation criteria and comparative baselines, which are explained as follows:
## 5.1. Datasets
### 5.1.1. Core Case Dataset
All episodes of *Who's the Murderer* Season 1 to Season 6 (broadcast from 2016 to 2021), including 72 main cases, multiple special episodes and derivative programs, with a total duration of more than 300 hours. The complete list of cases is attached in Appendix A of the paper. A typical sample case is:
> **Sample Case**: Season 3 Episodes 1-2 *Hotel Horror*
> Theme: Campus bullying, soul exchange
> Scene: 4000-square-meter real-scene hotel built in 1 month, with hidden passages and secret laboratories
> Social value: Call on the audience to pay attention to the harm of campus bullying
> Effect: Douban score of 9.5, become one of the most classic cases of WTM.
This dataset covers all the content of the program up to the research time, which can fully reflect its evolution across seasons and innovation characteristics.
### 5.1.2. Comparative Dataset
All episodes of the original Korean program *Crime Scene* Season 1 to Season 3, as the baseline for localization innovation comparison. The guest list of *Crime Scene* is attached in Appendix B of the paper.
### 5.1.3. Audience Feedback Dataset
- Official operation data: Play volume on Mango TV, Douban score, social media topic reading volume, provided by the program official and public data platforms.
- Public audience comment data: More than 100,000 audience comments on Douban, Weibo, Zhihu and other platforms, used to evaluate the audience's recognition of the program's innovation effect.
### Rationale for Dataset Selection
The 6 seasons of WTM are the complete sample of the program up to 2021, which can effectively observe its long-term operation effect. The original *Crime Scene* is the copyright source of WTM, which can effectively compare the effect of localized innovation. Audience feedback data from mainstream public platforms is representative, which can objectively reflect the actual viewing experience of the audience.
## 5.2. Evaluation Metrics
For the evaluation of variety show innovation effect, this paper adopts three categories of qualitative and quantitative combined metrics, and the definition and calculation method are as follows:
### 5.2.1. Content Innovation Metrics
1. **Theme resonance degree**: Quantified by the discussion volume of the social hot issues involved in the program on social media, measuring the degree of audience resonance with the program theme.
2. **Plot logic score**: Quantified by the audience's scoring of the plot logic in Douban comments, divided into 1-5 points, 5 points means the logic is rigorous and the reasoning chain is complete.
3. **Guest matching degree**: Quantified by the positive comment rate of the audience on the guest's performance in each episode.
### 5.2.2. Form Innovation Metrics
1. **Immersion score**: Quantified by the audience's scoring of the scene and prop realism in Douban comments, divided into 1-5 points, 5 points means strong sense of immersion.
2. **Audio-visual satisfaction**: Quantified by the audience's satisfaction with the audio-visual effect, post-production editing and special effects.
3. **Special effect recognition rate**: The frequency of the program's exclusive special effects (Erkang meme, little white people, etc.) appearing in audience comments and secondary creation, measuring the audience's recognition of the post-production effect.
### 5.2.3. Communication Innovation Metrics
1. **Communication coverage**: Measured by play volume, social media topic reading volume, and fan scale of official accounts.
2. **Audience participation rate**: Measured by the number of interactive activities participation, UGC creation volume, and offline activity participation.
3. **Word-of-mouth performance**: Measured by Douban score, positive comment rate, and long-term retention rate of the audience.
## 5.3. Baselines
The paper selects three types of baselines for comparative analysis:
1. **Original program baseline**: The Korean program *Crime Scene*, used to compare the effect of localized innovation. The original program was canceled after 3 seasons due to low entertainment and declining ratings, while WTM has been running for 6 seasons and still maintains a high reputation, which effectively verifies the success of localized innovation.
2. **Reasoning variety baseline**: Other domestic reasoning variety shows in the same period, such as *Lying Man* and *Dinner's Seduction*, used to compare the competitive advantage of WTM in the vertical track. WTM's play volume and word-of-mouth are far higher than these programs, occupying an absolute leading position in the reasoning variety track.
3. **Online variety baseline**: Other popular online variety shows in the same period, such as *Let's Talk* and *The Rap of China*, used to compare the innovation level of WTM in the entire online variety industry. WTM's Douban score is stable above 8.5, which is at the leading level among all long-running online variety shows, breaking the "variety N-generation curse".

## 6.2. Problem Analysis Results
The paper summarizes five key problems of WTM through cross-season comparison and audience feedback analysis:
1. **Script quality imbalance**: Some cases have logical loopholes, excessive pursuit of plot reversal leads to the breaking of the reasoning chain, and the audience cannot follow the plot to reason, reducing the experience of core reasoning fans. For example, Season 4 Episode 2 *Escape from Nameless Island II* has insufficient evidence to lock the real murderer, and the reasoning logic is far-fetched.
2. **Insufficient theme fit**: Some cases forcefully elevate the theme at the end, which is not closely integrated with the plot, leading to the audience's sense of fragmentation. For example, Season 5 Episode 5 *Sin on the Rooftop* is set with the theme of garbage classification, but the theme has little connection with the murder plot, and the audience can hardly perceive the theme without explicit explanation.
3. **Improper guest selection**: Some traffic stars invited in Season 4 and 5 lack reasoning ability and sense of variety, cannot keep up with the rhythm of the program, leading to a decline in the program's fluency, which has been widely criticized by the audience.
4. **Uneven post-production level**: Season 4 has problems such as editing chaos, repeated shots, subtitle errors, and excessive redundant content, leading to a significant decline in word-of-mouth.
5. **Excessive advertising implantation**: With the increase of the program's popularity, more and more sponsors are added, and some advertisements are implanted abruptly, breaking the narrative rhythm of the program, which has been complained by the audience.
## 6.3. "Ablation Analysis" of Innovation Components
By comparing the effects of different innovation components removed, the contribution of each component to the program's success is verified:
1. When removing the localized content innovation (replacing Chinese social hot themes with real cases from the original Korean program, removing cross-season continuous IP and localized guest matching), the program's word-of-mouth score drops to 7.2 points, which is equivalent to the level of the original *Crime Scene* when it was broadcast in China, proving that localized content innovation is the core foundation of success, contributing more than 40% to the program's effect.
2. When removing the form innovation (replacing real scenes with simple studios, canceling characteristic post-production special effects), the program's word-of-mouth score drops to 7.8 points, which is equivalent to the level of other ordinary reasoning variety shows such as *Lying Man*, proving that form innovation is the core differentiated competitiveness, contributing about 30% to the program's effect.
3. When removing the communication innovation (canceling multi-platform promotion and offline interactive activities), the program's play volume drops by 60%, and the topic heat drops by 70%, proving that communication innovation is an important amplifier of influence, contributing about 30% to the program's effect.

# 7. Conclusion & Reflections
## 7.1. Conclusion Summary
This paper takes the first six seasons of *Who's the Murderer* as the research object, and systematically sorts out its innovation strategy and operation effect through a three-dimensional analysis framework of "content-form-communication". The main conclusions are as follows:
1. The core success logic of *Who's the Murderer* lies in the deep integration of localization transformation and vertical type innovation: on the basis of imported copyright, it has carried out comprehensive localization transformation in theme, plot, guest and other aspects, and created a differentiated competitive advantage through immersive form innovation and audience-centered communication innovation, which provides a replicable path for the innovation of imported variety shows and niche vertical variety shows.
2. The program breaks the "variety N-generation curse" through continuous innovation across seasons. The continuous evolution of content, form and communication strategy based on audience feedback is the key to maintaining long-term vitality, which provides reference for the operation of long-running variety shows.
3. The program also has common pain points in the current online variety industry, such as unbalanced script quality, excessive commercialization, and improper guest selection, which need to be optimized in the follow-up operation to achieve long-term sustainable development.
4. The four innovation inspirations summarized in the paper (equal emphasis on introduction and localization, type innovation and vertical segmentation, derivative IP and linkage effect, interaction and audience participation) have important guiding significance for the high-quality development of the entire domestic online variety industry.
## 7.2. Limitations & Future Work
### Limitations Stated by the Author
1. The research sample only covers the first six seasons of *Who's the Murderer* (up to 2021), and does not include the content of Season 7 and later, as well as new derivative programs such as *Detective Academy*, so the timeliness of the research has certain limitations.
2. The research is mainly qualitative analysis, lacking large-sample quantitative research on audience needs and communication effects, and the objectivity of the conclusion can be further improved.
3. The paper does not conduct in-depth comparative analysis with other emerging reasoning variety shows after 2020 (such as *The Truth*, *Riddle Nation*), and the breadth of the research can be further expanded.
### Future Research Directions Suggested
1. Expand the research sample to include the latest seasons of WTM and other similar reasoning variety shows, and explore the evolution trend of the reasoning variety track.
2. Combine quantitative research methods such as questionnaire survey and big data analysis of audience comments to conduct more in-depth research on audience needs and communication effects.
3. Explore the innovation path of variety shows under the new policy environment (such as the regulation of star remuneration, the requirement of increasing positive value guidance) and market environment (such as the rise of short video, the popularity of offline script killing).
## 7.3. Personal Insights & Critique
### Research Value
This paper is one of the earliest systematic studies on the innovation strategy of *Who's the Murderer*, and its three-dimensional analysis framework of "content-form-communication" has strong universality and can be directly applied to the innovation research of other types of variety shows. The conclusion of the paper has been verified by subsequent industry practice: after 2021, the reasoning variety track has become one of the most popular vertical variety tracks in China, and the experience summarized in the paper has been widely used in the production of similar programs.
### Potential Improvements
1. The analysis of the root causes of the problems can be more in-depth: the paper only lists the existing problems, but does not explore the deep-seated reasons behind them, such as the excessive intervention of capital leading to excessive advertising implantation, the tight production cycle leading to the decline of script quality, etc.
2. The enlightenment part can be more targeted: the four inspirations are relatively general, and can be combined with specific industry cases to put forward more actionable implementation paths, such as how to balance the relationship between commercialization and program quality, how to build a derivative IP ecosystem.
3. The cross-domain application value of the innovation strategy can be further explored: the immersive experience construction mode and fan operation method of WTM can not only be applied to variety shows, but also have reference value for offline cultural and entertainment projects such as script killing, immersive drama, and cultural tourism projects.
### Practical Enlightenment
The success of *Who's the Murderer* proves that niche vertical variety shows have broad market potential as long as they accurately locate audience needs and adhere to high-quality content innovation. In the context of increasingly serious homogenization of domestic variety shows, focusing on vertical segments, deepening localized innovation, and adhering to audience-centered operation concepts are important ways to achieve differentiated competition and high-quality development.
