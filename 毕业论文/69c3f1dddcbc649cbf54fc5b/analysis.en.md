# 1. Bibliographic Information
## 1.1. Title
The paper's Chinese title is *《明星大侦探》综艺节目本土化研究*, with the English title *Research on the localization of Who’s the murderer*. Its core topic is to study the localized transformation of the South Korean imported variety show *Crime Scene* into the Chinese hit program *Who's the Murderer* (also called *Star Detective*), and explore replicable paths for the localized adaptation of overseas imported variety shows in the Chinese market.
## 1.2. Authors
The author is Chen Yang, a master's degree candidate in Journalism and Communication at Gansu University of Political Science and Law, supervised by Associate Professor Wei Wenhuan. The thesis is a degree-granting work of the university.
## 1.3. Publication Venue
This is a master's degree thesis from Gansu University of Political Science and Law, not published in a journal or conference. As an official academic output in the field of journalism and communication, it focuses on media industry and cultural communication research, and has practical reference value for the variety show production industry.
## 1.4. Publication Year
The thesis was submitted in May 2022, defended in June 2022, and the degree was awarded in June 2022, so its publication year is 2022.
## 1.5. Abstract
This paper points out that after 2010, China saw a boom in overseas variety show copyright imports, but many imported programs failed to achieve long-term development due to lack of localized adaptation. Taking *Who's the Murderer*, imported by Mango TV from South Korea JTBC's *Crime Scene*, as the research object, the paper uses cross-cultural communication and secondary coding theories as the theoretical basis. It first analyzes the motivations for localization: the differences between China and South Korea in cultural systems, values, variety industry development, and audience characteristics. Then it deconstructs the specific localized innovation practices of *Who's the Murderer* from three dimensions: content, program form and communication channels, which make the program completely separate from the original version and form its own style. Finally, it summarizes the enlightenment for the development of Chinese variety shows, arguing that imported variety shows must be rooted in national conditions, conform to mainstream values, balance entertainment and social responsibility, and win by content innovation to achieve sustainable development.

# 2. Executive Summary
## 2.1. Background & Motivation
### Core Problem to Be Solved
The core problem addressed in this paper is the widespread "acclimatization" phenomenon of overseas imported variety shows in China: many imported programs have short life cycles, serious homogenization, content that does not conform to Chinese cultural policies and audience habits, and even have negative social impacts, making it difficult to achieve long-term sustainable development.
### Importance of the Problem
After 2010, imported variety shows once occupied half of the Chinese variety market, but with the introduction of regulatory policies restricting imported programs and encouraging original content, the extensive introduction mode is no longer sustainable. Exploring a mature localization transformation path is of great significance for the high-quality development of the Chinese variety industry, the protection of minors, and the spread of mainstream values.
### Research Gaps in Prior Studies
Existing studies on *Who's the Murderer* mostly focus on narrative structure, advertising implantation, program advantages and carnival characteristics, with only 4 low-impact journal papers involving localization-related content, lacking systematic in-depth analysis. Most localization studies on imported variety shows focus on old hit programs such as *Running Man China* and *Where Are We Going, Dad?*, and lack research on long-running niche reasoning variety IPs, as well as summary of replicable full-chain localization experience.
### Entry Point of the Paper
The paper takes *Who's the Murderer*, which broke the curse of "declining quality of variety sequels" (6 seasons with an average Douban score of 8.8+ and total broadcast volume exceeding 21 billion times) as a typical successful case, to explore a systematic localization path suitable for the Chinese environment.
## 2.2. Main Contributions / Findings
1.  It systematically sorts out the multi-dimensional drivers of localization of imported variety shows, pointing out that the differences in cultural systems, values, industry development status and audience needs between the importing country and the original country are unavoidable reasons for mandatory adaptation.
2.  It constructs a three-dimensional localization practice system for imported variety shows, taking *Who's the Murderer* as an example:
    - Content innovation: Theme selection integrates Chinese social hotspots and traditional cultural IP, plot design balances entertainment and legal popularization, and post-production adopts localized expression methods suitable for young Chinese audiences.
    - Form innovation: Star guest lineup adapts to the characteristics of China's fan economy, immersive real scene construction improves viewing experience, and full-chain IP derivative industry layout extends the program life cycle.
    - Communication channel innovation: Make full use of local social media platforms, encourage user secondary creation, and operate fan communities to improve audience stickiness.
3.  It puts forward actionable enlightenment for the development of Chinese variety shows: variety shows must be rooted in national conditions and conform to mainstream values, balance entertainment and social responsibility, build core competitiveness to avoid homogenization, and pay attention to the excavation of Chinese cultural elements and real-life themes.

# 3. Prerequisite Knowledge & Related Work
## 3.1. Foundational Concepts
This section provides detailed explanations of key concepts required to understand the paper for beginners:
- **Cross-cultural communication**: Refers to the communication and interaction between people from different cultural backgrounds, as well as the process of flow, sharing, penetration and migration of various cultural elements in the global society. When cultural products (such as variety shows) are transmitted across borders, differences in ideology, values, customs and habits will lead to acceptance barriers, so targeted adaptation is needed to eliminate these barriers.
- **Secondary coding theory**: Derived from Stuart Hall's encoding/decoding communication model:
  - *Encoding*: The process where the information sender converts the meaning to be transmitted into symbolic forms (text, images, sound, etc.).
  - *Decoding*: The process where the information receiver restores the received symbols to understandable information and meaning.
    In cross-cultural communication, the local producer of imported content will re-encode the original content according to the local cultural context and audience needs to make it suitable for local dissemination, this re-encoding process is called secondary coding.
- **Variety show localization**: The process of adjusting and transforming the content, form, and communication mode of imported overseas variety show IPs according to the local political, economic, cultural, policy environment and audience preferences, so that the program can be accepted by local audiences, avoid acclimatization, and achieve sustainable development.
- **Douban score**: A widely recognized film and television work scoring system in China. Users rate works on a 1-10 scale, and the final score is a weighted average of valid user ratings, which is considered a relatively objective reflection of the work's word-of-mouth among Chinese audiences.
- **IP (Intellectual Property) derivative development**: For a well-known content IP (such as a hit variety show), expand its commercial and cultural value by developing related products: spin-off variety shows, offline physical stores, interactive dramas, concerts, peripheral products, etc., to extend the IP life cycle.
## 3.2. Previous Works
The paper sorts out the existing research status as follows:
1.  **Studies on *Who's the Murderer***: Prior studies are mostly divided into four categories:
    - Narrative perspective: Analyze the suspense setting, narrative structure and narrative skills of the program.
    - Advertising implantation: Study the innovative marketing strategy of combining advertising content with program plot.
    - Program advantages: Analyze the program's value guidance function, communication mode characteristics and post-production advantages.
    - Carnival theory: Analyze the carnival characteristics of online variety represented by the program.
      However, only 4 low-academic journal papers involve localization-related content, lacking systematic in-depth analysis of the full-chain localization practice.
2.  **Studies on variety show localization**: Existing studies mostly focus on Western and South Korean imported variety shows such as *The Voice of China*, *Running Man China* and *Where Are We Going, Dad?*, analyzing their partial adaptation measures, but lack research on long-running niche variety IPs, and insufficient summary of replicable successful experience.
3.  **Foreign related studies**: Western scholars almost have no research on localization of imported variety shows, because their variety production relies more on self-production or introduction from the same language environment. Their related studies focus on network video technology, media convergence and video website operation.
## 3.3. Technological Evolution
The development of Chinese imported variety shows has gone through three main stages:
1.  **Stage 1 (before 2010): Imitation and reference stage**: Domestic variety shows mostly learn from the form of overseas programs without formal copyright purchase, such as *Super Girl* referencing *American Idol*.
2.  **Stage 2 (2010-2015): Copyright introduction boom stage**: A large number of South Korean and Western variety IPs are formally introduced, many hit shows appear, but there are also problems such as serious homogenization, and some program content does not conform to Chinese policies and values.
3.  **Stage 3 (2016-present): Localized transformation stage**: The National Radio and Television Administration issued policies to restrict the broadcast frequency and duration of imported variety shows, encourage original content and localized operation, so the industry shifted from direct introduction to joint research and development and systematic localized transformation. *Who's the Murderer* studied in this paper is a typical successful case of this stage.
## 3.4. Differentiation Analysis
Compared with previous related studies, the core innovations of this paper are:
1.  It is the first master's thesis systematically studying the localization of *Who's the Murderer*, filling the research gap of this high-quality variety IP.
2.  It integrates cross-cultural communication and secondary coding theory, constructs a complete analysis framework from localization motivation to practical measures to development enlightenment, rather than only focusing on partial adjustment measures.
3.  It expands the research scope from content and form adjustment to communication channel operation and derivative IP layout, summarizing the full-chain localization experience of imported variety shows.

# 4. Methodology
This paper is a humanities and social science case study, with no mathematical formulas involved. Its research logic is deconstructed as follows:
## 4.1. Principles
The core research principle is: Based on the reality that cross-cultural communication of cultural products will produce acceptance barriers due to cultural differences, imported variety shows must undergo secondary coding (localized transformation) to eliminate barriers and achieve effective dissemination in the local market. The paper takes the typical successful case of *Who's the Murderer* as the entry point, analyzes the drivers of localization, deconstructs specific adaptation measures, and summarizes generalizable experience for the industry.
## 4.2. Core Methodology In-depth
The paper uses two main research methods:
### 4.2.1. Case Study Method
Take *Who's the Murderer* (Chinese version) and its original South Korean version *Crime Scene* as specific research cases, conduct in-depth comparative analysis:
1.  **Data collection process**:
    - Collect and watch all episodes of 6 seasons of the Chinese version and 3 seasons of the South Korean version, sort out the differences in theme selection, plot setting, link design, guest performance, post-production, etc.
    - Collect relevant industry data: Douban scores, broadcast volume, audience feedback, program team interview materials, and national regulatory policy documents on variety shows from 2016 to 2021.
2.  **Analysis process**:
    - Compare the differences in the design concept and presentation form of the two versions, sort out the specific localization transformation measures of the Chinese version.
    - Analyze the adaptation effect of localization measures through the comparison of word-of-mouth, broadcast performance, social influence and life cycle between the two versions, as well as the comparison with other imported variety shows of Mango TV.
### 4.2.2. Text Analysis Method
Analyze multi-dimensional text materials related to the program:
1.  **Program content text**: Analyze the theme selection, plot setting, value orientation of the program, as well as the design of guest lineup, scene construction and derivative products.
2.  **Communication text**: Analyze the operation of official social media accounts, user secondary creation content on platforms such as Bilibili and Douyin, and fan discussion content on Douban groups.
    Through the analysis of multiple texts, summarize the characteristics and practical effects of the localization transformation of *Who's the Murderer*.
The overall research logic is linear: First, sort out the development status of the Chinese program and the original South Korean program → Second, analyze the multi-dimensional drivers of localization → Third, deconstruct the specific localization practice system from three dimensions → Fourth, summarize the enlightenment for the development of Chinese variety shows.

# 5. Experimental Setup
As a humanities and social science case study, this paper does not have traditional experimental datasets and metrics of natural science papers. Its research setup is as follows:
## 5.1. Research Samples
The core research samples of the paper are two programs:
1.  **South Korea JTBC *Crime Scene***: The original version, broadcast 3 seasons from 2014 to 2017, with a total of 35 episodes. Its Douban scores are 8.8, 9.4 and 9.0 respectively, the target audience is high-income and high-education groups in South Korea, the theme selection is mostly based on real criminal cases, and the overall style is serious, focusing on logical reasoning.
2.  **Mango TV *Who's the Murderer***: The localized Chinese version, broadcast 6 seasons from 2016 to 2020, with a total of 72 episodes. Its Douban scores are 9.3, 9.1, 9.1, 8.7, 8.4 and 9.0 respectively, with a total broadcast volume of more than 21 billion times, 93% of the audience is under 35 years old. The style combines reasoning, entertainment and legal popularization, with both commercial value and social value.
    Additional auxiliary research materials include: 1) Relevant industry regulatory policies issued by the National Radio and Television Administration from 2016 to 2021; 2) Audience feedback data: Douban group discussions, Weibo topic discussions, Bilibili secondary creation data; 3) Interview materials of the program's director, screenwriter and post-production team.
## 5.2. Evaluation Indicators
The paper uses three main indicators to evaluate the effect of localization:
1.  **Word-of-mouth indicator: Douban score**
    - Conceptual definition: Reflects the overall evaluation of the program by Chinese audiences, the higher the score, the better the word-of-mouth.
    - Calculation formula:
      \$
      S = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} w_i \times r_i}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} w_i}
      \$
      Where $S$ is the final Douban score, $r_i$ is the 1-10 rating submitted by user $i$, $w_i$ is the credibility weight of user $i$ (adjusted according to user activity, historical rating behavior, etc., to avoid malicious scoring), and $n$ is the total number of valid rating users.
2.  **Communication effect indicator: Total broadcast volume**
    - Conceptual definition: Reflects the coverage and communication scope of the program, the higher the broadcast volume, the wider the audience.
    - Calculation method: The total number of valid views of the program on the official broadcast platform Mango TV, an independent viewing of more than 5 minutes is counted as one valid play.
3.  **Audience loyalty indicator: Fan community size and topic discussion heat**
    - Conceptual definition: Reflects the stickiness of the audience to the program, the larger the community size and the higher the discussion heat, the more loyal the audience.
    - Measurement method: The number of members of the official Douban discussion group, the reading volume of Weibo super topics, and the number of secondary creation works on video platforms.
## 5.3. Comparative Baselines
1.  **Core baseline**: The original South Korean version *Crime Scene*, used to compare the differences between the two versions and analyze the adaptation effect of the Chinese version's localization measures.
2.  **Auxiliary baselines**: Other imported variety shows of Mango TV, such as *Where Are We Going, Dad?* and *I Am a Singer*, which were suspended after several seasons, used to compare the longevity and sustainability of different localization modes.

# 6. Results & Analysis
## 6.1. Core Results Analysis
### 6.1.1. Localization Motivation Verification
The paper confirms that the four-dimensional differences between China and South Korea are the core drivers for the localization of *Who's the Murderer*:
1.  **Cultural system differences**: South Korea's cultural industry started earlier, with a policy orientation focusing on cultural export, and the media environment is relatively loose; China's cultural industry focuses on meeting the domestic people's spiritual and cultural needs, media has the responsibility of public opinion guidance, and policy regulation is stricter, requiring variety shows to conform to mainstream values.
2.  **Value differences**: South Korean variety shows pay excessive attention to entertainment, and even have a large number of violent, pornographic and other content that does not conform to Chinese aesthetic habits; Chinese media needs to take into account social responsibility, and the audience prefers positive content that is close to social reality and has educational significance.
3.  **Variety industry development differences**: South Korea's variety industry is mature, with a perfect screenwriter training system, star idol system, and rich outdoor variety production experience; China's variety industry started late, the fan economy is developed, and the audience's demand for original content with Chinese cultural characteristics is strong.
4.  **Audience reception differences**: South Korea has a perfect program rating system, which allows for content for different age groups; China does not have a program rating system, so variety shows need to take into account the protection of minors, and avoid inappropriate content.
### 6.1.2. Localization Practice Effect Verification
The three-dimensional localization practice system of *Who's the Murderer* has achieved remarkable results:
1.  **Content localization innovation effect**:
    - Theme selection: The South Korean version is mostly based on real criminal cases, with a single modern theme; the Chinese version covers modern, ancient, science fiction, fairy tale and other themes, and integrates a large number of Chinese social hot topics (campus violence, online violence, child sexual assault, appearance anxiety, academic fraud, etc.), as well as Chinese cultural IP elements (such as *Nirvana in Fire*, *A Chinese Odyssey*, Tang Bohu's stories), which is highly accepted by Chinese audiences.
    - Plot features: The South Korean version is serious and focuses on reasoning, with few entertainment elements; the Chinese version adds dramatic reversal, funny guest interaction, and integrates legal popularization content (explaining legal provisions through guests and legal experts at the end of the program), combining entertainment and popularization of law, which not only meets the audience's entertainment needs, but also achieves positive social value guidance.
    - Picture symbol processing: The South Korean version's subtitles only play the role of clue prompts; the Chinese version's post-production adds interesting subtitles, emoticons, and sound effects combined with popular network terms, which is more in line with the preferences of young Chinese audiences.
2.  **Form localization innovation effect**:
    - Guest lineup: The South Korean version invites not only entertainers but also lawyers, criminal psychology experts, and police, focusing on professional reasoning ability; the Chinese version invites well-known stars with strong variety sense and fan base, forming fixed "veteran players" and "high-energy players", which adapts to the characteristics of China's fan economy, and the audience has a high sense of identification with the guest lineup.
    - Real scene construction: The South Korean version is all shot in indoor studio scenes; the Chinese version started to build large-scale real scenes from the third season, such as Rose Hotel, Paradise Island, cruise ships, and trains, using movie-level shooting techniques, which greatly enhances the audience's immersive viewing experience.
    - Derivative product layout: The South Korean version only has 3 seasons of main episodes, no derivative development; the Chinese version has developed a complete IP derivative chain: spin-off variety *Detective Academy*, interactive dramas *Head Suspect* and *Target Person*, annual *NZND* concert, offline script killing physical stores, etc., extending the IP life cycle and realizing the multi-dimensional value of the IP.
3.  **Communication channel localization innovation effect**:
    - Social media operation: The official Weibo account has 4.371 million fans as of November 2021, and the super topic has 760,000 followers, which effectively expands the program's communication scope through interaction with audiences and star guest forwarding.
    - Secondary creation encouragement: A large number of user secondary creation works on platforms such as Bilibili and Douyin further expand the program's influence and improve audience stickiness.
    - Fan community operation: The official Douban discussion group has 137,215 members as of November 2021, which collects audience feedback in time and adjusts program content according to audience suggestions.

## 6.3. Practice Effect Validation
The paper compares *Who's the Murderer* with other imported variety shows of Mango TV: *Where Are We Going, Dad?* was suspended after 5 seasons due to policy issues and excessive consumption of child stars; *I Am a Singer* was suspended after 8 seasons due to lack of innovation and declining word-of-mouth. This confirms that the three-dimensional localization practice system (content + form + communication) of *Who's the Murderer* is the key to its success, and the lack of any link will lead to the decline of the program.

# 7. Conclusion & Reflections
## 7.1. Conclusion Summary
The main conclusions of this paper are:
1.  Imported overseas variety shows must carry out systematic localization transformation to achieve sustainable development in China, and the adaptation must be based on the differences in national cultural systems, values, industry development and audience characteristics, rather than simple copying.
2.  The successful localization of *Who's the Murderer* provides a replicable path for the industry: carry out content innovation integrating local social hotspots and cultural elements, carry out form innovation adapting to local industry characteristics and audience preferences, and carry out communication channel innovation making full use of local social media and fan communities, while balancing entertainment and social responsibility, conforming to mainstream values.
3.  The long-term development of Chinese variety shows should not only rely on imported IP, but also focus on excavating Chinese cultural elements and real-life themes, create original IP with Chinese characteristics, tell Chinese stories well, and enhance cultural confidence.
## 7.2. Limitations & Future Work
### Limitations of the Paper
1.  The study only takes *Who's the Murderer*, a niche reasoning variety show, as a case, and the applicability of the conclusion to other types of variety shows (such as talent shows, love variety shows) needs to be further verified.
2.  The study focuses on the successful experience of localization, and does not conduct in-depth analysis of the existing problems in the localization process of *Who's the Murderer*, such as the controversy of individual guest selections, the decline of script quality in some episodes, and the homogenization competition with similar domestic programs in recent years.
### Future Research Directions
1.  Expand the research sample, include more types of imported and original variety shows, and improve the universality of the localization path conclusion.
2.  Explore the reverse cross-cultural dissemination path of Chinese original variety IPs, and study how to export Chinese high-quality variety products overseas.
## 7.3. Personal Insights & Critique
### Inspiration
This paper not only summarizes the experience of variety show localization, but also provides reference for the cross-cultural dissemination of other cultural products (such as film and television dramas, games). For example, when Chinese cultural products are exported overseas, they also need to carry out targeted localized transformation according to the local cultural environment and audience preferences to eliminate acceptance barriers.
### Potential Improvements
The paper can add quantitative analysis, such as analyzing the correlation between different localization measures and the program's broadcast volume/Douban score, to make the conclusion more convincing. In addition, it can compare *Who's the Murderer* with domestic original reasoning variety shows (such as *The Detectives' Adventures*) to further analyze the core competitive advantage of its localization mode.
Overall, this paper is a very practical industry-oriented study, which has important guiding significance for the production and operation of variety shows in China, and also provides a reference for the cross-cultural dissemination of cultural products in the context of globalization.
