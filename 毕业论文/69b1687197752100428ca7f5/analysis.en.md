## 1.5. Abstract
The research objective is to explore successful marketing paths for online variety shows targeting "Generation Z" (individuals born after 1995).
- **Core Methodology:** A qualitative case study analysis of the variety show `Who's the Murderer`.
- **Main Results:** The show's success is attributed to three main factors: (1) Precise marketing based on deep analysis of the internet ecosystem and audience pain points; (2) Differentiated marketing materials tailored to specific platforms (e.g., text on Weibo, video on Douyin); (3) Leveraging user-generated content and mainstream media reports as indirect marketing tools.
- **Key Conclusions:** Effective integration of official and non-official marketing channels, combined with positive social values (legal education), creates a sustainable model for online variety shows.

# 2. Executive Summary

## 2.1. Background & Motivation
- **Core Problem:** In the internet era, while many television variety shows collaborate with online video platforms for multi-platform broadcasting, few manage to remain popular over multiple seasons. Domestic online variety shows often suffer from **lack of innovation** and **insufficient long-term audience stickiness** (loyalty).
- **Importance:** "Generation Z" (born post-1995) has become the core audience and consumer group for variety shows. They have high sensitivity to the online ecosystem and represent the main force of the "fan economy." Understanding how to capture their attention is crucial for the survival of online variety shows.
- **Entry Point:** The paper selects `Who's the Murderer`, China's first online mystery-solving variety show, as a successful paradigm. It has broadcast nine seasons (as of May 2024) with over 100 million views per episode and Douban scores above 8.0. The study aims to deconstruct its marketing strategy to provide a reference for the industry.

## 2.2. Main Contributions / Findings
- **Primary Contributions:**
    1.  **Theoretical Application:** Applies **Integrated Marketing Communication (IMC)** theory and <strong>Converged Media (融媒体)</strong> marketing concepts to the specific context of Chinese online variety shows.
    2.  **Strategic Framework:** Identifies a three-pronged marketing path: Precise Audience Targeting, Platform-Differentiated Content, and Leveraging Third-Party (Non-Official) Propagation.
    3.  **Social Value Integration:** Highlights the importance of embedding positive social values (e.g., legal education) into entertainment content to gain mainstream media support and long-term viability.
- **Key Findings:**
    - **Audience:** Gen Z prefers "intelligence as charm" and interactive entertainment over pure education.
    - **Official Strategy:** Using Mango TV for ecosystem building, Weibo for text/community interaction, and Douyin for short video highlights.
    - **Non-Official Strategy:** Mainstream media reports (expanding to older demographics), Fan communities on Douban (scoring and discussion), and User-Generated Content (UGC) on Bilibili (secondary creation).

# 3. Prerequisite Knowledge & Related Work

## 3.1. Foundational Concepts
To understand this paper, readers need to be familiar with the following concepts:

- <strong>Generation Z (`Z世代`):</strong> Refers to the demographic cohort born between 1995 and 2009. In the context of this paper, they are characterized as digital natives who are highly sensitive to the internet ecosystem, prefer interactive and intelligent entertainment, and are the core consumers of the "fan economy."
- **Integrated Marketing Communication (IMC):** A strategic approach where a company coordinates various promotional tools (advertising, public relations, social media, etc.) to deliver a consistent, clear, and compelling message about the organization and its products. The goal is to influence consumer behavior through unified communication.
- <strong>Converged Media (`融媒体`):</strong> Refers to the integration of traditional media (TV, newspapers) and new media (internet, mobile apps) to create a multi-platform content distribution system. It emphasizes resource sharing and content compatibility across different channels.
- **User-Generated Content (UGC):** Content (text, video, images) created by users rather than the official brand. In this paper, it refers to fans creating videos, discussions, or reviews about the show on platforms like Bilibili and Douban.
- **Cross-Media Storytelling:** A concept by Henry Jenkins where a story unfolds across multiple media platforms, with each medium making a unique contribution to the whole. In this paper, it relates to how the variety show extends its narrative through apps, social media, and offline experiences.

## 3.2. Previous Works
The author references several theoretical foundations and prior studies:
- **Integrated Marketing Communication Theory:** Originated in the US in the 1980s. The paper cites **Don Schultz (1991)**, who defined IMC as a process to manage all product and service information resources to highlight the status of consumers and maintain loyalty.
- **Cross-Media Narratives:** Cites **Henry Jenkins**, who described it as a process where each media makes a unique contribution to the unfolding story (e.g., games, TV, tourism).
- **Domestic Studies:** The paper references recent Chinese studies on `Who's the Murderer` regarding innovation (Liu Yanghongyun, 2019), sustainable development of "Variety Show N-Generations" (Xi Luning, 2021), and legal education practices (Fu Ruofei, 2024). These works establish that the show is a well-researched subject in media studies, but this paper focuses specifically on the *marketing path*.

## 3.3. Technological Evolution
- **Media Shift:** The field has evolved from traditional TV broadcasting to internet-based streaming. Variety shows now rely on **Video-on-Demand (VOD)** platforms like Mango TV.
- **Marketing Evolution:** Marketing has shifted from single-channel advertising (TV commercials) to **multi-channel digital marketing** (social media, short videos, algorithms).
- **Position of this Paper:** This work sits at the intersection of media studies and marketing management. It does not propose a new technological algorithm but applies existing marketing theories to a specific cultural product in the current digital landscape (2024-2025 context).

## 3.4. Differentiation Analysis
- **Vs. General Marketing Studies:** Unlike general IMC studies that focus on commercial products, this paper focuses on **cultural content** (variety shows) which has both economic and social (ideological) value.
- **Vs. Previous `Who's the Murderer` Studies:** Previous studies often focus on content innovation or narrative structure. This paper differentiates itself by focusing strictly on the **marketing communication path**, dividing it into "Official" and "Non-Official" strategies, and emphasizing the role of **legal education** as a marketing asset (gaining mainstream media support).

# 4. Methodology

## 4.1. Principles
The core methodology of this paper is a **Qualitative Case Study**.
- **Theoretical Basis:** The study is grounded in **Integrated Marketing Communication (IMC)** theory and **Converged Media** theory.
- **Intuition:** The author posits that the longevity and success of `Who's the Murderer` are not accidental but the result of a systematic marketing strategy that aligns with the habits of Gen Z and leverages the strengths of different media platforms.
- **Research Logic:**
    1.  **Contextualize:** Define the target audience (Gen Z) and the media environment.
    2.  **Deconstruct:** Break down the marketing activities into "Official" (producer-led) and "Non-Official" (user/media-led).
    3.  **Analyze:** Evaluate the effectiveness of each channel using qualitative data (engagement, media coverage) and quantitative indicators (view counts, scores).

## 4.2. Core Methodology In-depth (Layer by Layer)
Since this is a social science paper, there are no mathematical algorithms or complex formulas. However, the analytical framework can be described as a structured evaluation process.

### 4.2.1. Case Selection
The author selects `Who's the Murderer` based on specific criteria:
- **Representativeness:** It is China's first online mystery-solving variety show.
- **Longevity:** It has run for nine seasons (as of 2024), overcoming the typical "decline after Season 1" curse.
- **Performance:** High viewership (100 million+ per episode) and high reputation (Douban score > 8.0).

### 4.2.2. Data Collection Strategy
The author gathers data from multiple dimensions to validate the marketing effectiveness:
- **Platform Data:** Follower counts, interaction counts (likes, comments, shares) on Weibo, Douyin, and Mango TV.
- **Reputation Data:** Douban scores and review volumes.
- **Media Coverage:** Mentions in mainstream state media (e.g., `People's Daily`, `China.org.cn`).
- **Content Analysis:** Examining the type of content released on each platform (e.g., text vs. video).

### 4.2.3. Analytical Framework
The analysis follows a bifurcated structure:
1.  **Official Marketing Path:**
    - **Platform:** Mango TV (Ecosystem), Weibo (Community), Douyin (Traffic).
    - **Strategy:** Differentiation (tailoring content to platform norms).
2.  **Non-Official Marketing Path:**
    - **Source:** Mainstream Media, Fan Communities, UGC Creators.
    - **Strategy:** Endorsement and Organic Spread.

## 5.2. Evaluation Metrics
The paper uses several key metrics to measure marketing success. While no formulas are provided in the text, standard definitions apply:

- <strong>Viewership (播放量):</strong> The total number of times the video content has been played.
    - *Definition:* A count of play events.
    - *Significance:* Indicates reach and initial interest.
    - *Paper Data:* Each episode exceeds 100 million views.
- <strong>Douban Score (豆瓣评分):</strong> A weighted average rating from users on the Douban platform.
    - *Definition:* Users rate on a scale of 1 to 10 stars. The system calculates a comprehensive score.
    - *Significance:* Indicates quality perception and audience satisfaction.
    - *Paper Data:* All seasons score above 8.0.
- <strong>Social Media Engagement (转、赞、评):</strong> Refers to <strong>Reposts (转)</strong>, <strong>Likes (赞)</strong>, and <strong>Comments (评)</strong>.
    - *Definition:* Count of user interactions on a specific post.
    - *Significance:* Indicates audience stickiness and willingness to propagate content.
    - *Paper Data:* Weibo official account has 61.337 million cumulative interactions.
- <strong>Hot Search Count (热搜次数):</strong> The number of times a topic appears on the platform's trending list.
    - *Definition:* Frequency of appearing in the "Trending Topics" list based on real-time discussion volume.
    - *Significance:* Indicates public attention and viral potential.
    - *Paper Data:* Over 5,500 times on Weibo hot searches across 9 seasons.

# 6. Results & Analysis

## 6.1. Core Results Analysis
The analysis reveals that the show's success is driven by a combination of precise targeting, platform differentiation, and leveraging external endorsements.

### 6.1.1. Official Marketing Strategies
- **Mango TV Ecosystem:**
    - **Strategy:** Active push notifications and dedicated columns on the app homepage.
    - **Result:** High visibility for app users; the show becomes "electronic pickled vegetables" (a slang term for content consumed while eating, implying high frequency/habitual viewing).
    - **Innovation:** **Time-Difference Release.** The episode is split into three parts (Wednesday Teaser, Thursday Part 1, Friday Part 2). This extends the discussion period and gives the audience time to solve the mystery themselves, enhancing participation.
- **Weibo (Text & Community):**
    - **Strategy:** Use of text and images for detailed information (character timelines, evidence) which video cannot fully convey.
    - **Result:** 4.643 million followers; 61.337 million interactions.
    - **Function:** Serves as the official information hub and a place for fan clustering (Super Topics).
- **Douyin (Short Video):**
    - **Strategy:** Vertical video format. Content includes teasers, character looks, and "meme" clips (e.g., funny moments).
    - **Result:** 2.894 million followers; 93.262 million interactions.
    - **Function:** Attracts non-sticky users through humor and highlights, converting them into viewers.

### 6.1.2. Non-Official Marketing Strategies
- **Mainstream Media:**
    - **Strategy:** Coverage by state media like `People's Daily` due to the show's legal education value.
    - **Result:** Expands audience to older demographics (60+ via newspapers, 40-60 via government websites) who trust official sources.
    - **Significance:** Legitimizes the entertainment product as a tool for social good.
- **Fan Communities (Douban):**
    - **Strategy:** Organic discussion and scoring.
    - **Result:** 855,957 raters; consistent score > 8.0.
    - **Significance:** High scores act as a quality signal for new users.
- **UGC (Bilibili):**
    - **Strategy:** Fans create secondary content (edits, explanations, legal analysis).
    - **Result:** Free promotion and extended content lifecycle.
    - **Significance:** "Civilian partners" in legal education enhance the show's educational impact.

## 6.3. Ablation Studies / Parameter Analysis
As a qualitative case study, there are no ablation studies. However, the author implicitly analyzes the **necessity of each component**:
- **Without Legal Education:** The show would likely not receive mainstream media endorsement, limiting its social impact and longevity.
- **Without Platform Differentiation:** Posting the same video on Weibo and Douyin would cause audience fatigue (as noted in Section 1.2).
- **Without Fan Engagement:** The "stickiness" would decrease, as fans provide free promotion through reposts and UGC.

# 7. Conclusion & Reflections

## 7.1. Conclusion Summary
The paper concludes that `Who's the Murderer` succeeded due to three core innovations:
1.  **Core Values:** Aligning with socialist core values and legal education ensures official recognition and mainstream media support.
2.  **Media Strategy:** Utilizing internet platforms for low-cost, high-efficiency marketing with differentiated content for each platform (Weibo for text, Douyin for video).
3.  **Fan Economy:** Leveraging fans for free promotion (reposts, UGC) and extending marketing to offline products (script murder games, merchandise).

## 7.2. Limitations & Future Work
- **Limitations:**
    - **Single Case Study:** The findings are based on one show. While successful, the strategies might not be directly transferable to all variety show genres (e.g., music or outdoor reality shows).
    - **Qualitative Nature:** The reliance on reported metrics (e.g., "over 100 million views") lacks granular data analysis (e.g., conversion rates, demographic breakdowns beyond Gen Z).
    - **Time Sensitivity:** Social media platforms evolve rapidly. Strategies effective in 2024 (e.g., specific Douyin algorithms) may change.
- **Future Work:**
    - Comparative studies with other long-running variety shows.
    - Quantitative analysis of the correlation between specific marketing actions (e.g., a hot search event) and viewership spikes.
    - Investigation into the long-term impact of the "legal education" aspect on audience behavior beyond viewership.

## 7.3. Personal Insights & Critique
- **Strengths:** The paper provides a clear, structured breakdown of marketing channels. The distinction between "Official" and "Non-Official" is particularly insightful, recognizing that in the internet age, **users are part of the marketing team**. The emphasis on **social responsibility** (legal education) as a marketing asset is a valuable insight for content creators in regulated markets.
- **Weaknesses:** The paper is somewhat descriptive. It lists *what* was done but offers less depth on *how* decisions were made (e.g., the decision-making process behind splitting the episode into three parts). It also lacks a critical view on potential negatives (e.g., audience fatigue from over-marketing).
- **Transferability:** The concept of **"Differentiated Platform Content"** is highly transferable to other digital products. The idea of **"Embedding Social Value"** is crucial for any content product seeking longevity and government support in China.
- **Critical Reflection:** The paper assumes that "Gen Z" is a monolith. In reality, Gen Z preferences are diverse. Future research could explore sub-segments within Gen Z. Additionally, the reliance on fan labor (free marketing) raises ethical questions about the exploitation of fan affection, which is not addressed in the paper.
