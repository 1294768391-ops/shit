# 1. Bibliographic Information
## 1.1. Title
The core topic of the paper is *Research on the Reasoning Reality Show Who's the Murderer (Star Detective) from the Perspective of the "5W" Communication Model*. It systematically analyzes the success factors of the hit Chinese variety show *Who's the Murderer* (abbreviated as WTM, also translated as *Star Detective*) using the classic 5W communication framework, and summarizes actionable inspirations for the development of China's reality show industry.
## 1.2. Authors
- First author: Cao Qiumin, Master's student in Drama and Film Studies (research direction: Broadcasting and Hosting Art) at Harbin Normal University.
- Supervisor: Prof. Gong Zhaomin, Harbin Normal University.
## 1.3. Publication Venue
This is an officially published 2020 Master's thesis from Harbin Normal University, a leading higher education institution in northeast China for arts and media research. It is a representative academic work in the field of Chinese variety show communication research.
## 1.4. Publication Year
2020
## 1.5. Abstract
Against the background of widespread plagiarism, homogeneity and declining reputation of domestic reality shows in China, *Who's the Murderer* (the first domestic reasoning reality show) maintained high ratings (total 14.78 billion views) and high audience reputation (average Douban score of ~9/10 across 5 seasons) after its launch in 2016, and even outperformed its original Korean version *Crime Scene*. This paper takes WTM as the research object, uses Laswell's 5W communication model as the theoretical basis, adopts a combination of theoretical discussion and case analysis, and analyzes the five core communication elements of the show: communication subject, communication content, communication medium, communication object and communication effect. The study concludes that the success of WTM provides four key inspirations for China's reality show industry: (1) achieve localization innovation to surpass imported original models; (2) build unique intellectual property (IP) to avoid homogeneous competition; (3) integrate brand features with program content to create a native advertising model; (4) build correct value orientation to highlight social and cultural value.

# 2. Executive Summary
## 2.1. Background & Motivation
### 2.1.1. Core Problem to Be Solved
Before 2020, China's reality show industry faced three major pain points:
1.  Severe plagiarism and homogenization: Most shows directly copied foreign program models without localization adaptation, leading to audience aesthetic fatigue.
2.  "Seasonal decline curse": Most seasonal reality shows saw continuous drop in ratings and reputation as subsequent seasons were released.
3.  Lack of social responsibility: Many shows prioritized traffic and sensational content over positive value guidance, especially for young audiences.
    *Who's the Murderer* broke all three of these trends, so the paper aims to analyze its success mechanism to provide reference for the entire industry.
### 2.1.2. Research Gap
Prior research on reasoning reality shows was very limited:
- Foreign studies focused on general impact of reality shows, with no systematic research on reasoning reality shows as a separate category.
- Domestic research only had one master's thesis on overall reasoning variety shows, and most studies on *Who's the Murderer* only analyzed single aspects of the show (e.g., narrative structure, audio-visual language), with no comprehensive, multi-dimensional analysis from a communication perspective.
### 2.1.3. Innovative Entry Point
The paper is the first academic work to apply Laswell's classic 5W communication model to conduct a full, systematic analysis of *Who's the Murderer* across all stages of its production, dissemination and reception.
## 2.2. Main Contributions / Findings
1.  **Theoretical contribution**: It clarifies the definition and development history of reasoning reality shows as an independent program category, and verifies the applicability of the 5W model for analyzing new media variety shows.
2.  **Practical contribution**: It summarizes four replicable successful experiences for China's reality show industry:
    - Localized innovation can help imported program models surpass the original version and break the "imported shows are always better" stereotype.
    - Building a unique, multi-dimensional IP system can effectively avoid homogeneous competition and improve audience loyalty.
    - Native advertising that integrates naturally with program content can achieve a win-win situation for the show platform, advertisers and audiences.
    - Integrating positive value guidance into entertainment content can improve the social reputation of reality shows while maintaining high ratings.

# 3. Prerequisite Knowledge & Related Work
## 3.1. Foundational Concepts
All key concepts required to understand the paper are explained below for beginners:
### 3.1.1. 5W Communication Model
Proposed by American communication scholar Harold Laswell in 1948 in his paper *The Structure and Function of Communication in Society*, this is one of the most classic foundational models in communication studies. It breaks down any complete communication process into five interconnected core elements, in the order of transmission:
1.  **Who (Communication Subject)**: The sender of information, responsible for collecting, processing and distributing content.
2.  **Says What (Communication Content)**: The information transmitted in the communication process, including text, audio, video, values etc.
3.  **In Which Channel (Communication Medium)**: The carrier used to transmit information from the subject to the object.
4.  **To Whom (Communication Object / Audience)**: The receiver of the information, who will react to the received content.
5.  **With What Effect (Communication Effect)**: The change in the audience's cognition, attitude and behavior caused by the received information.
    These five elements correspond to the five core research fields of communication: control analysis, content analysis, media analysis, audience analysis and effect analysis.
### 3.1.2. Reasoning Reality Show
As defined in the paper: A type of reality show where voluntary participants are placed in a specific reasoning context, follow pre-set rules, analyze known clues to complete a preset competitive goal (e.g., find a hidden "murderer"), and the whole process is recorded, edited and broadcast to audiences. It combines the logic of reasoning games with the authenticity and entertainment of reality shows.
### 3.1.3. Key Contextual Definitions (For Non-Chinese Audiences)
- **Mango TV**: A leading Chinese online video platform owned by Hunan Broadcasting System, famous for producing original variety shows. It uses an exclusive broadcast model for its self-produced content.
- **Douban Score**: A 1-10 user rating system on Douban (China's most influential film, television and book review platform), widely recognized as an objective indicator of audience reputation for media content.
- **IP (Intellectual Property)**: In the context of Chinese variety shows, refers to a well-known program brand that can be expanded to derivatives, spin-off shows, offline activities and other commercial forms.
- **Native Advertising**: A form of advertising that blends naturally with the program content, rather than appearing as intrusive, separate ad breaks.
## 3.2. Previous Works
The paper cites the following key prior studies:
1.  Foreign general reality show research: *Reality TV: Realism and Revelation* by Anita Biressi and Heather Nunn, which discusses the social and cultural impact of reality shows, but does not involve reasoning reality shows as a separate category.
2.  Domestic reasoning variety show research: *Research on Chinese Reasoning Variety Shows* (2017, Master's thesis by Liu Jianping), the only prior systematic study on the category, which analyzes the development history and existing problems of reasoning variety shows in China, but does not conduct in-depth case analysis of *Who's the Murderer*.
3.  Single-aspect WTM research: Prior studies focused on:
    - Narrative structure (e.g., Li Dan's *Narrative Research on the Reasoning Game Reality Show Who's the Murderer*, 2019)
    - Audio-visual language (e.g., Hu You's *Research on the Function of Audio-visual Language in Who's the Murderer*, 2018)
    - Audience identity (e.g., Pan Ziling and Dai Chengcheng's *Audience Identity Analysis of Season 4 of Who's the Murderer*, 2019)
      None of these studies conduct a comprehensive, multi-dimensional analysis of the show's entire communication process.
## 3.3. Technological Evolution
The development of reasoning reality shows follows three stages:
1.  **Global reality show emergence (1999 onwards)**: The first reality show *Big Brother* launched in the Netherlands in 1999, and the format spread globally, gradually diversifying into different subgenres.
2.  **Foreign reasoning reality show maturity (2010 onwards)**: In regions with strong detective fiction culture (Europe, Japan, South Korea), reasoning reality shows matured, with representative works including South Korea's *Crime Scene* (2014), the original version of WTM.
3.  **Chinese reasoning reality show emergence (2011-2016)**: Early prototypes of reasoning shows appeared on Chinese TV (e.g., Jiangsu TV's *Very Fantastic*, 2011), but the first formal, large-scale reasoning reality show was *Who's the Murderer*, launched by Mango TV in 2016.
    This paper's research on WTM is the first comprehensive analysis of this emerging program category in China.
## 3.4. Differentiation Analysis
Compared to prior related works, the core innovations of this paper are:
1.  **Comprehensive perspective**: It is the first study to analyze WTM from all five dimensions of the 5W model, rather than focusing on a single aspect of the show.
2.  **Practical applicability**: It summarizes replicable experiences that can be directly applied to the production and operation of other reality shows in China, rather than just conducting theoretical analysis.
3.  **Systematic sample selection**: It takes all 60 episodes of the first 5 seasons of WTM as the research sample, ensuring the conclusions are generalizable, rather than only analyzing a small number of representative episodes.

# 4. Methodology
The paper uses a combination of three research methods, centered on the 5W communication model analysis:
## 4.1. Core Method: 5W Communication Model Analysis
### 4.1.1. Principles
The 5W model breaks down the entire communication lifecycle of *Who's the Murderer* (from production to reception) into five interconnected elements, allowing for systematic, multi-dimensional analysis of the show's success factors. The analysis of each element is detailed below:
---
#### 4.1.1.1. Analysis of Communication Subject (Who)
The communication subject of WTM consists of four complementary parts:
1.  **Editing and production team**:
    - Each 100-minute episode takes an average of 3 months to produce, with strict polishing of three aspects:
      1.  **Script polishing**: The writing team includes professional screenwriters, detective fiction writers, reasoning enthusiasts and even fans of the show. Each script is revised hundreds of times before recording, and integrates social hotspot topics to ensure logical rigor and positive value orientation. For example, the famous episode *Horror Nursery Rhyme* (Season 2, Episode 7) was revised more than 200 times over 4 months.
      2.  **Logic polishing**: The team consults professional experts (forensics, police, psychologists) to fix logical loopholes, and organizes at least two test play sessions (paper version and real-scene version) with high-level reasoning game players to find gaps before formal recording.
      3.  **Detail polishing**: The team aligns the in-show crime time with the actual broadcast date to increase audience immersion, designs character names that fit the guests' real names and character traits, and integrates guests' personal characteristics (e.g., actor Bai Jingting's well-known love for sneakers) into the plot to create entertainment effects.
2.  **Star players**:
    Each star player has dual roles:
    1.  **Game role**: Either a detective (leads the investigation), suspect (needs to prove innocence and help find the murderer), or the hidden real murderer (can lie to hide identity).
    2.  **Script role**: A character with a specific background and story in each episode's case.
        The casting follows three principles: love for reasoning content, certain acting skills for role-playing, and good expression skills for discussion. The cast is divided into:
    - **Permanent cast**: 5 core guests with clear, audience-favorite personas: He Jiong (smart, high EQ, logical), Sa Beining (legal expert, funny, "狗头侦探/ Goutou Detective" meme for frequent wrong votes), Wang Ou (highly intuitive, "Goddess of Intuition"), Bai Jingting (good at solving密室/secret room puzzles, "eternal scapegoat"), and Gui Gui (good at finding evidence, "evidence search dog").

    - **Flight cast**: Guests invited for specific episodes, selected to fit the episode theme (e.g., actors who have starred in detective films), increase entertainment value, or bring in new fan groups.
3.  **Amateur guests (added from Season 4)**:
    1.  **Detective assistants**: Top university students selected via competition, who help the detective search for evidence and analyze the case, and later starred in the spin-off show *Detective Academy*.
    2.  **Expert consultants**: Professionals from relevant fields (lawyers, psychologists, sociologists, etc.) who appear at the end of each episode to popularize legal and social knowledge related to the case, and provide correct value guidance.
4.  **Multi-layer host system**:
    Unlike traditional shows with a single fixed host, WTM uses three complementary host roles:
    1.  **Detective host**: The player who draws the detective role in each episode is responsible for guiding the program flow, organizing discussions and controlling rhythm.
    2.  **Hidden hosts**: Permanent cast members He Jiong and Sa Beining (both professional hosts) control the field and guide the discussion direction even when they are not playing the detective role, to ensure the program proceeds smoothly.
    3.  **Voice-over host**: An off-screen narrator who announces game rules, countdowns, and voting results to increase the sense of ritual and clarify the process.

#### 4.1.1.2. Analysis of Communication Content (Says What)
The communication content of WTM is designed to balance logical reasoning, entertainment and positive value guidance, with four core components:
1.  **Positive program themes**:
    - **Tie to social hotspots**: Almost every episode focuses on real social issues, including campus bullying, online violence, domestic violence, workplace harassment, waste classification, depression, etc., to guide audiences to think about these issues.
    - **Pay homage to classic works**: Many episodes adapt elements from classic films, TV series, novels and fairy tales (e.g., *Titanic*, *Malice* by Keigo Higashino, *Snow White*), which reduces the audience's acceptance threshold and improves the cultural value of the show.
2.  **Suspenseful plot setting**:
    The plot follows three core principles of suspense design:
    1.  **Suspension**: Multiple layers of questions are set from the start of the episode (Who is the murderer? What is the motive? What is the hidden story?) and are only revealed at the end, to maintain the audience's attention.
    2.  **Surprise**: The plot uses frequent reversals, unexpected events (e.g., the murderer fakes their own death, multiple timelines overlap, soul exchange) and fantasy elements to constantly surprise the audience.
    3.  **Satisfaction**: The final reveal of the truth releases the audience's accumulated tension, bringing a strong sense of psychological satisfaction.
3.  **Immersive stage setting**:
    The set is designed to let both players and audiences feel fully immersed in the case:
    1.  **Realistic scenes**: The show uses a closed "sandbox" set layout, with separate spaces for the crime scene, each suspect's private room, discussion room and voting room. From Season 3 onwards, the team built large real scenes for some episodes, including a 4000-square-meter hotel, an isolated island art museum, and a full luxury cruise ship.
    2.  **Detailed props**: Each episode has more than 3000 props, divided into narrative props (to show the character's personality and background) and clue props (to advance the case reasoning). All props are highly realistic and consistent with the case background.
    3.  **Character-specific styling**: Each guest's costume, makeup and accessories are specially designed to fit their character in the episode, helping guests enter the role quickly and increasing audience immersion. 4.  **Unique audio-visual effects**:
    - **Cinematic shooting and editing**: More than 20 cameras are used to record every movement and reaction of the guests. #### 4.1.1.3. Analysis of Communication Medium (In Which Channel)
WTM uses an omni-channel online + offline communication matrix to maximize reach and audience stickiness:
1.  **Mango TV exclusive broadcast advantage**:
    As a self-produced show of Mango TV, it gets multi-position recommendations on the platform's homepage, high-frequency information pushes before each episode's release, and entertainment-style promotional content that fits the show's tone, to attract audiences.
2.  **Online communication matrix**:
    - **Social media platforms**: Official accounts on Weibo (release program updates, create hot topics, interact with fans) and WeChat public account (release derivative content, sell peripheral products).
    - **Information exchange platforms**: The production team interacts with audiences on Zhihu and Douban forums, collects opinions, and initiates discussions on case reasoning.
    - **Opinion leader promotion**: Star guests promote the show on their personal social media accounts, and promote it on other variety shows they participate in, to bring in fan groups.
3.  **Offline promotion activities**:
    - Season launch press conferences with cast attendance to build audience expectation.
    - Offline evidence search experiences: Real scene replicas of cases are set up in shopping malls in first-tier cities, allowing fans to experience solving cases in person. The following figure shows the scene of an offline evidence search activity:

#### 4.1.1.4. Analysis of Communication Object (To Whom)
The show is targeted at young audiences, with a clear audience structure and demand orientation:
1.  **Audience group composition**:
    - **Age structure**: 80% of the audience is aged 20-29, the "net generation" group that grew up with the internet, and is familiar with online culture and reasoning games.
    - **Occupational characteristics**: The main audience groups are students (active in online interactions) and high-income, high-education groups (attracted by the show's social value and logical reasoning content).
    - **Audience type**: Divided into three categories: reasoning literature lovers (focus on case logic), reasoning game players (focus on game design and players' performance), and celebrity fans (focus on their favorite guests).
2.  **Audience demand analysis**:
    Based on the Uses and Gratifications theory (which states that audiences actively choose media content to meet their own needs), the show meets three core needs of audiences:
    1.  **Entertainment demand**: The show provides stress relief through the contrast between stars' on-screen personas and their real performance in the show, the fun of solving reasoning puzzles, and funny post-production effects. #### 4.1.1.5. Analysis of Communication Effect (With What Effect)
The show achieves excellent results at all three levels of communication effect:
1.  **Cognitive level**: Audiences gain legal knowledge, common sense of social issues, and learn correct solutions to problems such as campus bullying, online fraud and domestic violence.
2.  **Psychological and attitude level**: Audiences accept the positive values transmitted by the show, and produce strong emotional resonance with the social issues discussed.
3.  **Behavioral level**: The show has high and increasing view counts, audiences actively participate in online and offline interactions, and purchase related products (Mango TV memberships, peripheral products, advertised products).

## 5.2. Evaluation Metrics
The paper uses four widely recognized indicators to evaluate the show's performance:
### 5.2.1. Total Play Count
- **Conceptual Definition**: The total number of views of the show on the Mango TV platform, which directly measures the popularity and audience reach of the show.
- **Calculation**: Sum of the number of views of all episodes of all seasons.
- **Symbol Explanation**: No complex formula, this is directly counted by the platform.
### 5.2.2. Douban Score
- **Conceptual Definition**: An average rating from 1 to 10 given by Douban users, which objectively reflects the audience's reputation and satisfaction with the show.
- **Formula**:
  \$
  \text{Douban Score} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{N} R_i}{N}
  \$
- **Symbol Explanation**: $R_i$ = the 1-10 rating given by user $i$, $N$ = total number of users who rated the show.
### 5.2.3. Weibo Super Topic Reading Volume
- **Conceptual Definition**: The total number of views of content related to the show's official super topic on Weibo, which measures the social discussion heat and audience activity of the show.
- **Calculation**: Directly counted by Weibo's official background.
### 5.2.4. Audience Comment Sentiment
- **Conceptual Definition**: The tendency of audience comments on social media and review platforms to be positive, neutral or negative, which measures the audience's recognition of the show's content and values.
- **Evaluation method**: The paper conducts qualitative analysis of representative high-frequency comments to judge sentiment tendency.
## 5.3. Baselines
The paper uses two baselines for comparison:
1.  **Original Korean show *Crime Scene***: To compare the localization innovation of WTM and verify that it surpasses the original version in the Chinese market.
2.  **Other domestic seasonal reality shows**: Most domestic seasonal reality shows see a continuous decline in Douban scores and play counts as seasons increase. Comparing WTM to this baseline highlights its ability to maintain high reputation and popularity across multiple seasons.

# 6. Results & Analysis
## 6.1. Core Results Analysis
The core performance results of *Who's the Murderer* are:

| Indicator | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Total play count (Seasons 1-5) | 14.78 billion |
| Average Douban score | 9.1/10 |
| Weibo super topic reading volume | 97.5 billion |
| Performance compared to Korean original *Crime Scene* | Higher reputation and view count in the Chinese market |

These results prove that WTM has achieved both high popularity and high reputation, breaking the three major pain points of China's reality show industry mentioned earlier.
The 5W analysis results show that the success of WTM comes from the coordinated optimization of all five communication elements:
1.  The multi-layer communication subject (editing team, star cast, amateur guests, host system) ensures both the quality of the content and its entertainment value.
2.  The communication content balances logical reasoning, entertainment and positive value guidance, meeting the diversified needs of audiences.
3.  The omni-channel communication matrix maximizes audience reach and improves user stickiness.
4.  The clear audience positioning and demand-oriented content design ensure high audience loyalty.
5.  The three levels of communication effects (cognitive, attitudinal, behavioral) are all fully achieved, forming a positive cycle of word-of-mouth communication.

## 6.3. Practical Inspiration Results
Based on the analysis, the paper summarizes four replicable inspirations for China's reality show industry:
1.  **Innovation breaks the import curse**: Localized adaptation of imported program models based on domestic audience preferences can achieve better results than direct copying, and even surpass the original version.
2.  **Unique IP building avoids homogeneity**: Building a unique IP system through precise positioning, original non-replicable content, multi-dimensional interaction and derivative shows can effectively avoid homogeneous competition and improve audience loyalty.
3.  **Native advertising creates a new marketing model**: Integrating advertising content naturally into the program (native oral broadcast, native prop implantation, native themed video ads) can avoid audience resentment and achieve a win-win situation for the platform, advertisers and audiences.
4.  **Value guidance reflects social responsibility**: Integrating positive value guidance into entertainment content, and extending value guidance outside the program through topic discussion and offline activities, can improve the social reputation of reality shows while maintaining high ratings. # 7. Conclusion & Reflections
## 7.1. Conclusion Summary
This paper is the first comprehensive, systematic study of the hit reasoning reality show *Who's the Murderer* using the 5W communication model. It finds that the show's success comes from the coordinated optimization of all five core communication elements: a professional and dedicated production team, high-quality content that balances reasoning, entertainment and positive value, omni-channel online and offline promotion, clear positioning for young audiences, and excellent communication effects at cognitive, attitudinal and behavioral levels. The paper's conclusions fill the research gap in comprehensive reasoning reality show analysis, and provide actionable, replicable references for the development of China's reality show industry, especially in terms of localized innovation, IP building, advertising model innovation and social responsibility performance.
## 7.2. Limitations & Future Work
The authors point out the following limitations of the study:
1.  The study only covers the first 5 seasons of *Who's the Murderer* (up to 2020), and does not include analysis of later seasons (Season 6 onwards, released after 2020).
2.  The study focuses on the successful experience of the show, and does not conduct in-depth analysis of its existing problems, such as occasional logical loopholes in scripts, uneven performance of flight guests, and gradual homogenization of some plot designs in later seasons.
3.  The study does not conduct comparative analysis with other domestic reasoning variety shows that emerged after *Who's the Murderer* (e.g., *The Truth*, *Start Reasoning*), to verify the generalizability of the conclusions.
    Future research directions suggested by the paper include:
- Expanding the sample to include later seasons of WTM and other reasoning variety shows to improve the generalizability of conclusions.
- Adding quantitative research methods (e.g., big data sentiment analysis of audience comments, questionnaire surveys) to further verify the communication effect conclusions.
- Conducting in-depth interviews with the production team and audiences to obtain first-hand data and support more in-depth analysis.
## 7.3. Personal Insights & Critique
This paper is a highly practical, well-structured case study that has strong reference value for both academic research and industry practice:
1.  The application of the 5W model to variety show analysis is very appropriate, as it covers the entire lifecycle of the show from production to reception, avoiding the one-sidedness of previous studies that only focused on single aspects of the show.
2.  The conclusion that reality shows need to balance entertainment, content quality and social responsibility is very enlightening for the entire Chinese entertainment industry, which has long had a problem of prioritizing traffic over content and value.
3.  The native advertising model summarized in the paper is a very good solution to the long-standing contradiction between advertising revenue and audience experience in variety shows.
    Potential areas for improvement of the paper:
- Adding quantitative analysis of audience comments (e.g., using natural language processing to analyze the sentiment of a large number of comments) would make the analysis of communication effects more objective and convincing.
- Adding a special section to analyze the existing problems of the show and corresponding improvement suggestions would make the research more comprehensive and practical.
- The paper could also extend its conclusions to the global context, exploring how the successful experience of WTM can be applied to reality show production in other cultural contexts.
