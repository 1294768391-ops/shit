## 1.5. Abstract
The original first page of the document lacks a Chinese abstract, but the English abstract in the full text clearly states the core content of the paper:
- Research objective: To answer three core foundational questions for film and television semiotic research: Is film and television art a type of sign? What are the differences between it and natural language sign systems? What rules govern the generation of its sign system?
- Core methodology: Starting from structuralist semiotics, conduct a comparative study of film and television art and natural language in three dimensions: sign definition, encoding mechanisms, and meaning generation processes.
- Main results: Film and television art has a unique sign system significantly different from natural language, which constructs its sign forms based on the similarity (iconicity) principle. All image elements of a film and television work can be regarded as signs, and each work is a specially encoded sign set. Through repeated analysis and synthesis from the whole to the part and back to the whole, the inherent laws and expression forms of film and television works can be explored.
- Key conclusions: The similarity principle is the core logical starting point that distinguishes film and television sign systems from natural language, and it determines the unique encoding, combination and meaning generation rules of film and television art.

# 2. Executive Summary
## 2.1. Background & Motivation
### Core Problem to Be Solved
Before the 1990s, domestic Chinese film and television research mostly relied on subjective perceptual experience and traditional literary and art criticism methods, which lacked scientificity and systematicness, and usually only focused on partial dimensions (such as literary nature, dramatic nature) of film and television works, making it impossible to grasp the overall operating laws of film and television art as an independent art form.
### Importance and Research Gap
With the rapid development of film and television media, visual culture has gradually replaced print culture as the dominant form of contemporary culture, making it urgent to establish a systematic, scientific research paradigm for film and television art. Existing domestic research rarely systematically applies semiotic theories to clarify the essential differences between film and television sign systems and natural language, and lacks a complete analytical framework covering the whole process from micro sign encoding to macro meaning generation.
### Innovative Entry Point
The paper starts from the two main branches of modern semiotics (symbolic semiotics represented by Cassirer and Langer, structuralist semiotics represented by Saussure and Metz), focuses on structuralist semiotics, takes the similarity principle as the core breakthrough point, compares film and television art with natural language, and constructs a localized semiotic analytical framework suitable for Chinese film and television research.
## 2.2. Main Contributions / Findings
### Primary Contributions
1. Clarifies the essential differences between film and television image signs and natural language signs: Natural language has an arbitrary relationship between signifier and signified, while film and television signs follow the similarity principle, with signifier and signified almost integrated, having four unique characteristics: "active structure", universality, randomness and certainty.
2. Systematically sorts out three core functions of image signs: image function, indicating function and symbolic function, and explains the formation mechanism of each function.
3. Summarizes a complete set of encoding systems for film and television art: three encoding forms for individual image signs (documentary code, abstract code, imagery), five basic rhetorical devices (camera imaging, light and shadow, sound, montage, space art), and sign combination rules based on narrative perspective and context.
4. Introduces and applies Metz's eight syntagmas theory to explain the text composition rules of film and television works, combines Roland Barthes' writing theory to analyze the role of creator's style in meaning generation, and divides film and television artistic styles into three categories: documentary style, narrative style, and aestheticism style.
### Key Conclusions
Film and television art is a unique sign system completely different from natural language. Its meaning generation depends not only on the semiotic rules at the technical level, but also on the intervention of the creator's personal style. Compared with traditional subjective research methods, semiotics provides a more scientific and accurate research paradigm for film and television art research, which can effectively promote the systematization and scientization of film and television research.

# 3. Prerequisite Knowledge & Related Work
## 3.1. Foundational Concepts
To understand this paper, beginners need to master the following core semiotic and film studies concepts:
- **Semiotics**: An academic discipline that studies signs, sign systems, and the rules of meaning generation and communication through signs. It explores how humans use signs to convey meaning, construct and understand culture and social reality.
- **Signifier and Signified**: Core concepts proposed by Swiss linguist Ferdinand de Saussure, the two inseparable components of any sign:
  - `Signifier (signifiant)`: The material, perceptible part of a sign, such as the sound of a spoken word, the shape of a written word, or the visual image in a film.
  - `Signified (signifié)`: The abstract concept, meaning or idea that the signifier refers to, such as the concept of "snow" that the written word "snow" represents.
- **Arbitrariness of the sign**: A core Saussurean concept, meaning that there is no inherent, natural, necessary connection between the signifier and the signified of a natural language sign. For example, there is no inherent reason why the sound /snow/ (English) or /xuě/ (Chinese) must refer to the concept of frozen water crystals; this connection is purely a social convention agreed by the language community.
- **Similarity (Iconicity) principle**: Proposed by American philosopher Charles Sanders Peirce, referring to a type of sign (icon) where the signifier has a direct resemblance to the object it represents, such as a portrait, a photograph, or a film image of a cat that looks like a real cat. The connection between the signifier and signified of iconic signs is not arbitrary, but based on this similarity.
- **Structuralist semiotics**: A branch of semiotics developed from Saussure's structural linguistics, which studies sign systems as structured, rule-governed systems, focusing on the internal rules of sign combination and meaning generation, rather than the relationship between signs and external reality or individual psychology.
- **Symbolic semiotics (anthropological semiotics)**: Represented by Ernst Cassirer and Susanne Langer, which regards all human culture as a sign system, argues that humans are "symbolic animals", and art is a symbolic form that expresses human universal emotion.
- **Montage**: Derived from the French word for "assembly, editing", in film and television art, it refers to the technique of selecting, editing, and arranging separate shots into a coherent sequence to generate new meaning that no single shot has on its own.
- **Syntagmatic relation**: A Saussurean concept referring to the linear combination relationship between signs in a sequence, such as the order of words in a sentence, or the order of shots in a film sequence.
## 3.2. Previous Works
The paper is built on the following core foundational studies:
1. **Semiotic foundational theories**:
   - Ferdinand de Saussure, *Course in General Linguistics* (1916): Established structural linguistics, proposed the core concepts of signifier/signified, arbitrary sign, and syntagmatic/paradigmatic relations, laying the theoretical foundation for structuralist semiotics.
   - Charles Sanders Peirce: Classified signs into three categories: icons (based on similarity), indices (based on causal connection, e.g., smoke as a sign of fire), and symbols (based on social convention, e.g., natural language words), which is the theoretical source of the similarity principle discussed in the paper.
   - Ernst Cassirer, *An Essay on Man* (1944): Proposed that humans are "symbolic animals", and all human cultural forms (language, art, religion, science) are products of symbolic activities.
   - Susanne Langer, *Philosophy in a New Key* (1942), *Feeling and Form* (1953): Developed Cassirer's theory, argued that art is a symbolic form of human universal emotion, and film and television art is a virtual symbol that creates the perceptual effect of the real world.
2. **Film semiotics theories**:
   - Christian Metz: The founder of modern film semiotics, who applied structuralist semiotics to film research in the 1960s, proposed that film is a "language without a pre-existing language system" (no fixed, conventional code like natural language), and put forward the eight syntagmas theory as the structural rule for film text composition.
   - Umberto Eco: Developed the concept of iconic signs, emphasized that the similarity of iconic signs is not purely based on physical resemblance, but also shaped by cultural conventions.
   - Roland Barthes: Applied semiotics to popular culture research, proposed in *Writing Degree Zero* that writing is a combination of the horizontal axis of shared language structure and the vertical axis of personal style, which the paper uses to analyze the role of creator style in film meaning generation.
3. **Domestic film research context**: Before the 1990s, domestic Chinese film and television research mostly used traditional literary and art criticism methods, focusing on thematic content analysis and subjective evaluation, lacking systematic analysis of the formal structure and meaning generation rules of film and television as an independent sign system.
## 3.3. Technological Evolution
The development of film semiotics has gone through three main stages, and this paper is positioned in the stage of localization of Western film semiotic theories in China:
1. **Pre-1960s: Foundational stage**: Semiotic theories were gradually mature, but not systematically applied to film research. Film research focused on formalist montage theory (Eisenstein) and documentary realism theory (Bazin).
2. **1960s to early 1970s: First stage of film semiotics**: Represented by Metz, Pasolini, and Eco, research focused on comparing film language with natural language, analyzing the internal structure and encoding rules of film text at the technical level.
3. **Mid-1970s onwards: Second stage of film semiotics**: Combined with Lacanian psychoanalysis and Althusser's ideological theory, research expanded to audience psychology, ideological function of film, and narrative theory, showing obvious interdisciplinary characteristics.
4. **1990s onwards: Localization in China**: Western film semiotic theories were introduced to China, and this paper (2004) is one of the early domestic systematic studies that integrate Western film semiotics with Chinese film and television research practice.
## 3.4. Differentiation Analysis
- **Compared with traditional Chinese film and television research**: Traditional methods relied on subjective perception, focused only on content and thematic analysis, and lacked a systematic framework for analyzing formal structure and meaning generation rules. The semiotic framework proposed in this paper is more scientific and systematic, covering both content and formal dimensions, and avoiding the randomness of subjective criticism.
- **Compared with early Western film semiotics**: Early Western film semiotics focused almost exclusively on the technical structural analysis of film text, ignoring the role of the creator's personal style and the aesthetic relationship between film and the real life world. This paper integrates symbolic semiotics theories of Cassirer and Langer, adds analysis of creator style and aesthetic relationship with the life world, making the framework more suitable for the analysis of Chinese film and television works.

# 4. Methodology
## 4.1. Principles
The core methodological principle of the paper is to take structuralist semiotics as the main theoretical basis, combine with symbolic semiotics, conduct a comparative study between film and television art and natural language sign systems, take the similarity principle as the core logical starting point, deconstruct the film and television sign system from the micro level (individual signs) to the meso level (sign combination, encoding) to the macro level (text composition, meaning generation), to reveal the inherent operating laws of film and television art. The theoretical basis includes Saussure's structural linguistics, Peirce's sign classification, Metz's film semiotics, Cassirer and Langer's symbolic aesthetics, and Barthes' writing theory.
## 4.2. Core Methodology In-depth (Layer by Layer)
The methodology is structured in five progressive logical layers:
### Layer 1: Distinguish essential characteristics of film and television signs from natural language
First, compare the signifier-signified relationship of the two sign systems:
- For natural language signs: The relationship between signifier and signified is completely arbitrary, based only on social convention, with no inherent connection between the two parts.
- For film and television image signs: The relationship follows the similarity principle: the signifier (the image of an object in the film) directly resembles the real object it refers to, so the signifier and signified are almost integrated, making film and television signs a so-called "short-circuit sign". This is the core difference that determines all other characteristics of the film and television sign system.

  Then, summarize four unique qualities of image signs compared with written language:
1. **Active structure**: The signifier and signified are almost integrated, so the image of a book directly refers to a real existing book, without the intermediate conventional link required for natural language.
2. **Universality**: No long-term specialized learning is required to understand image signs. As long as people have basic visual experience, they can understand film and television images across cultural and educational levels, so film and television is a "humanistic vocabulary" with global communicability.
3. **Randomness**: For the same concept (e.g., "spring"), the natural language signifier is fixed (only a limited number of words can express it), but the image signifier can be infinitely creative. Each creator can shoot a unique image of spring according to their own aesthetic concept, so the creation of film and television signs is inherently artistic and personalized.
4. **Certainty**: The image sign is a specific, concrete image, so the audience does not need to go through the process of concretizing abstract concepts (as when reading written language), and can directly enter the judgment of its meaning. The ambiguity of film images is lower than that of oral or written language.
### Layer 2: Analyze three core functions of image signs
1. **Image function**: Image signs are the result of the creator shooting real objects from a specific perspective, with inherent "documentary quality". They have two types of generalization ability:
   - "Representing the whole with a point": For example, the image of the Statue of Liberty can represent the entire United States.
   - "Representing more with less": For example, the close-up of a hand being buried in loess in *Massacre in Nanjing* can represent countless Chinese people killed by Japanese invaders, without needing to show all the victims.
2. **Indicating function**: An image sign corresponds to multiple information sources, which evokes the audience's past knowledge structure and life experience. The perception process is also the process of subjective projection of the audience, so the audience will fill in the "blank" of the image sign according to their own experience, which conforms to the reception aesthetics theory that art works are open structures that need to be concretized by the recipient. For example, the image of an abandoned ancient temple will make ordinary people see ruins, nostalgic people recall the past, and Buddhist history researchers see precious research materials.
3. **Symbolic function**: The concrete intuitive image refers to abstract universal spirit, which has high aesthetic value. The symbolic function comes from two sources:
   - Biological factors (prototype symbols): E.g., flying birds symbolize freedom, fish in a pot symbolize predicament, which has universal cross-cultural commonality.
   - Cultural factors (conventional symbols): E.g., fish in Chinese culture symbolize "surplus every year" because of the homonym between "fish" (yú) and "surplus" (yú), which is only valid in the Chinese cultural circle.
### Layer 3: Deconstruct the encoding process of film and television art
Encoding refers to the process by which the creator selects and combines various signs to construct meaning, which is divided into two levels: encoding of individual image signs, and encoding of combination between signs.
#### Encoding forms of individual image signs
1. **Documentary code**: Pursue faithfulness to the shooting object, accurately show the natural state of the object, so that the audience has a sense of real identity. It is divided into two types: one is as objective and comprehensive as possible to copy real life, used for documentary works; the other is to select and refine objects according to the creator's aesthetic perspective on the basis of ensuring reality, used for realistic style works.
2. **Abstract code**: Deviate from the original state of the object under natural conditions, create a distance from the daily shape in the empirical world, and present the form desired by the subject. It is realized through personalized processing of light, color and lines of the shot object, e.g., in Zhang Yimou's *Hero*, the light processing of the fight scene in the ginkgo forest strengthens the aesthetic tendency, creates a strange and mysterious fantasy world, achieves the effect of "defamiliarization" proposed by Shklovsky, making the audience focus on the aesthetic feeling and deep connotation of the work.
3. **Imagery**: The product of the combination of subject and object, the core is to express meaning and create artistic conception through images, divided into three types:
   - **Individual imagery**: A single carefully arranged expressive image sign in the narrative chain, e.g., the shot of Wang Quan carrying a huge slate in *Old Well*, which has both narrative function and symbolic meaning of the heavy fate of the characters.
   - **Block imagery**: A fragment or scene composed of imagery created by similarity encoding, each unit image has the same tendency, forming a "field" that creates a specific emotional tone and cultural atmosphere, e.g., the scene of wild sorghum in *Red Sorghum*, the dancing sorghum forms an isomorphic correspondence with the wild vitality of the characters.
   - **Sequence imagery**: A combination of unit images with independent content and structure, each unit image can be heterogeneous, the meaning of individual imagery depends on the arrangement of the whole sequence and the context, e.g., the three scenes of "charging", "confrontation" and "retreating" in the TV series *Aunt Dan*, which form a strong contrast aesthetic effect, highlighting the connotation of evil cannot prevail over good.
#### Basic rhetorical devices for encoding
There are five core technical means for creators to encode:
1. **Camera imaging function**: The core structural element of film and television art. The change of shot size (close-up, medium shot, long shot) is the main way for the creator to intervene in style: e.g., Zhang Yimou is good at using long shots to show grand scenes, while some directors are good at using close-ups to portray the inner world of characters.
2. **Light and shadow**: Light is not only a necessary condition for exposure, but also a basic means of film modeling. Color can arouse people's psychological and emotional response: e.g., *Red Sorghum* uses yellow as the main tone, *Farewell My Concubine* uses dark blue as the main tone, color strengthens the artistic conception of the work and promotes the generation of higher-level meaning.
3. **Sound**: The combination of sound and image makes film change from pure visual art to audio-visual art, closer to the original form of life, and enhances the sense of reality. Sound includes dialogue, background sound, music, etc.
4. **Montage**: The combination and connection method of shots. Different combination orders of shots will produce different meanings: for example, three shots (a person laughing, a person's scared expression, a close-up of a pistol) arranged in the order of laugh → scared → pistol show that the person is cowardly; arranged in the order of pistol → scared → laugh show that the person is brave.
5. **Space art**: The space presented by film and television is an aesthetic space created by the creator, not a material reality. The three-dimensional space on the screen plus the time dimension forms the fourth dimension of film and television art. Space is not only the background of character activities, but also can promote the development of the plot and show the inner emotions of characters.
#### Sign combination rules
The combination of signs is governed by two core factors:
1. **Narrative perspective (focus)**: Divided into three types:
   - **Non-focused type (zero focus, God's perspective)**: The narrator is omniscient, can observe the story from all angles, freely move the position, suitable for works with large scale, complex clues and many characters.
   - **Internal focused type**: Everything is presented according to the feelings and consciousness of one or several characters, only转述 the information accepted by the character from the outside and his inner activities, shortens the distance between the character and the audience, but has a strict field of vision limit.
   - **External focused type**: The narrator only presents events from the outside, only provides the actions, appearance and objective environment of the characters, does not tell the motivation, purpose, thinking and emotion of the characters, mostly used in detective and martial arts works.
2. **Context (symbolic context)**: Divided into two types:
   - **Internal context**: The relationship between a certain image sign and a certain syntagmatic segment, including explicit logical causal relationship and implicit atmosphere context (e.g., the feudal and rigid atmosphere of the Zhou family in *The Family* that dominates the fate of the characters).
   - **External context**: The social and natural environment outside the image sign segment, including natural environment (weather, landscape, region) and social background (times, customs, interpersonal relationships), which can create atmosphere, increase connotation, shape characters and construct stories.
### Layer 4: Text composition of film and television works
Apply Metz's eight syntagmas theory, take the shot as the smallest unit of film discourse, the eight syntagmas are the structural rules of film text at the sentence segment level:
1. **Autonomous shot**: A single independent shot, generally a depth-of-field shot with large content capacity, can convey a clear and complete meaning alone, similar to a one-sentence paragraph in natural language, mostly used in documentary works.
2. **Parallel syntagma**: Composed of two or more alternating narrative themes without strict time relationship, the order can be exchanged without affecting the meaning, e.g., the alternating combination of natural spring scenes and human spring scenes.
3. **Parenthesis syntagma**: A syntagma representing memory, imagination, etc., inserted into other narrative sequences through special lighting effects or shot transition methods (fade in, fade out), the content is logically related to the current protagonist's thoughts and feelings, but the time and space are different from the current narrative.
4. **Descriptive syntagma**: Used to explain the environment where the characters appear, the shots form a diachronic fact to set off the atmosphere and create deep connotation, e.g., the combination of roaring wind, thousands of miles of sand, and scattered bows and arrows forms a descriptive syntagma of the ancient battlefield in the desert.
5. **Alternating narrative syntagma**: Alternately narrate two or more simultaneous events, with multiple narrative clues, similar to the "two flowers, each representing one branch" narrative method in traditional Chinese novels, suitable for works with complex plots.
6. **Scene**: The image refers to a continuous and uninterrupted fact process, the narration process is completely consistent with the narrated process, no omission or jump, maintains the time and space unity of the event, used for scenes that need to carefully portray the object with small time span.
7. **Single segment**: In the linear narrative syntagma, the image refers to a single non-continuous fact process, omitting some parts of the fact process through image jump, e.g., showing a person walking in the desert for a long time through the叠化 of shots of clothes and shoes.
8. **Scattered segment**: Refers to non-continuous fact processes that occur in different locations and times, the image jump is more free, skipping the event process that the creator thinks is of little value.
### Layer 5: Meaning generation of film and television signs
The meaning of the work does not exist before the visual style and scene scheduling, the intervention of the creator's style is the key to meaning generation:
- Apply Roland Barthes' writing theory: Film creation is similar to literary writing, the basic rules of film language (montage, shot size change, segment segmentation) are the horizontal axis of shared language structure, which is the common rule that all creators must abide by; the creator's personal style is the vertical axis, which is closely related to the creator's personal experience, temperament, and memory, with great personal freedom and privacy, and style is naturally presented in the work without deliberate pursuit.
- The creator first creates his own "film dictionary" according to the similarity principle, processes the image through modeling, light and shadow, color and other means to make it "personalized", integrating personal preferences and experiences into the image signs.
- According to the different encoding methods of basic rhetorical devices, film and television styles are divided into three types: documentary style, aestheticism style, and narrative style.

# 6. Results & Analysis
This paper uses case analysis to verify its theoretical framework, and does not have quantitative experimental results.
## 6.1. Core Results Analysis
Through case analysis, the paper verifies that the semiotic framework it proposes has strong explanatory power for film and television works:
1. The four characteristics of image signs based on the similarity principle can well explain the cross-cultural universality of film and television art, and the difference between film and television appreciation and written text appreciation.
2. The classification of three encoding forms (documentary code, abstract code, imagery) can accurately correspond to the creation methods of different style works: documentary works use more documentary codes, aestheticism works use more abstract codes, and works with high artistic value all have a large number of well-designed imagery.
3. The five basic rhetorical devices cover all the main technical means of film and television creation, and the analysis of the narrative perspective and context can explain the difference in meaning generation caused by different narrative methods of the same material.
4. Metz's eight syntagmas theory can effectively deconstruct the text structure of different types of film and television works, and has strong operability for both creation and criticism.
5. The analysis of the creator's style intervention can explain the unique artistic features of works by different directors, and the three-style classification (documentary, narrative, aestheticism) basically covers the main style types of existing film and television works.
## 6.2. Comparative Analysis with Baseline Frameworks
- Compared with traditional Chinese film and television criticism framework: The semiotic framework proposed in this paper is more systematic and scientific, covering both content analysis and formal structure analysis, avoiding the subjectivity and randomness of traditional criticism, and providing a unified analytical paradigm for film and television research.
- Compared with early Western film semiotics framework: This paper integrates the symbolic semiotics theory of Cassirer and Langer, and combines the local context of Chinese film and television creation, increasing the analysis of the creator's style and the aesthetic relationship between film and television and the life world, making the framework more suitable for the analysis of Chinese film and television works, and avoiding the pure technical structuralism tendency of early Western film semiotics.
## 6.3. Scope of Application Analysis
The paper points out that the semiotic analytical framework is applicable to almost all types of film and television works, including feature films, documentaries, TV series, etc., but different types of works focus on different parts of the framework: documentary works focus on documentary code and non-focused perspective, aestheticism works focus on abstract code and symbolic function of signs, and narrative feature films focus on montage, narrative perspective and syntagmatic combination.

# 7. Conclusion & Reflections
## 7.1. Conclusion Summary
This thesis systematically constructs a semiotic analytical framework for film and television art by integrating structuralist semiotics and symbolic semiotics theories, combining comparative analysis between film and television signs and natural language, and case analysis of classic film and television works. The main conclusions are:
1. Film and television art is a unique sign system different from natural language, its core is the similarity principle, which makes its signifier and signified almost integrated, with four unique characteristics of active structure, universality, randomness and certainty.
2. Film and television image signs have three core functions: image function, indicating function and symbolic function.
3. The encoding process of film and television works includes three encoding forms of individual signs, five basic rhetorical devices, sign combination rules based on narrative perspective and context, and text composition based on Metz's eight syntagmas.
4. The meaning generation of film and television signs depends not only on the semiotic rules at the technical level, but also on the intervention of the creator's personal style, and film and television works can be divided into three styles: documentary, narrative and aestheticism.
5. Semiotics provides a more scientific and accurate research method for film and television art research than traditional methods, and has broad application prospects.
## 7.2. Limitations & Future Work
### Limitations
The author points out that the research is still at the stage of introducing and applying basic semiotic theories, there are still deficiencies in the clear definition of image signs, the boundary between signifier and signified of film and television signs, and the determination of the basic rhetorical devices system of film and television semiotics.
### Future Research Directions
1. Further combine the latest developments of Western post-structuralist film semiotics (psychoanalytic semiotics, ideological semiotics) to deepen the analysis of audience psychology and ideological function of film and television works.
2. Combine with the practice of Chinese local film and television creation, further localize the semiotic framework, and form a film and television semiotic theory suitable for Chinese cultural context.
3. Expand the research scope to new media video forms (network video, short video, interactive video, etc.) that have emerged in recent years, and explore the semiotic rules of new media video.
## 7.3. Personal Insights & Critique
### Inspirations
This thesis is one of the early systematic studies of film and television semiotics in China, which provides a complete and operable analytical framework for domestic film and television research, and bridges the gap between traditional subjective criticism and scientific systematic research. Its method can be transferred to the research of other visual art forms (photography, drama, new media art, etc.), and can also be applied to the analysis of visual culture and mass communication.
### Potential Improvements
1. The paper mostly uses Western semiotic theories, and the integration with traditional Chinese aesthetic theories (e.g., artistic conception theory) is insufficient, which can be further deepened in future research.
2. The analysis of the audience's meaning interpretation process is relatively insufficient, and more reception theory and audience research can be combined to analyze the difference in meaning interpretation of the same film and television work by different audiences.
3. The paper was published in 2004, and did not involve the semiotic characteristics of emerging new media video forms (short video, live broadcast, interactive video, etc.) that have developed rapidly in recent years, which is an important direction for the expansion of this research.
4. The classification of styles is relatively simple, and with the diversification of film and television creation, more style types can be added, and the boundaries between different styles can be further clarified.
