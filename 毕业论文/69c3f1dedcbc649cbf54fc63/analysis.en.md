## 1.5. Abstract
Mango TV is an online video platform controlled by Hunan Broadcasting System (HBS, also known as Hunan Satellite TV) and operated by Hunan Happy Sunshine Interactive Entertainment Media Co., Ltd. Within two years of its revamp and launch, it entered the top 4 domestic video websites in China, following a "content first, channel second" development path that re-emphasized the value of high-quality content. The study analyzes content features of Mango TV's core variety show and TV drama channels, using literature analysis, inductive analysis, comparative analysis and case study methods. The paper consists of 4 chapters: an overview of domestic video sites and Mango TV, an analysis of special elements of Mango TV's video content, a summary of advantages and disadvantages of its content production as a broadcaster-affiliated video site, and a critical reflection on potential problems including pan-entertainment, copyright infringement and consumerism.

# 2. Executive Summary
## 2.1. Background & Motivation
### Core Problem to Solve
By the end of 2016, China's online video user scale reached 545 million, accounting for 74.5% of all internet users, making online video the 4th most widely used internet application in the country. Domestic video sites are divided into four categories: video sharing sites, portal-affiliated sites, live streaming sites, and radio and television (RT)-affiliated sites. Mango TV, as the most successful RT-affiliated video site in China, achieved rapid growth relying on HBS's high-quality content, but prior academic research lacked systematic analysis of the core factors behind its content success, especially the specific characteristics of its high-traffic content. This paper aims to fill this research gap.
### Research Gap
Prior research on Mango TV mostly focused on its operational strategy, media integration and brand building, with few studies systematically deconstructing its content production system from the dimensions of content selection, technology application and cultural construction.
### Innovative Entry Point
The paper takes the dual identity of Mango TV (RT-affiliated + market-oriented internet platform) as the core entry point, analyzes how it balances the genes of traditional TV and internet in content production, and summarizes the replicable experience and existing shortcomings of its development model.
## 2.2. Main Contributions / Findings
### Primary Contributions
1. Systematically sorted out Mango TV's content strategy system for variety shows and TV dramas, including content positioning, IP development, technology application and cultural integration.
2. Clarified the dual impact of Mango TV's RT background on its content production: it enjoys exclusive content, talent, policy and brand support from HBS, but also faces constraints such as high dependence on the parent platform, limited creative space and single content subject matter.
3. Proposed targeted development breakthroughs for Mango TV: expanding content selection range, introducing social interaction chains to improve user stickiness, and gradually reducing dependence on HBS to build independent brand value.
### Key Conclusions
1. Mango TV's rapid growth comes from its precise user positioning (targeting young female users aged 18-30, who account for 70% of its total users), flexible IP development strategy and active application of new interactive technologies.
2. Its content has dual cultural attributes: it integrates internet subculture to meet the entertainment needs of young users, while adhering to the dissemination of mainstream values, which provides a reference for other video platforms to balance commercial interests and social responsibility.

# 3. Prerequisite Knowledge & Related Work
## 3.1. Foundational Concepts
All core terms are explained for beginners below:
- **Mango TV**: The only official online video platform under Hunan Broadcasting System, revamped and launched in April 2014. It initially adopted an "exclusive broadcast" strategy, meaning all self-produced content of Hunan Satellite TV was only broadcast on Mango TV, rather than being distributed to other video sites.
- **Classification of domestic video sites (as defined in the paper)**:
  1. **Video sharing sites**: Early video platforms centered on *User Generated Content (UGC)* (content created by ordinary non-professional users rather than professional teams), such as early Youku, Tudou and Bilibili. Later, they added professional content and copyright purchases to enrich their content library.
  2. **Portal-affiliated video sites**: Operated by large internet portal groups, with sufficient capital and cross-platform user diversion support, such as Tencent Video (backed by Tencent), iQiyi (backed by Baidu) and Sohu Video (backed by Sohu).
  3. **Live streaming sites**: Focus on real-time streaming content, including game live, talent show live and event live, such as Douyu and Huya.
  4. **RT-affiliated video sites**: Backed by state-owned radio and television institutions, with exclusive content resources of the parent TV station and policy advantages including content operation licenses, such as Mango TV (backed by HBS) and CNTV (backed by China Central Television).
- **IP (Intellectual Property) in the content industry**: Refers to content with high popularity and a stable fan base, including variety shows, novels, animation, games, etc. It can be developed into multiple derivative products (e.g. a hit variety show can have spin-off programs, peripheral products, film and television adaptations) to maximize its commercial value.
- **Pan-entertainment**: A phenomenon where excessive shallow entertainment content crowds out serious, informative and cultural content, leading to superficial cultural consumption and insufficient dissemination of mainstream values.
- **PGC (Professional Generated Content)**: Content created by professional media production teams, such as TV dramas, variety shows and news programs produced by formal media companies.
- **5W Communication Model**: Proposed by communication scholar Harold Lasswell, the five core elements of a communication activity are: *Who* (communicator), *Says What* (content), *In Which Channel* (medium), *To Whom* (audience), *With What Effect* (communication effect). It is a classic framework for analyzing media communication activities.
- **Gatekeeper Theory**: Proposed by psychologist Kurt Lewin, refers to individuals or institutions that filter and check information before it is disseminated to the public, to ensure that the content complies with legal, ethical and platform positioning requirements. For live content, gatekeeping is particularly important due to its real-time and uncontrollable characteristics.
## 3.2. Previous Works
The paper divides prior relevant research into four categories:
1. **Industry market research reports**: From authoritative institutions including CNNIC (China Internet Network Information Center), iResearch Consulting and DCCI (Data Center of China Internet). These reports provide basic industry data such as video site user scale, user behavior, revenue structure and development trends. For example, CNNIC's semi-annual *China Internet Development Statistics Report* provides official data on the overall scale of internet users, and iResearch's reports cover topics such as traditional TV station transformation and online video paid user market development.
2. **Academic research on general video sites**:
   - **Business model research**: Focuses on profit models, operation models and development strategies of video sites. For example, Chen Xiaoyan's master's thesis *Research on Business Models of Video Sites* systematically analyzes the business models of sharing, P2P and vertical video sites, and proposes optimization ideas.
   - **Media integration research**: Focuses on the integration path of traditional TV and online video platforms. For example, studies on collaborative development strategies between provincial satellite TV and video sites, and research on the operation mode of TV stations' official online platforms under the background of media integration.
   - **Copyright research**: Focuses on copyright disputes between traditional media and new media, and copyright infringement problems of video sites. For example, research on the copyright game between traditional TV stations and commercial video sites, and analysis of Mango TV's exclusive broadcast strategy from the perspective of copyright protection.
   - **Content strategy research**: The latest research direction, focusing on self-produced content of video sites after the rise of content competition. For example, research on iQiyi's self-produced content strategy represented by the hit variety show *U Can U BiBi (Qi Pa Shuo)*.
3. **Research on Mango TV**: Prior studies mainly focused on its development strategy, brand building and self-produced content layout, but lacked systematic analysis of the core success factors of its high-traffic content, which is the key research gap this paper fills.
## 3.3. Technological Evolution
The development of China's domestic video industry has gone through three main stages:
1. **2004-2010: Initial development stage**: Represented by LeTV, Tudou and Youku, the industry was dominated by the UGC sharing mode, with low copyright awareness and unclear profit models.
2. **2011-2014: Copyright competition stage**: As user demand for high-quality content increased, platforms began to purchase a large number of copyrights for TV dramas and variety shows, capital competition intensified, and portal-affiliated video sites rose rapidly relying on the capital support of parent groups.
3. **2014-present: Self-produced content stage**: After the cost of copyright content rose sharply, platforms turned to self-produced content to build differentiated competitive advantages, and IP development became the core strategy of major platforms.
   This paper's research on Mango TV (launched in 2014) is located at the beginning of the self-produced content era, exploring the development path of RT-affiliated video sites in the context of intensified content competition.
## 3.4. Differentiation Analysis
Compared with prior related research, this paper has two core innovations:
1. **Research perspective innovation**: Different from previous studies that mostly analyzed Mango TV from the perspectives of operation and media integration, this paper is the first to systematically deconstruct its core content system from the dimensions of content selection, technology application and cultural construction, revealing the internal logic of its content success.
2. **Research object innovation**: Different from general video site content research that focuses on commercial private platforms, this paper focuses on the particularity of RT-affiliated video sites, analyzing the dual impact of traditional TV background on content production (both supporting advantages and restrictive disadvantages).

# 4. Methodology
## 4.1. Principles
The core research idea of the paper is to take Mango TV's two core content categories (variety shows and TV dramas) as the main research object, combine macro industry environment analysis with micro content text analysis, explore the characteristics and formation mechanism of its content system, and summarize its operational advantages, existing shortcomings and future development directions.
## 4.2. Core Methodology In-depth (Layer by Layer)
The paper uses four main research methods, as detailed below:
### 4.2.1. Literature Analysis Method
- **Core definition**: Collect and sort relevant academic literature, industry reports, official statistical data and policy documents to fully understand the development status of the domestic video industry and prior research results, laying a theoretical foundation for the study.
- **Application in the paper**: The author collected more than 30 relevant literatures, including academic papers on video site business models, media integration, copyright and content strategy, as well as industry reports from CNNIC, iResearch and DCCI, to sort out the development background of the video industry and the research status of Mango TV.
### 4.2.2. Inductive Analysis Method
- **Core definition**: Classify and summarize the research objects, extract common features and internal rules from scattered cases and data, and draw general conclusions.
- **Application in the paper**: The author classified Mango TV's variety shows and TV dramas according to subject matter, source and style, summarized their content selection rules, technical application characteristics and cultural expression features. For example, it summarized that Mango TV's variety show content selection mainly comes from two paths: localized transformation of overseas mature copyright programs, and development of large IP and derivative programs; its TV dramas are mainly divided into four categories suitable for young female users: idol dramas, ancient style dramas, realistic family dramas and Korean dramas.
### 4.2.3. Case Analysis Method
- **Core definition**: Select typical, representative cases for in-depth analysis, explore the particularity and universal rules of the research object, and support research conclusions with specific cases.
- **Application in the paper**: The author selected more than 20 typical programs of Mango TV as analysis cases, including hit variety shows such as *Where Are We Going, Dad?*, *Takes a Real Man* and *I Am a Singer*, and popular TV dramas such as *The Journey of Flower*, *Sparrow* and *Golden Matchmaker*. It analyzed their content characteristics, production strategies and communication effects in detail, and verified the universality of the summarized content rules through these cases.
### 4.2.4. Comparative Analysis Method
- **Core definition**: Compare the research object with other similar objects in the industry, find out the differences and similarities between them, and highlight the unique characteristics and competitive advantages of the research object.
- **Application in the paper**: The author compared Mango TV with the other three types of domestic video sites (sharing sites, portal-affiliated sites, live streaming sites), analyzed the particularity of its RT background; compared the content subject of Mango TV with traditional TV stations and other commercial video sites, analyzed its differentiated user positioning and content strategy.

## 5.2. Evaluation Metrics
The paper uses four core indicators to evaluate the communication effect of Mango TV's content, with detailed explanations as follows:
### 5.2.1. User Scale
- **Conceptual Definition**: Measures the coverage of the platform, including sub-indicators such as Daily Active Users (DAU, the number of unique users who use the platform every day), Monthly Active Users (MAU, the number of unique users who use the platform every month) and mobile terminal activation volume.
- **Mathematical Formula**: It is a count indicator with no standardized calculation formula, obtained by counting the number of unique users within a statistical period (duplicate users are only counted once).
### 5.2.2. Video View (VV)
- **Conceptual Definition**: The total number of times video content is played, which measures the total traffic brought by content. Each time a user opens and plays a video, it counts as 1 VV, even if the same user watches the same video multiple times.
- **Mathematical Formula**:
  \$
  VV = \sum_{i=1}^{N} C_i
  \$
- **Symbol Explanation**: $N$ is the total number of videos on the platform, $C_i$ is the number of plays of the $i$-th video within the statistical period.
### 5.2.3. Per Capita Effective Viewing Duration
- **Conceptual Definition**: The average time each active user spends watching video content on the platform within a certain period, which measures user stickiness (the degree of user loyalty and dependence on the platform). A longer duration means higher user recognition of the platform's content.
- **Mathematical Formula**:
  \$
  \text{Per Capita Effective Viewing Duration} = \frac{\text{Total Effective Viewing Duration of All Users}}{\text{Number of Active Users in the Statistical Period}}
  \$
- **Symbol Explanation**: Total effective viewing duration excludes the time when the video is paused or the user leaves the playback page.
### 5.2.4. Social Media Discussion Volume
- **Conceptual Definition**: The number of related topics, readings and discussions of content on social media platforms (represented by Sina Weibo in the paper), which measures the social influence and topic heat of content.
## 5.3. Comparative Objects (Equivalent to Baselines)
The paper compares Mango TV with two types of objects to highlight its characteristics:
1. **Other types of domestic video sites**: Including video sharing sites (Youku, Bilibili), portal-affiliated sites (Tencent Video, iQiyi, Sohu Video) and live streaming sites (Douyu, Huya), to analyze the particularity of RT-affiliated video sites.
2. **Traditional TV stations**: Represented by Hunan Satellite TV, to analyze the differences in content positioning and expression between online platforms and traditional TV media.

#### 2. Technology application strategy
Mango TV actively introduces new interactive technologies to improve user experience:
- **Multi-camera self-selection**: When watching live programs such as New Year's Eve concerts and *I Am a Singer* finals, users can freely choose different camera angles (e.g. stage overall view, backstage, artist lounge, specific artist follow-up), and even interact with the camera team to decide shooting content.
- **VR (Virtual Reality) zone**: Launched VR viewing function for programs such as *I Am a Singer* and *Super Girl 2016*, allowing users to have a 360-degree immersive viewing experience, becoming the first domestic video platform to apply VR technology to variety shows.
- **"Lingxi" extended scene**: Embedded hyperlinks in video content, providing functions such as same-style product purchase, character introduction, related video recommendation, realizing "watch while buy" interaction, which greatly improves the conversion rate of advertising.
- **Differentiated live broadcast**: Adopts dual-platform (Hunan Satellite TV + Mango TV) live broadcast mode, with strict content gatekeeping relying on HBS's mature content review system, and launches 24-hour live broadcast programs such as *Perfect Holiday* to meet users' demand for real-time content.
#### 3. Cultural cultivation strategy
Mango TV's content integrates dual cultural attributes:
- **Integration of internet subculture**: Uses internet buzzwords, ghost editing (a popular online editing style with repeated funny clips) and online expression forms (bracketed pinyin, emoticons, etc.) in program post-production, which is deeply loved by young users.
- **Output of orthodox cultural values**: Attaches importance to the dissemination of mainstream values: *Takes a Real Man* popularizes national defense education; parent-child programs promote family concepts and filial piety culture; *Day Day Up* spreads traditional Chinese etiquette culture through the special section *The Beauty of Chinese Civilization*.
### 6.1.4. TV Drama Content Feature Results
Mango TV's TV drama content has the following characteristics:
#### 1. Differentiated content strategy
Based on its user structure (70% female, 90% under 30 years old), it focuses on four subject types:
- **Idol dramas**: Focus on high-appearance-value actors and romantic plots, such as *Love Weaves Through a Millennium* and *Sparrow*.
- **Aesthetic ancient style dramas**: Focus on elegant ancient costumes and fairy-tale visual style, such as *The Journey of Flower* and *Love Yunge from the Desert*.
- **Realistic family dramas**: Focus on social hot topics such as marriage, education and women's growth, such as *The Most Beautiful Is You* and *Tiger Mom*.
- **Korean dramas**: Introduce popular Korean romantic dramas, and remake Korean dramas into Chinese versions, such as *Pretty Li Huizhen* (remade from Korean drama *She Was Pretty*).
#### 2. Taiwan-network linkage
Covers all three hit drama theaters of Hunan Satellite TV (Golden Eagle Solo Theater, Diamond Solo Theater, Youth in Progress Theater), realizing synchronous broadcast on TV and online, diverting TV audiences to the online platform and maximizing the communication effect of content.
#### 3. IP drama development
Purchases and self-produces a large number of IP dramas adapted from popular online novels, such as *Noble Aspirations* and *The Mystic Nine*, which reduces the market risk of new dramas by relying on the original fan base of IP.
#### 4. Cultural features
- **Integration of local Hunan culture and internet culture**: Broadcasts many TV dramas with Hunan regional characteristics such as *Blood of Hunan West* and *Battle of Changsha*, while also launching internet-style dramas such as *Demon Girl*.
- **Mimetic presentation of real society**: Integrates modern social hot topics such as "leftover women" and "blind date" into drama plots (even in ancient costume dramas), to arouse user resonance.
- **Warm tone emotion control**: Uses warm tones (orange, red, yellow) as the main color of the website and drama pictures, conveying the brand positioning of "youth and happiness", which is consistent with Hunan Satellite TV's "Happy China" brand style.
### 6.1.5. Communication Effect Results

- 6 of the top 10 variety shows on Sina Weibo 2016 annual hot search list are broadcast on Mango TV. *Where Are We Going, Dad? Season 4* has more than 40 billion Weibo readings, and *Takes a Real Man 2* has 6.33 billion Weibo readings.
- Operation data: DAU exceeds 43 million, average daily VV exceeds 200 million, PC-side monthly coverage reaches 164 million, mobile APP activation exceeds 370 million. The per capita weekly effective viewing duration of variety shows exceeds 1.5 hours, ranking first in the domestic video industry.

### 6.1.6. Advantages and Disadvantages Results
#### Core Advantages
1. **Production side**: Exclusive HBS resource support, including all HBS content copyright, cross-appointment talent team (senior managers of Mango TV and Hunan Satellite TV take turns to hold positions), sufficient capital support (HBS holds 82.48% of Mango TV's shares), and inheritance of HBS's brand effect.
2. **Communication side**: "One cloud, multiple screens" layout, covering PC, mobile, internet TV, IPTV and other terminals; dual-platform linkage with Hunan Satellite TV, realizing mutual diversion of TV audiences and online users.
#### Core Disadvantages
1. **High dependence on parent platform, insufficient self-produced content quality**: Most hit content is transferred from HBS or developed based on HBS IP, and original self-produced content has low market influence.
2. **Single subject matter**: Content mainly focuses on entertainment, youth and romance subjects, lacks diversity, which limits the expansion of user scale to middle-aged and male user groups.
3. **Stricter review and social supervision**: Due to its RT background, audiences and regulators have higher requirements for its content, which limits the creative space of content compared with commercial video sites.
## 6.2. Parameter Analysis
The paper analyzes that the communication effect of Mango TV's content is mainly affected by three core factors:
1. **User positioning precision**: Its core user group (young female users) has high loyalty and consumption willingness, so content targeted at this group can achieve high user conversion rate and commercial return.
2. **IP utilization efficiency**: Developing derivative programs based on mature IP can greatly reduce production risk and improve the return on investment of content.
3. **Technology application**: The introduction of interactive technologies such as multi-camera selection, Lingxi and VR improves user experience and increases user stickiness.

# 7. Conclusion & Reflections
## 7.1. Conclusion Summary
This paper systematically analyzes the content characteristics, operational advantages, existing problems and development directions of Mango TV, and draws the following core conclusions:
1. Mango TV's rapid growth verifies the "content is king" strategy in the video industry. Its success comes from precise user positioning targeting young female users, flexible IP development and active application of new interactive technologies, as well as the exclusive resource support from HBS.
2. As a RT-affiliated video site, Mango TV's background is both its biggest advantage and disadvantage: it can obtain exclusive content, talent, policy and brand support from HBS, but also faces constraints such as high dependence on the parent platform, single content subject and stricter supervision.
3. Mango TV's content has dual cultural attributes: it not only integrates internet subculture to meet the entertainment needs of young users, but also adheres to the dissemination of mainstream values, which provides a reference for other video platforms to balance commercial interests and social responsibility.
## 7.2. Limitations & Future Work
The author points out the following limitations of the study:
1. **Limited research scope**: Only analyzed variety shows and TV dramas, the two core content categories, did not cover other content types such as movies, documentaries and UGC content, so it cannot fully summarize the characteristics of all Mango TV content.
2. **Timeliness limitation**: The data is only up to 2016, and the development of Mango TV and the domestic video industry is very fast, so the conclusions have certain timeliness limitations.
3. **Lack of user survey data**: The analysis of user behavior and psychology is mainly based on industry statistical data, and lacks large-scale user survey support, so the depth is insufficient.
### Future Research Directions
1. Expand the research scope to cover all content types of Mango TV, and track its subsequent development changes to update the conclusions.
2. Conduct large-scale user surveys to deeply analyze the impact of Mango TV's content on user behavior and attitude.
3. Compare Mango TV with other RT-affiliated new media platforms to explore the general development law of traditional media transformation in the internet era.
## 7.3. Personal Insights & Critique
### Insights
1. The success of Mango TV shows that for platforms in the growth stage, differentiated positioning and precise user targeting are more important than blind pursuit of content diversity. Focusing on serving the core user group well can achieve rapid growth.
2. For traditional media transformation, it is necessary to give full play to the advantages of original content production and value guidance, while actively integrating internet culture and new technologies to meet the needs of online users. The experience of Mango TV has important reference value for the transformation of other traditional media.
### Potential Problems and Improvements
1. **Timeliness update**: The paper was published in 2017, and the domestic video industry has changed greatly since then: short video platforms (Douyin, Kuaishou) have risen, long video platforms have entered the era of paid membership, and Mango TV's development has also changed significantly: it has launched a large number of original self-produced content such as *Sisters Who Make Waves*, and its dependence on HBS has decreased significantly. The conclusions of the paper need to be updated in combination with the new development situation.
2. **Insufficient depth of problem analysis**: The paper's analysis of the pan-entertainment problem is relatively superficial, and it does not deeply analyze the negative impact of Mango TV's overemphasis on entertainment content and appearance value on the values of young users, especially minor users.
3. **Lack of paid content analysis**: The paper does not involve the analysis of Mango TV's paid content strategy, which has become the core profit source of long video platforms now, so this part needs to be supplemented.
### Application Value
The content strategy of Mango TV summarized in the paper has strong reference value for traditional media transformation and vertical video platform operation: IP derivative development, precise user positioning, and integration of mainstream values and internet culture are all applicable to current content operation practice.
